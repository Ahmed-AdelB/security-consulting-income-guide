    1. Executive Summary (Key Findings)¶

    • Federal cyber spending for 2025 is projected at ~$20.4B across civilian and defense sectors.
    • DoD alone accounts for ~$14.5B in cyber budget allocations.
    • CMMC 2.0 is mandatory for Defense Industrial Base (DIB) contractors and drives recurring compliance cycles.
    • CMMC consulting hourly rates cluster around $250-$400.
    • CMMC readiness projects range from $15k-$300k depending on size and complexity.
    • CMMC gap assessments often fall in the $3.5k-$20k range.
    • C3PAO assessment fees range from $4k-$6k (Level 1) to $105k-$118k (Level 2).
    • DoD 3-year small business CMMC estimate totals ~$487,970.
    • FedRAMP readiness programs are priced at $300k-$500k with total compliance costs $250k-$750k+.
    • Cleared technical professionals average ~$119,131; senior cleared engineers reach $167k-$240k+.
    • Cleared bill rates cluster around $90-$225/hour, with IR leads at $250-$400+/hour.
    • Top Secret clearance adds ~$20k-$30k to base compensation.
    • A&D pay medians show DevSecOps at $133,931 and cyber consultants at $121,697.
    • Threat intelligence in A&D shows $117,371 median; SOC roles show $101,846.
    • Security training instructor pay in A&D is listed at $85,316.
    • Government pentest engagements carry $50k-$500k budgets due to FedRAMP/FISMA/DoD requirements.
    • Defense contracting networking requires clearance or ability to obtain clearance.
    • ITAR/EAR export control awareness is required on sensitive defense programs.
    • vCISO rates range $200-$350/hour; CMMC/FedRAMP specialists range $350-$600/hour.
    • Gov contractor vCISO retainers run ~$8k-$15k per month.
    • OnFrontiers expert network rates list $250-$500/hour for federal contractor expertise.
    • CMMC readiness case study shows $60k-$100k fees over 6-9 months.

    Treasury ~$150M Financial sector infrastructure, risk analytics

    2.2 Macro Demand Drivers (Internal)¶

    • Federal cyber spending is projected at ~$20.4B for FY2025.
    • DoD remains the largest opportunity pool for defense-aligned consultants.
    • CMMC 2.0 rollout is mandatory for defense contractors and generates recurring assessment cycles.
    • DIB market size is cited as hundreds of thousands of defense contractors.
    • DevSecOps market growth is cited at $3.7B (2021) to $41.6B (2030).
    • Compliance programs (CMMC, FedRAMP) create recurring readiness and remediation work.
    • Security awareness programs are expanding due to regulations including CMMC.
    • LinkedIn market signals highlight CMMC 2.0 as a key US regulation driver.

    2.3 Demand Anchors for A&D Consulting¶

    • CUI handling and secure delivery operations are mandatory for federal work.
    • Zero Trust mandates drive identity, segmentation, and policy enforcement work.
    • Supply-chain security initiatives increase vendor vetting and SBOM work.
    • Legacy modernization continues to drive integration and migration security projects.
    • Government pentest budgets are $50k-$500k for mandatory compliance testing.

    3. Aerospace & Defense Buyer Map¶

    3.1 Core Buyer Segments¶

    • Department of Defense (primary budget owner).
    • Defense agencies and mission programs requiring cleared support.
    • Civilian agencies with defense-adjacent mandates (CISA, HHS).
    • Prime contractors and major integrators (SETA primes and HACS holders).
    • Defense Industrial Base (DIB) subcontractors and suppliers.
    • Aerospace and defense manufacturers with export-controlled programs.

    3.2 Example A&D and Defense Brand Signals (Internal)¶

    • Lockheed Martin appears as a top A&D employer in salary benchmarking.
    • US Department of Defense is explicitly listed in salary and platform client lists.
    • Thales Group is tagged as Defense/Aerospace in internal search logs.

    3.3 Acquisition Paths (Federal Market)¶

    • GSA MAS SIN 54151HACS is the default acquisition path for cyber services.
    • Subcontracting under existing primes is the fastest entry path for new consultants.
    • SETA roles offer stable income but require clearance and create OCI constraints.
    • IDIQs, GWACs, and BPAs dominate long-term delivery vehicles.

    3.4 Buyer Priorities (Observed)¶

    • Compliance readiness (CMMC, RMF, FedRAMP).
    • Cleared staffing for on-site or SCIF delivery.
    • Evidence-based documentation (SSP, SAR, POA&M).
    • Incident response readiness and exercises.
    • Continuous monitoring and vulnerability management.

    4. Regulatory & Compliance Stack (Defense-Adjacent)¶

    4.1 Federal Compliance Core¶

    • FISMA for federal system security modernization.
    • NIST 800-53 for baseline control requirements.
    • NIST RMF lifecycle (Categorize Select Implement Assess Authorize Monitor).
    • NIST 800-171 for CUI protection requirements.

    4.2 Defense-Contractor Specific¶

    • CMMC 2.0 aligned to NIST 800-171 and focused on CUI protection.
    • DFARS clauses that require safeguarding CUI (referenced in CMMC training content).
    • FAR / DFARS considerations for contract compliance.

    4.3 Cloud & Authorization¶

    • FedRAMP authorization for federal cloud services.
    • FedRAMP aligned to NIST 800-53 moderate baseline.

    4.4 Export Controls & Sensitive Programs¶

    • ITAR/EAR export control awareness is required on sensitive programs.
    • Export control checks should be embedded in delivery onboarding.

    4.5 Executive Orders & Policy Drivers¶

    • EO 14028 and associated guidance drive Zero Trust, logging, and secure software supply chain work.

    4.6 Compliance Artifacts That Drive Consulting Hours¶

    • System Security Plan (SSP).
    • Security Assessment Plan (SAP).
    • Security Assessment Report (SAR).
    • Plan of Actions and Milestones (POA&M).
    • Evidence mapping and control narratives.
    • Continuous monitoring strategy and reporting cadence.

    5. Clearance & Eligibility Requirements¶

    5.1 Clearance Gating (Defense Market)¶

    • Defense contracting networking is closed-loop; clearance is expected or required.
    • SETA roles almost universally require TS/SCI clearance and on-site presence.
    • Cleared SMEs reduce competition but increase delivery constraints.

    5.2 Clearance Premium Signals¶

    • Top Secret clearance adds ~$20k-$30k to base compensation (internal signal).
    • Cleared technical pay averages ~$119,131 with senior roles at $167k-$240k+.
    • Vulnerability researcher roles with Top Secret clearance show $170k-$270k ranges (Arlington, VA).

    5.3 DoD Baseline Certifications¶

    • Security+ is often required baseline for DoD 8570 compliance.
    • Government/DoD cert preferences include OSCP, GPEN, Security+.
    • CEH is listed as ANSI-accredited and DoD 8140 approved.

    5.4 CMMC Certification Prerequisites (CCP)¶

    • DoD Tier 3 investigation or NAC/DoD clearance required.
    • DoD CUI Awareness Training required within 3 months of exam.
    • 2+ years of cyber/IT experience or a technical degree is acceptable.
    • Training includes CMMC 2.0, NIST SP 800-171, FAR, DFARS.

    5.5 Platform Vetting Signals (Defense-Adjacent)¶

    • DoD-aligned PTaaS platforms use background checks and identity verification.
    • Citizenship/residency validation appears in platform vetting processes.
    • Sanctioned-country restrictions are enforced by at least one major PTaaS platform.

    7. CMMC Consulting Economics (Defense Sector)¶

    7.1 Market Overview¶

    • CMMC is mandatory for defense contractors in the DoD supply chain.
    • CMMC 2.0 rollout starting 2025 creates massive demand for consultants and assessors.
    • Market size is cited as hundreds of thousands of defense contractors.
    • Re-assessment cycles occur every 3 years.

    7.2 Project Fee Ranges¶

    • Documentation assistance: $15,000-$40,000.
    • Level 2 readiness project: $15,000-$40,000.
    • Larger readiness projects: $50,000-$300,000.
    • Gap assessments: $3,500-$20,000.
    • Mock audits (small/prepared): $10,000-$30,000.
    • Mock audits (extensive support): $30,000-$50,000.
    • Comprehensive certified assessment: $30,000-$100,000+.
    • Remediation and implementation: $35,000-$250,000+.

    7.3 C3PAO Assessment Fees (DoD Estimates)¶

    • Level 1 certification: $4,000-$6,000.
    • Level 2 self-assessment: $37,000-$49,000.
    • Level 2 C3PAO assessment: $105,000-$118,000.
    • Level 3: Level 2 costs + ~$41,000.

    7.4 Total Compliance Cost Signals¶

    • Small (<50 employees) Level 1: $5,000-$10,000.
    • Small (<50 employees) Level 2: $50,000-$150,000.
    • Large organizations Level 2/3: hundreds of thousands to millions.
    • Overall range cited: $5,000-$300,000+ (excluding larger enterprise cases).
    • DoD 3-year small business estimate: ~$487,970 total.
    • Initial implementation: ~$175,700 (labor ~$148,200 + hardware/software ~$27,500).
    • Recurring annual: ~$103,800.
    • Other costs: ~$104,670.

    7.5 Consultant Income Projections (Signals)¶

    • Independent CMMC consultant: 20 hours/week @ $300/hour = $312,000/year.
    • Project model: 10 readiness projects/year @ $25,000 = $250,000/year.
    • Mixed model: 5 readiness + 10 gap assessments = $225,000/year.

    7.6 Certification Requirements (CCP)¶

    • College degree in technical field OR 2+ years in cyber/IT.
    • DoD Tier 3 investigation OR NAC/DoD clearance.
    • DoD CUI Awareness Training within 3 months of exam.
    • Training: 5 days from licensed training provider.
    • Exam covers CMMC 2.0, NIST SP 800-171, FAR, DFARS.

    14. Delivery Playbooks (Synthesis)¶

    14.1 CMMC Readiness Workflow (Synthesis)¶

    • Define the CUI boundary and system inventory.
    • Map existing controls to NIST 800-171 requirements.
    • Conduct gap assessment and document missing evidence.
    • Build a remediation roadmap with owners and timelines.
    • Draft and update SSP and policy suite.
    • Establish POA&M tracking and evidence repository.
    • Implement technical controls and process fixes.
    • Run mock assessment and close audit findings.
    • Prepare assessment package for C3PAO or self-assessment.
    • Plan for 3-year re-assessment cycle.

    14.2 FedRAMP Readiness Workflow (Synthesis)¶

    • Determine FedRAMP baseline target (Moderate typical).
    • Complete readiness gap analysis and sponsor strategy.
    • Build SSP and control narratives aligned to NIST 800-53.
    • Collect evidence artifacts and implement monitoring plan.
    • Coordinate with 3PAO for assessment scheduling.
    • Address SAR findings and close POA&M items.
    • Establish continuous monitoring cadence and reporting.

    14.3 Zero Trust Implementation Workflow (Synthesis)¶

    • Perform identity and access management hardening.
    • Define network segmentation and micro-perimeters.
    • Integrate EDR and endpoint telemetry.
    • Establish policy-as-code for access and segmentation.
    • Map deliverables to EO 14028 and agency ZT guidance.
