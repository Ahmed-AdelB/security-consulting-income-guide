## Executive Summary

The cybersecurity freelance market continues to be defined by chronic talent shortages, aggressive compliance requirements, and high-impact security risks. This combination sustains premium pricing for senior specialists while simultaneously creating a crowded, price-competitive market for generalist roles. The three dominant platforms in this report occupy distinct positions in the market:

- **Toptal:** Premium tier, heavy vetting, high client budgets, and top-end rates of $60-$250+/hour with 0% freelancer commission but significant client-side markups.
- **Upwork:** The volume leader, with median cybersecurity rates around $60/hour, typical range $40-$90/hour, and a flat 10% freelancer fee. Strong escrow protections but high competitive pressure.
- **Fiverr:** A gig-based marketplace optimized for packaged services. Typical security task pricing begins at $22-$30 for basic services, while Fiverr Pro listings can command $100+/hour-equivalent. Platform fee is 20% for freelancers, the highest among major platforms.

**Key Strategic Takeaways:**

- The most effective pricing strategy is a **three-tier model** with a low-friction entry package, a revenue-efficient core package, and a premium package that anchors value.
- **Net rate targeting** is essential. A $100/hour desired net rate becomes $111/hour on Upwork and $125/hour on Fiverr after platform fees.
- **Success stories** repeatedly highlight escrow reliability on Upwork, premium-client access on Toptal, and rapid client acquisition on Fiverr for well-packaged offers.
- **Raising rates** in planned increments (e.g., $50/hour every 3 months after reviews) outperforms “set-it-and-forget-it” pricing.
- The **highest earnings** come from combining platform acquisition with long-term direct relationships, particularly for vCISO retainers ($1,600-$20,000/month) and compliance packages ($5,000-$50,000+ per project).

## Market Context: Cybersecurity Freelancing in 2025

### Demand and Pricing Environment

The cybersecurity workforce is characterized by persistent gaps between demand and supply. Internal research notes cite **457,398 cybersecurity job openings** in the U.S. and **750,000+ unfilled roles**, with a global shortage exceeding **3.5 million positions**. This gap keeps specialized rates high but creates intense competition for entry-level and generalist freelancers.

**Rate anchors from internal research:**

- Global average freelance cybersecurity consultant rate: **~$144/hour**
- U.S. average: **$63.41/hour** (~$131,892/year)
- Senior specialists: **$100-$200+/hour**
- vCISO retainers: **$1,600-$20,000/month**
- Compliance readiness packages: **$5,000-$50,000+** depending on scope

### Security Service Categories Driving Demand

1. **Compliance & GRC:** SOC 2, ISO 27001, PCI DSS, GDPR, HIPAA, FedRAMP.
2. **Penetration Testing:** Web, mobile, cloud, network, and API testing.
3. **Cloud Security:** AWS, Azure, GCP architecture reviews and IAM hardening.
4. **Incident Response:** Breach response, forensics, and containment support.
5. **vCISO & Strategy:** Executive advisory, security roadmap, and governance.
6. **Secure SDLC & DevSecOps:** CI/CD security, SAST/DAST, and code review.

### Pricing Pressure vs. Premium Positioning

Internal guides emphasize a tension between platform-driven pricing pressure and premium consulting rates. For example, GRC consulting notes indicate **freelance marketplaces often compress rates to $60-$90/hour**, while direct B2B consulting sustains **$200+/hour** in specialized niches. Platforms are useful for discovery, but long-term profitability usually requires retainer relationships or specialized packages.

## Platform Deep Dive: Toptal (Premium Tier)

### Positioning & Market Fit

Toptal positions itself as a premium marketplace for the **top 3% of global talent**. The platform is optimized for senior consultants, security architects, and experienced penetration testers who can command premium rates and handle complex, long-term client engagements.

**Best fit:**

- Senior cybersecurity consultants (5+ years)
- Specialists with recognized certifications (CISSP, CISM, OSCP)
- Security leaders offering vCISO-level advisory services
- Consultants targeting enterprise or well-funded startups

### Acceptance & Vetting (Internal Research Summary)

- Acceptance rate: **<3%**
- Multi-stage vetting: language/communication screening, personality assessment, technical evaluation, and experience validation
- Minimum experience: **2+ years**, but successful applicants typically exceed 5 years

### Security Services Typically Offered

- vCISO / security strategy advisory
- Security risk assessment and program development
- Penetration testing and vulnerability management
- Secure code review and architecture validation
- Compliance implementation (ISO 27001, SOC 2, PCI, HITRUST)

### Rate Benchmarks and Fee Structure

**Freelancer Rate Range:** $60-$250+/hour (internal research notes emphasize higher-than-market positioning)

**Fees:**

- Freelancer commission: **0%** (freelancers keep their stated rate)
- Client fees: $79/month subscription, $500 initial deposit, and reported markups up to 50%
- Buyout fee: **$50,000** for full-time hires

### Payment Reliability

Toptal manages invoicing, billing, and disputes directly, resulting in **strong payment reliability**. Internal notes indicate freelancers report minimal payment issues compared to open marketplaces.

### Strengths and Constraints

**Strengths:**

- Premium client base and higher budgets
- Strong payment handling and managed billing
- Brand signal (“top 3%”) boosts external credibility
- Higher probability of longer-term contracts

**Constraints:**

- Extremely competitive entry requirements
- Client-side fees can deter some mid-market clients
- Vetting timeline slows onboarding for urgent engagements

### Success Stories & Sentiment (Extracted)

- General consensus from internal user experience notes: **“Premium clients and rates, but nearly impossible to get in (3%) and high client-side fees.”**
- Trustpilot synthesis positions Toptal as a **premium tier platform** with high ratings and strong recommendation rates.

### Pricing Strategy for Toptal Freelancers

**1) Anchor at premium rates.**
Because Toptal clients already pay a premium, underpricing often signals lower confidence rather than competitiveness. Start with a clear rate that aligns to senior-market expectations ($120-$200+/hour) and adjust based on specialization.

**2) Lead with business outcomes.**
Pitch engagements around outcomes like “reduced audit risk,” “faster compliance readiness,” or “secure cloud migration,” rather than listing tools. Premium pricing is easier to sustain when tied to cost avoidance.

**3) Use retainer structures.**
For vCISO or security program management, convert engagements into **monthly retainers** ($5k-$20k/month) with defined meeting cadence and deliverables.

**4) Set minimum engagement size.**
To protect margin and focus on quality clients, define a minimum duration (e.g., 20-40 hours or 1-month retainers). Toptal’s premium positioning supports this.

**5) Showcase proof of impact.**
Provide pre-validated case studies with measurable outcomes (e.g., audit completion time reduced by 30%, vulnerabilities reduced by 70%). This supports high-rate justification.

## Platform Deep Dive: Upwork (Volume Marketplace)

### Positioning & Market Fit

Upwork is the largest global freelancing marketplace with **12M+ freelancers** across multiple categories. It is a high-volume platform suitable for entry to mid-level security consultants, as well as senior professionals who can differentiate with specialized expertise and strong reviews.

**Best fit:**

- Consultants building a portfolio and client base
- Specialists in high-demand areas (cloud security, compliance, pentesting)
- Freelancers comfortable with active proposal writing and competitive bidding

### Key Platform Indicators (Internal Research Summary)

- Client rating: **4.7/5.0**
- Cybersecurity developer median rate: **$60/hour**
- Typical range: **$40-$90/hour**
- Network security engineer median: **$35/hour** (range $25-$55)

### Service Categories and Job Types

- Security assessments and risk analysis
- Cloud security reviews (AWS/Azure/GCP)
- SOC 2 / ISO 27001 readiness support
- Penetration testing (web, network, API)
- Security questionnaires and vendor risk responses
- Security policy writing and governance documentation

### Fee Structure (Updated in Internal Research)

- Freelancer fee: **10% flat** service fee
- Client fees: **5.5% transaction fee** on orders and **$14.95 initiation fee** on new contracts
- Marketplace fees: 5%-8% service fee for Talent Marketplace transactions

### Payment Reliability and Risks

**Strength:** Escrow system provides strong payment protection.

**Risk:** Fake job postings and scope creep are known issues. Internal user feedback highlights both reliable payments and occasional disputes.

### Success Stories & Sentiment (Extracted)

- **Upwork user (4 years):** “Never had to chase payment thanks to escrow.”
- General consensus: “Great for building social proof despite 10% fee, but beware race-to-the-bottom pricing and unethical clients.”

### Pricing Strategy for Upwork Freelancers

**1) Start with a “proof package.”**
Offer a smaller fixed-price assessment (e.g., $400-$800) to build initial reviews, then upsell to larger projects.

**2) Price with net-rate math.**
To achieve a net $80/hour, set a gross rate of **$89/hour** ($80 / 0.9). For net $120/hour, set **$133/hour**.

**3) Use milestone-based billing.**
Break security projects into milestones (discovery → assessment → report → remediation support). Milestones reduce risk and improve client confidence.

**4) Specialize and stack credentials.**
Internal research highlights that **niche specialization** (cloud security, compliance) outperforms generalist profiles. Align proposals with certifications (CISSP, CISM, OSCP).

**5) Raise rates incrementally.**
The internal experience guide recommends **raising rates by $50/hour every 3 months** after accumulating strong reviews. This avoids sudden pricing shocks to clients.

**6) Maintain a response SLA.**
Fast response times (under 1 hour) materially improve win rates on competitive listings. Mentioning response SLAs can increase perceived professionalism.

## Platform Deep Dive: Fiverr (Gig Marketplace)

### Positioning & Market Fit

Fiverr operates as a gig-based marketplace, optimized for productized services. Security consultants succeed on Fiverr when they package clearly defined deliverables with transparent pricing tiers. Fiverr Pro provides a vetted tier with premium positioning.

**Best fit:**

- Consultants offering smaller, repeatable security services
- Early to mid-level professionals building client base
- Specialists able to productize audits, scanning, or compliance checklists

### Rate Benchmarks and Fee Structure

**Typical security task pricing:** $22-$30 for basic tasks (entry-level gigs)

**Fiverr Pro:** Rates can exceed $100/hour-equivalent when package pricing and upsells are structured effectively.

**Fees:**

- Freelancer commission: **20%** (highest among major platforms)
- Client fee: **5.5% transaction fee** on orders and tips
- Fiverr Business/Pro subscription: **$149/year** for team access

### Pro Seller Examples (Internal Research Highlights)

- Highly rated Pro consultants with 4.8-5.0 ratings in penetration testing, cloud security, and CISO advisory services.

### Payment Reliability and Risks

**Strength:** Platform manages payments and holds funds until delivery approval.

**Risk:** High fees compress margins. Lower client quality on standard tiers can lead to scope creep and underpricing.

### Success Stories & Sentiment (Extracted)

- **Reddit entrepreneur:** “Two Fiverr devs became equity partners.”
- General consensus: “Lower quality clients, good for quick gigs, but 20% fee is brutal.”

### Pricing Strategy for Fiverr Sellers

**1) Package everything.**
Fiverr rewards clear, productized offerings. Package security audits or assessments into Basic / Standard / Premium tiers with scoped deliverables and response times.

**2) Use gig extras to restore margin.**
Because of the 20% fee, extras (rush delivery, executive summary, compliance mapping) are critical to preserve profitability.

**3) Build a funnel of repeatable work.**
Use Fiverr gigs as entry points, then build a retainer pipeline (e.g., monthly security monitoring or quarterly risk assessments).

**4) Leverage the Pro badge.**
If eligible, upgrade to Fiverr Pro to escape low-budget demand and justify higher rates.

**5) Anchor with premium tier.**
Position the Premium package as a value anchor, enabling the Standard package to appear more affordable while still achieving healthy margins.


## Extended Platform Playbooks (Workflow, Profile, Positioning)

This section expands the tactical playbook for each platform to include workflow design, profile optimization, and conversion tactics. The intent is to move beyond high-level pricing and provide end-to-end guidance for how security freelancers should operate inside each marketplace.

### Toptal Playbook: From Vetting to Long-Term Retainers

**Positioning goals:**

- Showcase seniority and specialization (cloud security, compliance, vCISO, pen testing).
- Emphasize business impact rather than tool proficiency.
- Provide evidence of leadership in incident response, governance, or security architecture.

**Preparation before application:**

1. **Audit your professional narrative.** Toptal’s “top 3%” brand is built on credibility. A strong narrative should highlight security outcomes, not just tasks. Example: “Reduced audit prep time by 30% for fintech clients” is more compelling than “Reviewed security policies.”
2. **Create a sanitized portfolio.** Compile two to three redacted reports (penetration test, risk assessment, compliance mapping) to demonstrate methodology.
3. **Define a clear niche.** Strong applications highlight specialization. In internal research, specialists consistently justify premium rates more effectively than generalists.

**Engagement workflow:**

1. **Matching phase:** Toptal matches you with client needs. Emphasize clarity in project scope and desired outcomes.
2. **Trial period:** Many engagements start with a trial. Offer a clear “first two weeks” plan with deliverables (e.g., assessment plan, initial findings report).
3. **Full engagement:** Transition to retainer or milestone-based contracts with defined deliverables and cadence.
4. **Ongoing reporting:** Provide weekly updates and executive summaries to build trust and justify continued premium pricing.

**Common pitfalls to avoid:**

- Underpricing to secure a project (signals lower confidence in premium environment).
- Overextending scope during trial periods without clear milestone alignment.
- Neglecting executive communication (decision makers pay for outcomes and clarity).

**Suggested positioning assets:**

- “vCISO readiness” one-pager
- Risk register template and sample executive summary
- Proof of compliance outcomes (SOC 2 readiness, ISO audits)

### Upwork Playbook: Winning in a High-Volume Marketplace

**Positioning goals:**

- Stand out in search through specialization and keyword alignment.
- Build early social proof quickly.
- Maintain a response time advantage to win competitive listings.

**Profile optimization steps:**

1. **Headline clarity:** Use niche-specific titles (“Cloud Security Architect – AWS/Azure Hardening & SOC 2 Readiness”) rather than generic labels.
2. **Credential stacking:** Certifications are trust accelerators on Upwork. Place CISSP, CISM, OSCP, and Security+ near the top.
3. **Portfolio coverage:** Include at least one report artifact and one process diagram (security roadmap, architecture diagram, or compliance matrix).
4. **Case-study bullets:** Use short outcome-based bullets rather than long narratives.

**Proposal workflow:**

1. **First 2 lines:** State the client’s problem in their language.
2. **Next 2 lines:** Provide a concise plan for delivery.
3. **Deliverables list:** Provide a concrete checklist of outputs.
4. **Timeline and fee:** Present a milestone or hourly proposal aligned with scope.

**Upwork selection strategy:**

- Prioritize listings with clear scope and reasonable budgets.
- Avoid listings with vague or unrealistic deliverables.
- Track win rates by niche (cloud, GRC, penetration testing) and focus on high-conversion categories.

**Rate ladder recommendations:**

- Stage 1 (0-5 reviews): $40-$60/hour with small fixed-price packages for reviews.
- Stage 2 (6-20 reviews): $70-$110/hour with larger scoped projects.
- Stage 3 (20+ reviews): $120-$180/hour for high-complexity work.

### Fiverr Playbook: Productization and Conversion

**Positioning goals:**

- Build productized security offerings with clear scope and fixed outcomes.
- Maximize visibility by aligning gig titles with client search behavior.
- Protect margins using add-ons and premium tiers.

**Gig construction workflow:**

1. **Keyword alignment:** Use keywords that match client search behavior (e.g., “SOC 2 readiness,” “cloud security audit,” “penetration testing report”).
2. **Visual proof:** Include screenshots of anonymized deliverables (risk register, report templates).
3. **Gig tiers:** Basic, Standard, Premium should increase scope, not just delivery time.
4. **FAQ clarity:** Address scope boundaries to reduce revision risk.
5. **Delivery checklist:** Provide an onboarding questionnaire to accelerate project start.

**Pricing ladder on Fiverr:**

- Basic: Entry-level assessments ($150-$300)
- Standard: Full assessment ($500-$1,200)
- Premium: Assessment + remediation + compliance mapping ($1,500-$3,000+)

**Retention playbook:**

- Use post-delivery reports to propose monthly check-ins.
- Create optional quarterly security review packages as add-ons.
- Offer a discounted retainer if client commits to 3+ months.

## Pricing Strategy Playbook (Cross-Platform)

### 1) Rate Architecture by Experience Level

Internal research suggests the following baseline ranges for cybersecurity freelancers:

- **Beginner (0-2 years):** $40-$60/hour
- **Mid-level (3-5 years):** $60-$100/hour
- **Senior (5+ years):** $100-$200+/hour
- **Specialist/vCISO:** $150-$250+/hour or $5k-$20k/month retainer

### 2) Hourly vs. Fixed vs. Retainer

- **Hourly:** Best for uncertain scopes or ongoing operational support.
- **Fixed-price projects:** Best for defined deliverables like security assessments or compliance readiness.
- **Retainers:** Most profitable for ongoing security governance or vCISO services.

### 3) Platform Fee Gross-Up Strategy

- Always calculate fees before publishing a rate.
- Example: Desired net $120/hour on Fiverr requires a $150/hour equivalent package price.
- Build fee recovery into extras and add-ons rather than raising base price alone.

### 4) Value-Based Pricing (Premium Positioning)

Value-based pricing anchors on business outcomes rather than time. This is particularly effective for compliance and risk reduction projects:

- SOC 2 readiness: $5,000-$20,000 per project
- ISO 27001 implementation: $30,000-$50,000 end-to-end
- GDPR DPO-as-a-Service: $2,000-$8,000/month retainers

Value framing example: “This audit readiness project unlocks enterprise deals worth $200k+ and reduces breach risk.”

### 5) Tiered Packages (Especially for Fiverr)

**Template structure:**

- **Basic:** Quick scan, 5-10 findings, no remediation support
- **Standard:** Full assessment, prioritized findings, remediation roadmap
- **Premium:** Full assessment + remediation guidance + compliance mapping

This structure creates a clear upgrade path and improves average order value.

### 6) Raising Rates Strategically

Internal experience guidance recommends **increasing rates by $50/hour every 3 months** for consultants who consistently achieve strong reviews. Use milestones (e.g., 10 reviews, 20 reviews) to justify rate increments.

### 7) Deposits and Milestones

Internal best practices emphasize **25%-50% upfront deposits** and milestone-based payments for larger projects. Platforms with escrow (Upwork) make this easier, while Fiverr requires clear scope boundaries to avoid scope creep.

## Platform-Specific Pricing Strategy

### Toptal Pricing Strategy Blueprint

1. **Start with premium anchors:** $120-$180/hour for senior consultants; $200+ for specialized vCISO or compliance leads.
2. **Retainer first:** Offer monthly governance support (security roadmap, risk register updates, executive briefings).
3. **Position as enterprise-ready:** Use audit-ready deliverables and include executive summaries.

1. **Entry package:** $400-$800 fixed-price security assessment to win reviews.
2. **Mid-tier hourly rate:** $70-$110/hour once Top Rated or consistent reviews are achieved.
3. **Proposal differentiation:** Include a short plan of attack and immediate deliverables in every proposal.
4. **Specialization focus:** Cloud security, SOC 2 readiness, or API security yield higher acceptance rates.
5. **Outcomes > hours:** Tie proposals to measurable outputs like “50 prioritized findings” or “SOC 2 gap analysis report.”

### Fiverr Pricing Strategy Blueprint

1. **Package clarity:** Ensure each tier has explicit scope boundaries and deliverables.
2. **Use “extras” to recover fees:** Rush delivery, compliance mapping, and remediation coaching.
3. **Premium upsell:** Position Pro services as executive-level or compliance-oriented.
4. **Gig SEO:** Use targeted keywords such as “SOC 2 readiness,” “cloud security audit,” and “penetration testing report.”
5. **Repeatable workflows:** Standardize templates to deliver quickly and protect margins.

## Tactical Proposal Frameworks

### Short Proposal Template (Upwork)

## Pricing Mechanics and Negotiation (Deep Dive)

Pricing in cybersecurity consulting is not only about hourly rates—it reflects perceived risk reduction, compliance acceleration, and executive confidence. The following tactics build pricing power across all three platforms.

### 1) The “Risk Offset” Narrative

Clients pay for risk reduction more than for hours. For example, an ISO 27001 implementation at $40,000 can feel inexpensive when it enables a $500,000 enterprise deal. Frame proposals in terms of risk offset and opportunity creation.

### 2) Tiered Value Anchoring

Offer three tiers to anchor value:

- **Basic (compliance check):** minimal effort, lower price.
- **Standard (full assessment):** core value and recommended choice.
- **Premium (assessment + remediation + executive brief):** highest margin and anchor.

### 3) Discovery as a Paid Product

Charge for scoping work. A $500-$1,500 “security discovery sprint” can reduce unpaid pre-work and improve proposal accuracy. This is particularly effective on Upwork and Toptal where clients value formal planning.

### 4) Cost of Delay Pricing

Quantify the cost of delay (e.g., missed compliance deadlines or breach exposure). Position premium pricing as a method to reduce the cost of delay.

### 5) Escalation-based Pricing

Offer discounts for longer commitments (3-6 month retainers), but charge higher rates for urgent, high-intensity work (incident response or short timelines).

### 6) Negotiation Script Example

“Based on your timeline and audit objectives, I recommend the Standard package at $7,500. It includes full gap analysis and remediation planning. If you need executive board-level reporting, the Premium package at $10,500 includes quarterly governance presentations. Either option helps you reach audit readiness in 6-8 weeks.”

## Reporting & Deliverable Standards for Security Freelancers

Clients often decide whether to rehire a freelancer based on the **quality of documentation**. Consistent, executive-ready reporting is a key differentiator for premium pricing.

### Minimum Report Structure (Assessment or Pentest)

1. **Executive summary:** Risks, business impact, and priority actions.
2. **Scope & methodology:** Tools and frameworks used (OWASP, NIST, CIS).
3. **Findings overview:** Summary table with severity and affected assets.
4. **Detailed findings:** Evidence, reproduction steps, impact, remediation.
5. **Risk register:** Prioritized remediation roadmap.
6. **Appendix:** Technical detail, tooling output, and references.

### Standardization Tips

- Create a reusable report template for each service type.
- Provide both a technical appendix and executive summary.
- Use consistent severity scoring (e.g., CVSS scale).
- Include remediation timelines (short, mid, long term) to guide clients.

### Executive Communication Enhancements

- Provide a one-page “security posture snapshot.”
- Use visual charts for vulnerability density or compliance coverage.
- Highlight the top three actions that deliver maximum risk reduction.

### Overhead Considerations

- Tools and licenses (scanners, cloud labs, compliance tools)
- Insurance (professional liability, E&O)
- Proposal time and platform fees

Incorporate a 10%-20% overhead margin into pricing to ensure profitability.

## Client Qualification & Red Flags

Selecting the right clients is as important as choosing the right platform. The following checklist helps minimize disputes, scope creep, and payment risk.

### Positive Client Signals

- Clear scope definition and well-written job post.
- Budget aligns with expected scope and complexity.
- Willingness to provide access and documentation quickly.
- Decision makers present in kickoff calls.
- Prior experience working with security consultants.

### Red Flags (Proceed With Caution)

- Vague requirements such as “make us secure.”
- Unrealistic timelines for complex audits or penetration testing.
- Low budgets paired with large scope demands.
- Requests to communicate off-platform immediately.
- Reluctance to provide asset inventory or environment access.

### Qualification Questions

- What is the business objective driving this engagement?
- What deadlines or external dependencies exist (audits, product launch)?
- Who will review the final deliverables and make decisions?
- What internal resources are available to support remediation?

### Scope Control Tactics

- Require written approval for scope changes.
- Maintain a change request template and log.
- Offer fixed-price packages for defined outputs.

## Appendix C: Rate Scenario Workbook

Use this workbook-style appendix to benchmark pricing decisions across platforms. The goal is to connect target annual income to required billable hours and platform-specific rates.

### Step 1: Define Target Income

Example targets:

- Conservative: $80,000/year
- Moderate: $150,000/year
- Aggressive: $250,000/year

### Step 2: Estimate Billable Utilization

- Conservative utilization: 20 billable hours/week
- Moderate utilization: 25 billable hours/week
- Aggressive utilization: 30 billable hours/week

### Step 3: Calculate Required Net Hourly Rate

Formula:

`Required Net Rate = Target Annual Income / (Billable Hours per Week × 48 weeks)`

Examples:

- $150,000 / (25 × 48) ≈ $125/hour net
- $250,000 / (30 × 48) ≈ $173/hour net

### Step 4: Convert Net Rate to Platform Gross Rate

**Upwork (10% fee):** Net ÷ 0.90

- $125 net → $139/hour gross
- $173 net → $192/hour gross

**Fiverr (20% fee):** Net ÷ 0.80

- $125 net → $156/hour gross
- $173 net → $216/hour gross

**Toptal (0% fee):** Net = Gross

- $125 net → $125/hour gross
- $173 net → $173/hour gross

### Step 5: Translate to Package Pricing

If a security assessment takes 20 hours at a $150/hour net target, the base package should be priced at $3,000. On Fiverr, that same package would need to be priced around $3,750 to maintain net earnings.

### Step 6: Validate Against Market Benchmarks

Compare your required rate to platform benchmarks:

- If your required rate is significantly above platform averages, focus on premium niches or retainer models.
- If your required rate is below market ranges, you have room to raise prices or reduce workload.
