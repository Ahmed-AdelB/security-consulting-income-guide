    GRC Compliance Consulting Research (SOC 2, ISO/IEC 27001, GDPR)¶

    Scope, methodology, and limitations¶

    This report consolidates practical knowledge about GRC compliance consulting for SOC 2, ISO/IEC 27001, and GDPR. It is designed to help buyers of consulting services understand typical engagement models, deliverables, and indicative rate ranges. The content reflects general industry practice and publicly known framework requirements, but it does not include live, real-time pricing data or direct citations. Use this document as a structured starting point, then validate with current market quotes, regional firms, and legal counsel.

    Important limitation: due to the environment constraints for this draft, the rate ranges are indicative and should be treated as hypotheses. Always validate via competitive bids and legal review, and adjust for geography, sector risk, and regulatory exposure.

    Executive summary¶

    GRC (governance, risk, and compliance) consulting demand remains strong across SaaS, fintech, healthcare, and supply-chain organizations. SOC 2 and ISO/IEC 27001 are frequently used as assurance signals for customers and vendors, while GDPR is a regulatory requirement for any organization handling EU personal data. Most consulting programs fall into one of three modes: a short readiness assessment, a full program build (policies, controls, evidence), or ongoing compliance operations. Pricing varies widely by provider type and region, but common structures include hourly rates, fixed-fee projects, and monthly retainers for vCISO or compliance operations.

    Key themes: - Integration wins: SOC 2, ISO 27001, and GDPR can share a single control library and evidence plan if built intentionally. - Rates vary most by provider type: independent consultants or boutique firms tend to offer the best cost/value for mid-market scope; large firms command higher rates for brand and scale. - Readiness > audit: the core consulting spend is typically before audit/certification; the audit fee is a separate line item. - Documentation is necessary but insufficient: achieving compliance is less about policy paperwork and more about consistent operational evidence.

    Market landscape and demand drivers¶

    Demand drivers: - Enterprise procurement requirements (SOC 2 or ISO 27001 demanded by customers). - Regulatory exposure and enforcement (GDPR fines and data breach response). - M&A and investor readiness (due diligence readiness and security posture). - Competitive differentiation for SaaS providers and managed services. - Insurance underwriting expectations (cyber insurance requiring minimum controls).

    Market dynamics: - Consulting supply is bifurcated: large firms with high margins, and specialized boutiques/independents with strong cost efficiency. - Talent scarcity in security governance, privacy engineering, and audit preparation elevates rates. - Tooling (GRC platforms, evidence collection tools) can reduce hours but add subscription cost. - Multi-framework alignment is increasingly expected, reducing duplicated work across SOC 2, ISO 27001, and GDPR.

    Framework overview and consulting focus¶

    SOC 2 (AICPA Trust Services Criteria)¶

    Purpose: Provide assurance about controls related to security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy. SOC 2 reports are widely used in vendor risk management.

    Core elements: - Trust Services Criteria (TSC) categories and points of focus. - System description (scope, services, architecture, boundaries). - Control activities and evidence for design and operating effectiveness.

    Report types: - Type I: Control design at a point in time. - Type II: Control design and operating effectiveness over a period (typically 3–12 months).

    Consulting focus areas: - Scoping and boundary definition. - Control selection and mapping to TSC. - Policy and procedure development. - Evidence strategy and collection automation. - Remediation of gaps and control testing preparation. - Audit coordination and report review.

    ISO/IEC 27001:2022¶

    Purpose: Establish a certified Information Security Management System (ISMS) aligned to ISO standards. ISO 27001 certification is globally recognized and often required for international B2B contracts.

    Core elements: - ISMS scope and context. - Risk assessment and treatment plan. - Statement of Applicability (SoA) aligned to Annex A controls. - Internal audit and management review. - External certification audit (Stage 1 and Stage 2).

    Consulting focus areas: - ISMS design and documentation. - Risk assessment methodology and treatment plans. - SoA customization and control implementation. - Internal audit preparation and corrective actions. - Certification body selection and audit coordination.

    GDPR (EU General Data Protection Regulation)¶

    Purpose: Regulate processing of personal data for individuals in the EU/EEA. GDPR compliance is required for any organization that processes EU personal data or targets EU residents.

    Core elements: - Lawful bases for processing. - Data subject rights and procedures. - Records of Processing Activities (RoPA). - Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA). - Vendor and cross-border transfer management. - Security of processing and breach notification.

    Consulting focus areas: - Data mapping and RoPA creation. - Privacy governance, policies, and training. - DPIA frameworks and prior consultation triggers. - Contractual updates (DPAs, SCCs). - Incident response and breach notification workflow. - DPO (Data Protection Officer) advisory or outsourced DPO services.

    Integrated compliance strategy (SOC 2 + ISO 27001 + GDPR)¶

    An integrated approach reduces duplication by creating a shared control library, evidence plan, and policy framework. Common cross-framework elements include:

    • Governance: roles, responsibilities, risk ownership, management review.
    • Risk management: risk assessment methodology, risk treatment plans, exception handling.
    • Access control: identity lifecycle, least privilege, MFA, review cycles.
    • Change management: standardized deployments, approvals, testing evidence.
    • Incident response: detection, escalation, communication, post-incident review.
    • Vendor management: due diligence, contract clauses, monitoring.
    • Business continuity: backup strategy, disaster recovery testing.
    • Privacy and data handling: data retention, minimization, deletion workflows.

    Integrated consulting engagements typically map SOC 2 TSC points to ISO 27001 Annex A controls and GDPR obligations. This provides a unified narrative and reduces audit fatigue.

    Engagement models and deliverables¶

    Common engagement models¶

    1. Readiness assessment (4–8 weeks): quick diagnostic to identify gaps.
    2. Program build (8–24+ weeks): implement policies, controls, evidence.
    3. Audit/certification support (4–12 weeks): prepare for external audit.
    4. Continuous compliance retainer (ongoing): evidence collection, monitoring, audit prep.
    5. vCISO / privacy officer (ongoing): strategic governance and leadership.

    Typical deliverables¶

    • Scoping and boundary definition document.
    • Risk assessment report and risk register.
    • Control library and control owners.
    • Policy suite (security, privacy, vendor, change, incident, BCDR).
    • Evidence matrix with collection cadence.
    • Gap remediation plan with timelines.
    • Audit/assessment support and evidence packaging.

    Pricing structures and rate drivers¶

    Pricing structures¶

    • Hourly billing: common for ad-hoc advisory or remediation support.
    • Daily/weekly rate: typical for project-based engagements.
    • Fixed-fee projects: priced by scope and deliverables.
    • Monthly retainers: used for vCISO, compliance ops, or privacy officer services.
    • Hybrid: fixed-fee baseline with hourly out-of-scope adders.

    Rate drivers¶

    • Provider type: independent, boutique, mid-tier, large global firm.
    • Geography: US/Canada, UK/EU, APAC, LATAM.
    • Industry risk: regulated sectors (fintech, healthcare) command premium rates.
    • Company size: complexity, number of systems, and geographic footprint.
    • Audit readiness: starting from scratch vs. mature control environment.
    • Scope breadth: number of frameworks, scope of systems, and third parties.
    • Evidence tooling: integrated GRC tooling can reduce hours.
    • Timeline urgency: accelerated timelines add premium rates.

    Indicative rate ranges (consulting services)¶

    Note: The ranges below are indicative, not definitive. Treat these as planning estimates to inform budgeting and RFPs; validate with local bids.

    Hourly rate ranges by provider type (USD-equivalent)¶

    • Independent consultants: $75–$150/hour (general), $150–$250/hour (senior specialists).
    • Boutique GRC firms: $125–$250/hour (standard), $200–$350/hour (senior).
    • Mid-tier consultancies: $175–$325/hour (standard), $250–$450/hour (senior/partner).
    • Large global firms: $250–$500+/hour (standard), $400–$750+/hour (partner-led).
    • Offshore/nearshore resources: $40–$120/hour (analyst-to-senior).

    Day rate ranges (USD-equivalent)¶

    • Independent consultants: $600–$1,200/day.
    • Boutique firms: $1,000–$2,000/day.
    • Mid-tier firms: $1,400–$2,800/day.
    • Large global firms: $2,000–$4,500/day.

    Monthly retainer ranges (USD-equivalent)¶

    • vCISO / compliance lead (part-time): $3,000–$12,000/month.
    • Outsourced DPO (part-time): $2,500–$10,000/month.
    • Continuous compliance ops: $5,000–$20,000/month depending on scope.

    Regional modifiers (indicative)¶

    • US/Canada: baseline as above.
    • UK/Ireland: often 10–20% lower in GBP-equivalent, depending on market.
    • Western EU: similar to UK, with variance by country.
    • APAC (Australia, Singapore): near US parity for senior experts.
    • Eastern Europe: often 20–40% lower for equivalent skill.
    • LATAM: often 30–50% lower for equivalent skill.
    • India/SEA: often 40–70% lower for analyst to mid-level work.

    Typical project budget ranges (indicative)¶

    These ranges describe consulting-only costs, not including external audit/certification fees or tooling.

    SOC 2 readiness and audit support¶

    • Seed/early-stage SaaS (small scope): $15k–$50k.
    • Mid-market SaaS (multi-system): $40k–$120k.
    • Enterprise (multi-region, complex): $120k–$350k+.

    ISO 27001 implementation and certification support¶

    • Small scope (single business unit): $20k–$70k.
    • Mid-market scope: $50k–$150k.
    • Enterprise scope (multi-region): $150k–$400k+.

    GDPR compliance program build¶

    • Startup/small data footprint: $15k–$60k.
    • Mid-market (multiple products, vendors): $50k–$200k.
    • Enterprise (multi-entity, EU presence): $200k–$600k+.

    Audit and certification fee context (not consulting rates)¶

    External audit/certification is typically priced separately from consulting. - SOC 2 audit fees: often $15k–$60k+ depending on scope and auditor. - ISO 27001 certification fees: often $10k–$50k+ per cycle depending on audit days. - GDPR: no formal certification; legal counsel or external assessment may add fees.

    SOC 2 consulting rate details¶

    Typical work packages¶

    1. Scoping & system description
    2. Control design workshop
    3. Policy & procedure development
    4. Evidence collection plan and tooling
    5. Gap remediation (access controls, logging, change management)
    6. Pre-audit readiness assessment
    7. Audit support and evidence packaging

    SOC 2 cost drivers¶

    • Type II period length and evidence complexity.
    • Number of in-scope systems and vendors.
    • Security tooling maturity (SSO, SIEM, ticketing).
    • Volume of manual processes (greater manual evidence = higher hours).
    • Complexity of system description and subservice carve-outs.

    Example SOC 2 pricing scenarios¶

    • Scenario A: Early-stage SaaS, 10–20 employees, single product
    • Readiness assessment: $8k–$15k
    • Full readiness + Type I support: $20k–$45k

    • Type II preparation: $15k–$40k additional

    • Scenario B: Mid-market SaaS, 50–200 employees

    • Readiness + control implementation: $45k–$100k

    • Type II evidence and audit support: $30k–$80k

    • Scenario C: Enterprise SaaS with multiple products

    • Program build: $120k–$300k+
    • Type II support: $80k–$200k+

    ISO/IEC 27001 consulting rate details¶

    Typical work packages¶

    1. ISMS scope & context definition
    2. Risk assessment and risk treatment plan
    3. Statement of Applicability (SoA)
    4. Policy and control implementation
    5. Internal audit and management review prep
    6. Certification audit coordination

    ISO 27001 cost drivers¶

    • Number of sites and business units.
    • Scope complexity (cloud and on-prem hybrid).
    • Existing security management maturity.
    • Need for external internal auditor vs. in-house.
    • Evidence maturity and KPI reporting.

    Example ISO 27001 pricing scenarios¶

    • Scenario A: Single-site SaaS
    • ISMS build: $25k–$60k

    • Certification support: $10k–$30k

    • Scenario B: Mid-market, multi-team

    • ISMS build: $60k–$140k

    • Internal audit support: $15k–$40k

    • Scenario C: Enterprise, multi-region

    • ISMS build: $150k–$400k+
    • Audit cycle support: $50k–$150k+

    GDPR consulting rate details¶

    Typical work packages¶

    1. Data mapping and RoPA creation
    2. Lawful basis assessment and consent modeling
    3. DPIA framework and execution
    4. Privacy notices and transparency updates
    5. Vendor DPAs and SCCs
    6. Data subject rights handling procedures
    7. Incident response and breach notification
    8. DPO advisory or outsourced DPO

    GDPR cost drivers¶

    • Data processing complexity and volume.
    • Cross-border transfer needs and SCC updates.
    • Product changes, marketing tech stack, and analytics usage.
    • Sector-specific requirements (health, finance, edtech).
    • Frequency of DPIAs and supplier reviews.

    Example GDPR pricing scenarios¶

    • Scenario A: Small SaaS with EU customers
    • Data mapping + RoPA: $8k–$20k
    • Baseline GDPR program: $20k–$50k

    • DPO retainer: $2.5k–$7k/month

    • Scenario B: Mid-market with EU processing partners

    • Full GDPR program: $50k–$150k

    • DPIA support: $5k–$20k per DPIA

    • Scenario C: Enterprise with EU presence

    • Comprehensive GDPR program: $200k–$600k+
    • Ongoing privacy operations: $10k–$30k/month

    Timeline expectations¶

    SOC 2 Type I: 6–12 weeks for readiness if core controls exist. SOC 2 Type II: 3–12 months evidence period, 4–8 weeks audit prep. ISO 27001: 3–9 months for implementation, 1–3 months for audit cycle. GDPR: 2–6 months for baseline compliance, ongoing updates for new products.

    Accelerated timelines can be achieved with strong security tooling and executive sponsorship, but often increase consulting intensity and cost.

    Typical staffing model¶

    Roles commonly engaged in a full program build: - Engagement lead / partner: stakeholder management, scope control. - GRC program manager: day-to-day management, control ownership. - Security architect: technical controls and tooling integration. - Policy and process consultant: documentation and governance. - Privacy counsel or privacy lead: GDPR legal interpretation and DPIAs. - Analyst / evidence coordinator: evidence requests and tracking.

    Deliverables: SOC 2 checklist¶

    Core deliverables often include: - System description and boundary definition. - Control matrix aligned to TSC. - Policies (access control, change management, incident response, data retention). - Risk assessment and risk register. - Evidence schedule with control owners. - Incident response tabletop exercise and after-action report. - Vulnerability management and patching procedures. - Access reviews and user lifecycle processes. - Vendor risk management workflow. - Audit readiness packet (evidence index, diagrams, narratives).

    Deliverables: ISO 27001 checklist¶

    Core deliverables often include: - ISMS scope and context statement. - Risk assessment methodology and results. - Risk treatment plan and SoA. - Policy suite (information security, asset management, access control, cryptography). - Internal audit plan and report. - Management review meeting minutes and action items. - Corrective action log. - Certification audit support and evidence indexing.

    Deliverables: GDPR checklist¶

    Core deliverables often include: - Data inventory and RoPA. - Data flow diagrams and transfer assessment. - Lawful basis mapping and consent records. - DPIA templates and completed DPIAs. - Updated privacy notices and data subject request procedures. - DPA and SCC templates for vendors. - Incident response plan aligned to GDPR timelines. - Training materials and privacy awareness program.

    Evidence management and tooling¶

    Consultants often recommend tooling to reduce manual evidence collection: - GRC platforms for control mapping and evidence tracking. - Identity and access management (SSO/MFA) for access control evidence. - Ticketing systems (Jira, ServiceNow) to capture change approvals. - Endpoint management tools to demonstrate patching and encryption. - SIEM/logging solutions for monitoring and detection. - Cloud configuration tools for baseline security evidence.

    Tooling can reduce consulting hours but add subscription costs; weigh total cost of ownership against reduced manual effort.

    Vendor selection and RFP guidance¶

    Key vendor evaluation criteria¶

    • Framework expertise and certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001, privacy).
    • Industry experience (SaaS, fintech, healthcare, critical infrastructure).
    • Ability to provide a mapped control library across frameworks.
    • Proven audit support experience with recognized auditors or certification bodies.
    • Clear deliverables and evidence ownership definitions.
    • Availability for ongoing compliance or internal knowledge transfer.
    • References and sample project artifacts (redacted).

    RFP sections to include¶

    • Scope and objective description.
    • In-scope systems, services, and locations.
    • Desired framework(s) and report types.
    • Timeline and deadlines.
    • Required deliverables and acceptance criteria.
    • Preferred engagement model and staffing expectations.
    • Pricing format (hourly, fixed-fee, retainer).
    • NDA and data handling requirements.

    Contracting and negotiation tips¶

    • Request a clear scope definition and out-of-scope change process.
    • Align deliverables to internal owners to avoid “consultant-owned” compliance.
    • Ask for a control ownership matrix before contract signature.
    • Include knowledge transfer in scope, not as an add-on.
    • Negotiate audit support hours explicitly for Type II periods.
    • Ensure privacy counsel tasks and legal review are clearly separated if required.

    KPI and governance structure¶

    To keep compliance programs operating, define KPIs such as: - Evidence completion rate per month. - Access review completion within SLA. - Security incident MTTR and post-incident actions completed. - Vendor risk assessment completion rate. - DPIA completion rate for new products. - Policy review and training completion metrics.

    Common pitfalls and how to avoid them¶

    • Over-scoping: Start with the minimum viable scope to achieve objectives.
    • Under-documenting: Policies without evidence are insufficient.
    • Siloed ownership: Control owners must be internal, not just consultants.
    • Weak change management: Most audit findings trace back to informal changes.
    • Vendor sprawl: Untracked vendors create GDPR and SOC 2 exposure.
    • Missing executive sponsor: Programs fail without leadership support.

    Scenario-based reference models¶

    Scenario 1: SaaS startup targeting enterprise customers¶

    • Goal: SOC 2 Type I in 4 months, Type II in 12 months.
    • Approach: minimal scope, strong tooling, lean control set.
    • Consulting needs: 1 program lead, 1 technical advisor, evidence coordinator.
    • Budget: $25k–$70k consulting, $20k–$40k audit fees.

    Scenario 2: Mid-market SaaS moving to ISO 27001¶

    • Goal: certification within 9–12 months.
    • Approach: align existing SOC 2 controls to ISO Annex A.
    • Consulting needs: ISMS build, internal audit support, training.
    • Budget: $60k–$150k consulting, $15k–$45k certification fees.

    Scenario 3: EU data processing company needing GDPR program¶

    • Goal: GDPR compliance baseline in 4–6 months.
    • Approach: data mapping, DPIA framework, vendor DPAs, DSR procedures.
    • Consulting needs: privacy lead, legal counsel, process mapping support.
    • Budget: $50k–$180k consulting, ongoing DPO retainer.

    RFP template (lightweight)¶

    Section 1: Company background - Company size, revenue range, data types processed, in-scope systems.

    Section 2: Compliance objectives - Desired frameworks (SOC 2 Type I/II, ISO 27001, GDPR). - Target timeline and milestones.

    Section 3: Scope boundaries - Locations, products, infrastructure, and key vendors.

    Section 4: Deliverables - Specific deliverables required, acceptance criteria.

    Section 5: Staffing expectations - Required roles, experience levels, and onsite/remote split.

    Section 6: Pricing - Format (hourly/fixed/retainer), rate cards, assumptions.

    Section 7: References - Prior engagements, industry experience, audit outcomes.

    Suggested sources to validate pricing and scope¶

    While this draft does not provide live citations, the following sources are commonly used to validate market rates and requirements: - AICPA SOC 2 guidance and Trust Services Criteria documentation. - ISO/IEC 27001:2022 standard and ISO 27002 guidance. - European Data Protection Board (EDPB) guidelines. - National data protection authorities (CNIL, ICO, etc.). - Industry analyst reports (e.g., cybersecurity advisory market surveys). - Vendor risk management questionnaires from customers. - Job postings and RFPs for compliance roles. - Legal advisory updates for GDPR enforcement trends.

    Glossary¶

    • SOC 2: Service Organization Controls report focused on security and other criteria.
    • ISMS: Information Security Management System (ISO 27001).
    • SoA: Statement of Applicability for ISO 27001 controls.
    • DPIA: Data Protection Impact Assessment.
    • RoPA: Record of Processing Activities.
    • DPO: Data Protection Officer.
    • TSC: Trust Services Criteria.

    Appendix A: SOC 2 readiness checklist (condensed)¶

    1. Document system boundaries and services.
    2. Identify in-scope infrastructure and vendors.
    3. Establish security governance and risk ownership.
    4. Document asset inventory and classification.
    5. Formalize access request, approval, and deprovisioning.
    6. Enforce MFA and privileged access controls.
    7. Implement periodic access reviews.
    8. Document change management workflow.
    9. Ensure code reviews and deployment approvals.
    10. Implement logging and monitoring.
    11. Define incident response playbook and escalation paths.
    12. Conduct incident response tabletop exercise.
    13. Establish vulnerability management process.
    14. Define patching cadence and evidence collection.
    15. Maintain backup and recovery procedures.
    16. Document business continuity plans.
    17. Enforce secure configuration baselines.
    18. Maintain encryption policies for data at rest and in transit.
    19. Establish vendor risk assessment workflow.
    20. Track vendor contracts and security addenda.
    21. Implement security awareness training.
    22. Document HR onboarding and termination processes.
    23. Establish data retention and disposal policies.
    24. Create a central evidence repository.
    25. Assign control owners for each control.
    26. Schedule evidence collection cadence.
    27. Review and approve policies annually.
    28. Prepare management sign-off on risk exceptions.
    29. Document system diagrams and data flow.
    30. Create a SOC 2 readiness report and remediation plan.

    Appendix B: ISO/IEC 27001 control families (illustrative)¶

    ISO 27001:2022 includes controls grouped into organizational, people, physical, and technological themes. Common implementation topics include:

    Organizational controls - Information security policy and governance. - Roles and responsibilities. - Segregation of duties and conflict management. - Supplier relationships and contract controls. - Incident management and lessons learned. - Business continuity planning.

    People controls - Background checks and onboarding. - Security awareness training. - Acceptable use and confidentiality agreements. - Offboarding and access revocation.

    Physical controls - Physical access control and visitor management. - Secure areas and equipment protection. - Environmental safeguards and power redundancy.

    Technological controls - Identity and access management. - Secure authentication and MFA. - Network security and segmentation. - Encryption and key management. - Logging, monitoring, and detection. - Secure development lifecycle (SDLC). - Vulnerability management and patching.

    Appendix C: GDPR compliance checklist (condensed)¶

    1. Identify all personal data processing activities.
    2. Build a RoPA with processing purposes and lawful bases.
    3. Document data flows and storage locations.
    4. Verify data minimization and retention policies.
    5. Update privacy notices for transparency.
    6. Confirm consent management where required.
    7. Establish data subject request (DSR) workflow.
    8. Ensure contracts with processors include GDPR clauses.
    9. Implement SCCs for cross-border transfers where needed.
    10. Complete DPIAs for high-risk processing.
    11. Assign DPO or responsible privacy lead.
    12. Train staff on privacy obligations.
    13. Define breach notification workflows and templates.
    14. Maintain records of consent and DSR outcomes.
    15. Review security measures and access controls.
    16. Ensure deletion and anonymization procedures.
    17. Validate marketing and tracking technologies.
    18. Track vendor risk and subprocessor changes.
    19. Maintain compliance monitoring and audits.
    20. Review and update GDPR documentation annually.

    Appendix D: Evidence library starter list¶

    Governance and risk - Risk register and risk treatment plan. - Security committee meeting minutes. - Management review notes.

    Access control - IAM configurations, MFA enforcement screenshots. - Access review reports and sign-offs. - Joiner/mover/leaver evidence.

    Change management - Change tickets and approvals. - Code review records. - Deployment logs.

    Incident response - IR plan and contact list. - Incident tickets and post-mortems. - Tabletop exercise reports.

    Asset management - Asset inventory list. - Encryption status reports.

    Vendor management - Vendor list and risk ratings. - Due diligence questionnaires. - DPAs and SCCs.

    Appendix I: Additional rate estimation heuristics¶

    • Use role-based rate cards: partner, manager, senior, analyst.
    • Estimate hours by phase: discovery, design, implementation, evidence.
    • Adjust for tooling maturity: stronger tooling reduces manual hours.
    • Include a buffer: 10–20% for remediation and unplanned evidence.
    • Separate audit fees: treat as an external line item.

    Appendix J: Sample 6-month integrated project plan (outline)¶

    1. Month 1: scope definition, data mapping, risk assessment.
    2. Month 2: control library, policy drafting, initial remediation.
    3. Month 3: evidence plan, tooling integration, vendor review.
    4. Month 4: SOC 2 readiness, ISO SoA finalization.
    5. Month 5: GDPR DPIAs, internal audit prep.
    6. Month 6: audit support, evidence finalization, management review.

    Appendix K: Quick readiness self-assessment questions¶

    1. Do we have a documented risk assessment and owners?
    2. Are access reviews performed and recorded on schedule?
    3. Is there a defined incident response plan and evidence of testing?
    4. Are vendor contracts tracked with security and privacy clauses?
    5. Can we produce evidence of change approvals and testing?
    6. Do we track data flows and have a RoPA?
    7. Is there a clear policy suite with annual review?
    8. Do we have a process to handle data subject requests?
    9. Are backups tested and recovery objectives defined?
    10. Are key controls monitored for exceptions?
