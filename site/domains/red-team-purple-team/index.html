    2. Definitions and Positioning¶

    • Red team: objective-based attack simulation designed to test people, process, and technology.
    • Adversary simulation: campaign-style engagement that mimics a specific threat actor or TTP set.
    • Adversary emulation: simulation of attacker tactics for testing defenses (glossary definition).
    • Purple team: collaborative red + blue engagement focused on detection improvement and training.
    • BAS (Breach and Attack Simulation): automated testing platform; consultants tune and validate.
    • PTaaS: platform-led penetration testing with continuous or on-demand access.
    • Objective-based scope: success criteria defined as outcomes, not simply vulnerability discovery.
    • Outcome-based pricing: engagements priced on objectives and risk reduction, not hours alone.
    • Red team vs pentest: red team emphasizes stealth, persistence, and end-to-end chains.
    • Purple team vs red team: purple team prioritizes transparency and iterative detection tuning.
    • Threat-informed approach: scenario built from intelligence or sector-specific threat data.
    • TTP: tactics, techniques, and procedures mapped to MITRE ATT&CK.
    • APT: advanced persistent threat, often state-aligned and highly targeted.
    • OSINT: open-source intelligence used to profile and target environments.
    • C2: command and control infrastructure enabling simulated adversary operations.
    • Kill chain narrative: timeline describing how the red team advanced toward objectives.
    • Detection engineering: creating and tuning analytics to detect adversary behaviors.
    • Retest cycle: validation of fixes or detection improvements after remediation.
    • Campaign model: multi-week engagement with recon, access, lateral movement, and reporting.
    • Retainer model: monthly subscription for a set number of objectives or attacks.
    • Workshop model: collaborative, short-form TTP execution for training and detection updates.
    • Wireless red team: scenario-driven RF attack simulation with stealth and objectives.
    • LLM red teaming: testing AI systems for prompt injection, jailbreaks, and data leakage.
    • OT red teaming: adversary simulation in industrial environments with high safety constraints.

    3. Market Overview and Demand Drivers¶

    • Attack surface expansion in cloud and API environments increases adversary simulation demand.
    • Buyers expect scoped, repeatable engagements with retest cycles.
    • PTaaS adoption accelerates continuous assurance expectations.
    • Regulatory pressure (PCI, SOC 2, FedRAMP, sector audits) drives repeat testing cycles.
    • Ransomware initial access techniques heighten demand for social engineering testing.
    • AI and deepfakes create new social engineering vectors and testing requirements.
    • Remote and hybrid work models increase endpoint and identity attack vectors.
    • Insurance carriers increasingly require proactive testing for coverage eligibility.
    • M&A due diligence favors limited technical testing; red/purple team exercises are rare without approval.
    • BFSI budgets typically fall between $30K-$100K+ for testing engagements.
    • Healthcare budgets typically fall between $20K-$50K for testing engagements.
    • Technology/SaaS budgets typically fall between $15K-$40K for testing engagements.
    • Government budgets range from $50K-$500K depending on compliance requirements.
    • Retail and e-commerce budgets typically fall between $15K-$35K.
    • Clients expect remediation guidance, not just vulnerability lists.
    • Outcome-based simulations are perceived as higher value than automated scans.
    • Threat intelligence alignment raises perceived realism and increases budget acceptance.
    • Purple team engagements are growing fastest due to internal training value.
    • Market gap persists for cloud-native adversary simulation expertise.
    • OT/SCADA adversary simulation remains underserved and commands premium rates.
    • LLM red teaming demand is accelerating for AI-enabled organizations.
    • Wireless security teams increasingly request red team-style RF simulations.

    4. Service Taxonomy¶

    4.1 Core Red Team Services¶

    • Objective-based internal or external campaign execution.
    • Stealth operations with low-noise initial access and persistence.
    • Multi-vector access methods (phishing, password reuse, supply chain, physical).
    • Active Directory compromise and privilege escalation chains.
    • Lateral movement and credential access testing.
    • Objective execution (data exfiltration, crown jewels access, domain admin).
    • Post-exploitation staging and evidence capture.
    • Executive and technical reporting with kill chain narrative.

    4.2 Adversary Simulation Packages¶

    • Campaign model with explicit threat actor emulation.
    • Industry-aligned scenarios (APT29, ransomware affiliates, insider threat).
    • TTP mapping to MITRE ATT&CK and custom playbooks.
    • Long dwell time simulations (weeks) to test detection drift.
    • Objectives tied to business impact (e.g., financial transfer, IP access).
    • Campaign debrief with red/blue leadership and C-suite stakeholders.

    4.3 Purple Team Services¶

    • Collaborative execution of mapped TTPs with defenders.
    • Live detection tuning and validation during execution.
    • Training and enablement for SOC and detection teams.
    • Detection engineering workshops (3-5 days typical).
    • Per-user or per-endpoint pricing options for scale.
    • Government and defense time-and-materials models.
    • Purple team exercises sold by MSSPs as premium add-ons.

    4.4 Social Engineering and Physical Testing¶

    • Phishing, vishing, and smishing campaigns.
    • Physical access testing (tailgating, badge cloning, media drops).
    • Full-spectrum social engineering as part of red team operations.
    • Adversary emulation packages priced $45,000-$100,000+.
    • AI-enhanced vishing testing for deepfake resilience.

    4.5 LLM and AI Red Teaming¶

    • Prompt injection and jailbreak testing for LLM guardrail bypasses.
    • Output validation for PII or sensitive data leakage.
    • Adversarial ML testing for data poisoning or model inversion.
    • AI governance and AI RMF alignment as part of security program.
    • Specialized LLM red teaming rates at $500-$1,000+ per hour.

    4.6 Wireless Red Teaming¶

    • RF attack simulation with stealth and defined objectives.
    • Credential capture, rogue AP, and segmentation bypass scenarios.
    • WLAN attack paths that pivot into internal networks.
    • Wireless red team specialists at $350-$550 per hour.
    • Day rates for wireless adversary emulation at $2,800-$4,400.

    4.7 BAS Management Services¶

    • Managed tuning of BAS platforms (Cortex XSOAR, Cymulate, etc.).
    • Scenario library development and quarterly testing cycles.
    • Monthly management fees at $7,000-$15,000.
    • Supports purple team objectives by automating baseline testing.

    4.8 Cloud-Native Adversary Simulation¶

    • Focused on AWS, Azure, or GCP attack paths.
    • Emphasizes identity, IAM misconfigurations, and cloud lateral movement.
    • Differentiates from legacy AD-centric red teams.
    • Command premium rates as a niche specialization.

    4.9 OT/SCADA Red Teaming¶

    • Emphasizes safety, availability, and strict ROE constraints.
    • Integrates OT threat intelligence and adversary mapping.
    • Requires deep protocol knowledge and industrial environment experience.
    • High barrier to entry supports premium pricing.

    5. Engagement Models¶

    5.1 Campaign Model (Project-Based)¶

    • Duration: 4-12 weeks, aligned to threat actor campaign.
    • Price range: $60,000-$150,000+ per campaign.
    • Recon/OSINT phase: 2-3 weeks.
    • Initial access and persistence: 2-4 weeks.
    • Lateral movement and objectives: 2-4 weeks.
    • Reporting and debrief: 1-2 weeks.

    5.2 Retainer Model (Continuous Red Team)¶

    • Monthly subscription for objectives per quarter.
    • Price range: $15,000-$40,000 per month.
    • Supports continuous assurance and quarterly executive updates.

    5.3 BAS Management Model¶

    • Monthly tuning and scenario management.
    • Price range: $7,000-$15,000 per month (software excluded).
    • Often paired with quarterly purple team workshops.

    5.4 Workshop Model (Purple Team)¶

    • 3-5 day live execution workshops.
    • Price range: $15,000-$25,000 per workshop.
    • High margin once playbooks are created.

    5.5 Per-User or Per-Endpoint Model¶

    • Base fee (e.g., $10,000) plus per-user or per-endpoint fee.
    • Example: $10,000 base + $75 per user for first 250 users.

    5.6 Government and Defense T&M Model¶

    • Time-and-materials pricing with day rates.
    • Daily rates: 1,750-2,500 ($2,200-$3,150) per consultant.

    5.7 Platform-Enabled Model¶

    • PTaaS or mission-based work through vetted platforms.
    • Synack Red Team missions typically $85-$150 per hour.
    • Cobalt-style PTaaS rates typically $100-$200 per hour.

    7. Red Team Methodology (Campaign Model)¶

    7.1 Pre-Engagement Planning¶

    • Define business objectives and crown jewels.
    • Align threat actor profile to industry and risk profile.
    • Establish rules of engagement and kill switches.
    • Document legal authorizations and safe bounds.
    • Align on communication cadence and escalation contacts.
    • Confirm data handling and retention requirements.
    • Identify excluded systems and safety constraints.

    7.2 Reconnaissance and Targeting¶

    • Conduct OSINT to identify exposed assets and personnel.
    • Map external attack surface and third-party dependencies.
    • Develop phishing personas or pretext narratives.
    • Profile identity providers and MFA configurations.
    • Identify candidate initial access vectors by risk/impact.

    7.3 Infrastructure Preparation¶

    • Build staging infrastructure with safe logging controls.
    • Configure C2 and operational logging for evidence capture.
    • Prepare payloads and test for stability and safety.
    • Establish secure operator communication channels.

    7.4 Initial Access¶

    • Execute social engineering, credential reuse, or web exploitation.
    • Validate access without destabilizing production systems.
    • Capture proof-of-access artifacts for reporting.

    7.5 Persistence and Privilege Escalation¶

    • Establish controlled persistence that respects ROE.
    • Escalate privileges to test segmentation and identity controls.
    • Document each escalation path and required mitigations.

    7.6 Lateral Movement¶

    • Pivot across network segments and cloud tenants.
    • Use credential access and token manipulation as needed.
    • Emphasize stealth to test detection thresholds.

    7.7 Objective Execution¶

    • Access target data repositories or systems.
    • Validate path to crown jewels without data destruction.
    • Demonstrate data access or transfer in controlled manner.

    7.8 Command and Control and OPSEC¶

    • Maintain operational security to emulate real adversaries.
    • Monitor for detection and simulate anti-forensics where approved.
    • Avoid sensitive data exfiltration unless explicitly authorized.

    7.9 Evidence and Reporting¶

    • Capture artifacts (screenshots, logs, timestamps) at each stage.
    • Map techniques to MITRE ATT&CK for detection mapping.
    • Provide kill chain narrative and business impact analysis.

    7.10 Debrief and Remediation¶

    • Conduct executive debrief with impact summary.
    • Provide technical report with reproducible steps and fixes.
    • Align remediation with prioritized risk register.

    8. Purple Team Methodology (Collaborative Model)¶

    8.1 Planning and Alignment¶

    • Establish shared objectives with SOC and detection engineers.
    • Identify key TTPs to validate against existing detections.
    • Map TTPs to MITRE ATT&CK for coverage analysis.
    • Confirm logging sources and telemetry visibility.

    8.2 Execution and Validation¶

    • Execute selected TTPs in a controlled, transparent manner.
    • Observe detection and response workflows in real time.
    • Capture telemetry and alert outputs for tuning.

    8.3 Detection Engineering Loop¶

    • Tune existing detection rules based on observed gaps.
    • Develop new detections for uncovered techniques.
    • Validate alert quality (signal-to-noise, false positives).

    8.4 Training and Enablement¶

    • Provide hands-on coaching to analysts and engineers.
    • Document playbooks and response steps per scenario.
    • Align with SOC operating procedures and escalation paths.

    8.5 Retest and Closure¶

    • Retest key TTPs after detection updates.
    • Measure improvement in MTTD and response quality.
    • Deliver final report with improvements and next steps.

    11. Go-To-Market Strategy and Sales Messaging¶

    11.1 Positioning and Differentiation¶

    • Position red teaming as business stress testing, not vulnerability finding.
    • Emphasize outcome-based objectives aligned to business risk.
    • Highlight detection engineering improvements from purple team work.

    11.2 Packaging and Pricing Tactics¶

    • Offer campaign packages with fixed deliverables.
    • Provide retainer tiers for continuous assurance.
    • Bundle social engineering and wireless components as premium add-ons.

    11.3 Discovery and Scoping Questions¶

    • Which business processes are most critical to protect?
    • What threat actors are most relevant to your industry?
    • Which detection gaps are most concerning to leadership?
    • What telemetry sources are currently in place?

    11.4 ROI Messaging¶

    • Measure reduction in detection gaps and improved response speed.
    • Align outcomes with compliance or insurance requirements.
    • Quantify potential loss avoided by reducing dwell time.

    11.5 Retainer and Expansion Strategy¶

    • Start with a pilot campaign and expand to quarterly cycles.
    • Use purple team workshops to lock in recurring engagements.
    • Offer BAS management for continuous baseline coverage.

    • Exploit developer or tooling specialist.
    • Social engineering specialist (phishing, vishing, physical).
    • Threat intelligence analyst for actor selection.
    • Purple team detection engineer or threat hunter.

    12.2 Technical Skills¶

    • Active Directory exploitation and privilege escalation.
    • Cloud IAM abuse and lateral movement.
    • Web and API exploitation for initial access.

    • Scripting and automation for tooling development.

    • Clear articulation of risk and business impact.
    • Collaboration with blue team and SOC leadership.
    • Ethical decision-making and safety awareness.

    13. Risk Management, Legal, and Safe-Bound Controls¶

    13.1 Rules of Engagement¶

    • Document in-scope assets and explicit exclusions.
    • Define acceptable hours and safety limitations.
    • Establish escalation paths for unexpected impact.
    • Require written authorization for social engineering.

    13.2 Safe-Bound Controls¶

    • Use non-destructive payloads and controlled proof of access.
    • Avoid data exfiltration unless explicitly authorized.
    • Maintain logs and evidence for accountability.

    13.3 M&A Diligence Considerations¶

    • Red/purple team exercises are rare during diligence.
    • Use scoped testing and safe review windows.
    • Post-close engagements recommended for deeper simulations.

    13.4 Legal and Insurance¶

    • Ensure contract language covers liability and indemnification.
    • Carry professional liability insurance for red team operations.
    • Validate data retention and deletion policies.

    15. Metrics, KPIs, and Value Measurement¶

    15.1 Operational Metrics¶

    • Mean time to detect (MTTD) before and after engagement.
    • Mean time to respond (MTTR) for simulated incidents.

    15.2 Coverage Metrics¶

    • MITRE ATT&CK coverage percentage by tactic.
    • Detection gaps per TTP class.
    • Remediation completion rate after retest.

    15.3 Outcome Metrics¶

    • Objective completion rate for red team campaign.
    • Dwell time achieved before detection.
    • Improvement in SOC workflow efficiency.

    18. Glossary and Quick Reference¶

    18.1 Key Terms¶

    • Adversary emulation: simulation of attacker tactics for testing defenses.
    • Red team: objective-based adversary simulation.
    • Purple team: collaborative red and blue engagement.
    • TTP: tactics, techniques, and procedures (MITRE ATT&CK aligned).
    • APT: advanced persistent threat, state-linked or highly capable actor.
    • IOC: indicator of compromise for detection validation.
    • OSINT: open source intelligence.

    18.2 Quick Reference Rate Card¶

    • Red team operator: $80-$130 per hour.
    • Senior red team lead: $150-$250 per hour.
    • Expert/niche red team: $300-$500+ per hour.
    • Red/purple team enterprise rates: $250-$600 per hour.
    • Purple team day rate: $2,500-$3,150.
    • Wireless red team: $350-$550 per hour.
    • LLM red teaming: $500-$1,000+ per hour.
    • Adversary simulation campaigns: $60,000-$150,000+.

    18.3 Quick Reference Engagement Durations¶

    • Red team campaigns: 2-4 weeks (typical) or 4-12 weeks (campaign model).
    • Purple team workshops: 3-5 days.
    • Social engineering campaigns: 1-4 weeks depending on scope.
    • Continuous PTaaS: ongoing monthly allocation.
