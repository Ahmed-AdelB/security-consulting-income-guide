    Full-Time CISO vs vCISO:

    • Full-Time Cost: $200,000 - $500,000/year (total comp + overhead)
    • vCISO Cost: $36,000 - $60,000/year (typical)
    • Savings: 60-75% reduction in security leadership costs
    • Alternative Calculation: 70-80% savings (30-70% of full-time costs)

    ROI Factors:

    • No recruiting costs ($10,000 - $50,000 saved)
    • No benefits overhead (30-40% of salary saved)
    • No training/development costs
    • Immediate expertise vs hiring timeline (3-6 months)
    • Scalable engagement (adjust hours as needed)

    1. Scope of Services: Advisory only vs full program management
    2. Industry Experience: Regulated industries (finance, healthcare) command premium
    3. Organization Size: Enterprise pricing 2-3x higher than SMB
    4. Geographic Location: Major metro areas 20-30% premium
    5. Contract Duration: Long-term agreements receive 10-20% discount
    6. Certifications: CISSP, CISM, CISO certification add 15-25% premium

    Tax Comparison¶

    W-2 Employee:

    • Employee FICA: 7.65% (Social Security 6.2% + Medicare 1.45%)
    • Employer FICA: 7.65% (paid by company)
    • Income Tax: Based on tax bracket
    • Benefits: Provided by employer (health, 401k, PTO)

    1099 Contractor:

    • Self-Employment Tax: 15.3% (full FICA, no employer portion)
    • Income Tax: Based on tax bracket
    • QBI Deduction: Up to 20% deduction on qualified business income
    • Business Expenses: Deductible (home office, equipment, software, insurance)
    • Benefits: Self-funded (no employer contribution)

    • Client Request: Lower rate for 30-day trial
    • Strategy: Maintain rate, offer flexible terms
    • Counter: "I maintain my standard rate of $150/hr, but I'm happy to structure this as a 30-day trial with a 2-week termination clause for either party."
