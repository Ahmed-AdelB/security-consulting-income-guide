    1. Executive Summary¶

    • Cybersecurity due diligence is typically compressed into 2–6 weeks, making scope clarity and access the main success drivers.
    • M&A diligence pricing is driven by deal size, regulatory exposure, testing depth, and speed-to-close, not by a single rate.
    • Indicative diligence rates span $150–$350/hr (independent) up to $350–$900+/hr (global firms) with blended-team discounts.
    • Typical diligence budgets range from $25k–$150k for standard deals to $600k–$1.5M+ for global, regulated targets.
    • VC-backed startups prefer fractional security leadership with $150–$500/hr or $2k–$25k/month retainers.
    • Advisory equity is commonly staged: 0.25%–0.75% (Seed), 0.10%–0.30% (Series A), 0.05%–0.20% (Series B+).
    • Board and advisory panels pay $5k–$60k/year retainers, $1k–$5k meeting fees, and $200–$900/hr for senior advisors.
    • Expert networks used in VC/PE diligence typically deliver $250–$500/hr calls, with elite niches reaching $1,000+/hr.
    • Growth drivers include regulatory scrutiny, insurance underwriting, cloud/SaaS sprawl, and investor expectations.
    • Market opportunities cluster around pre-LOI screening, confirmatory diligence, portfolio security uplift, and board oversight.
    • Advisory firms win by productizing Tier 0–2 diligence, SOC 2 readiness, and security roadmaps for portfolio companies.
    • Referral-driven channels dominate: 60% conversion via direct referrals and 40% via partner handoffs.
    • Cold outbound is low yield (<2% conversion) for high-value advisory engagements.

    3. VC/PE Market Context & Demand Drivers¶

    3.1 Why VC/PE Cyber Advisory Demand Is Rising¶

    • Regulatory exposure increases scrutiny for fintech, healthcare, and critical infrastructure targets.
    • Cyber insurance pressure forces diligence evidence and risk memo requirements.
    • Cloud and SaaS sprawl expands attack surface and third-party dependency risk.
    • Incident visibility directly influences valuation, escrow, and indemnity negotiations.
    • Investor expectations demand quantified cyber risk input in investment committee memos.

    3.2 Startup-Specific Security Drivers¶

    • Enterprise sales pressure forces SOC 2 / ISO 27001 readiness earlier (Seed–Series A).
    • Board/investor scrutiny ties security posture to fundraising and valuation.
    • Regulatory expansion (privacy laws, SEC disclosure) increases early-stage obligations.
    • CISO burnout and short tenure increases reliance on fractional leadership.
    • Cost sensitivity limits full-time CISO hiring (favoring advisory retainers).

    3.3 Portfolio-Level Drivers (VC/PE)¶

    • Concentration risk: a single breach can impact fund performance and exit timing.
    • Operational leverage: repeatable security playbooks reduce portfolio risk at scale.
    • Exit readiness: buyers expect documented security programs and incident history.
    • Customer diligence: security questionnaires appear earlier in revenue cycles.
    • Board duty of care: cyber oversight is now a governance expectation.

    3.4 Diligence Timing Realities¶

    • Pre-LOI screening is brief and externally focused.
    • Confirmatory diligence compresses 2–6 weeks into parallel evidence review and testing.
    • Post-close integration is where remediation budgets and 100-day plans are executed.

    4. Engagement Models Across the Deal Lifecycle¶

    4.1 Pre-LOI / Target Screening (Tier 0)¶

    • External attack-surface review and OSINT risk flags.
    • Public breach history scan and regulatory headlines.
    • High-level product and data sensitivity assessment.
    • Initial risk memo for investment committee screening.

    4.2 LOI → Confirmatory Diligence (Tier 1–2)¶

    • Data room review and evidence requests by workstream.
    • Management interviews and governance validation.
    • Targeted technical testing (cloud posture, vulnerability scans).
    • Draft risk register with materiality assessment.

    4.3 Deep Diligence (Tier 2–3)¶

    • Penetration testing add-ons and targeted code reviews.
    • Architecture walkthroughs and identity access reviews.
    • Incident history deep dive and response maturity validation.
    • Compliance evidence validation (SOC 2, ISO, PCI, HIPAA).

    4.4 Post-Close Integration Advisory¶

    • 30/60/90-day integration roadmap.
    • Identity, endpoint, and logging consolidation planning.
    • Security governance and reporting alignment for the combined entity.
    • Remediation budget sequencing and KPI tracking.

    4.5 Sell-Side Readiness Model¶

    • Gap assessment before the data room opens.
    • Evidence package creation with expected buyer requests.
    • Management Q&A playbook and remediation timeline.
    • Shorter, lower-cost scope than buy-side diligence.

    4.6 Portfolio Security Advisory Model (VC/PE)¶

    • Quarterly portfolio risk reviews and KPI dashboards.
    • Standardized security roadmap templates across portfolio.
    • Shared vendor negotiations (EDR, IAM, SOC tools).
    • Incident response readiness exercises across multiple companies.

    4.7 Expert Network Call Model¶

    • 60–90 minute domain expert calls to validate market claims.
    • Typical VC/PE use cases: product security, compliance roadmap, vendor comparisons.
    • Rate bands based on expert seniority and specialization.

    4.8 Board & Advisory Panel Engagements¶

    • Formal board seats with fiduciary responsibilities.
    • Advisory panels with non-fiduciary guidance.
    • Committee roles (risk/tech/cyber) with premium compensation.

    5. Core Deliverables by Deal Stage¶

    5.1 Pre-LOI Deliverables¶

    • External attack surface snapshot.
    • Public breach history summary.
    • Cyber risk heatmap for screening.
    • Go / No-go decision support memo.

    5.2 Confirmatory Diligence Deliverables¶

    • Executive summary and red-flag memo.
    • Prioritized risk register (severity, likelihood, impact).
    • Remediation cost estimates with rough timelines.
    • Closing-condition or indemnity recommendations.
    • Evidence inventory and data room gaps log.

    5.3 Deep Diligence Deliverables¶

    • Technical validation findings (scans, code review, cloud posture).
    • Incident response maturity assessment.
    • Compliance evidence review summary.
    • Expanded integration plan with tooling alignment.

    5.4 Post-Close Deliverables¶

    • 100-day security integration plan.
    • Governance model for combined entity.
    • KPI dashboard and reporting cadence.
    • Remediation project roadmap with owners.

    5.5 Sell-Side Deliverables¶

    • Readiness assessment and gap remediation plan.
    • Evidence package optimized for buyer diligence.
    • Management Q&A responses and escalation guide.

    6. Startup Advisory & Fractional CISO Models¶

    6.1 Advisory Role Archetypes¶

    • Lightweight advisor (1–2 hrs/month): Strategy calls, investor Q&A, security posture feedback.
    • Active advisor (2–6 hrs/month): Roadmap ownership, compliance prep, customer diligence support.
    • Fractional CISO (10–30 hrs/month): Security leadership, board reporting, incident readiness.
    • Board/committee member: Governance oversight with fiduciary responsibilities.

    6.2 Startup Advisory Packages by Stage¶

    Pre-Seed

    • Security fundamentals checklist.
    • Minimal viable policies (access, incident response, vendor risk).
    • Investor-ready risk statement.

    Seed

    • Security roadmap and SOC 2 readiness plan.
    • Lightweight risk assessment and top-10 risks.
    • Advisor-led customer security questionnaire support.

    Series A

    • Compliance preparation and audit readiness.
    • Incident response playbooks and tabletop exercises.
    • KPI reporting cadence to CEO/board.

    Series B–C

    • Security program scaling and staffing plan.
    • SDLC security integration and vulnerability management KPIs.
    • Board risk reporting and vendor risk upgrades.

    Series D+ / Pre-IPO

    • Mature governance, disclosure readiness, and IPO security narrative.
    • Privacy and regulatory readiness (SEC, GDPR, HIPAA as needed).
    • Third-party assurance strategy and audit schedule.

    6.3 Startup Pricing Models (Local Benchmarks)¶

    • Hourly: $150–$500/hr for short advisory or incident response.
    • Monthly retainer: $2k–$25k/month depending on hours and scope.
    • Project-based: $5k–$100k+ for SOC 2 readiness, gap assessments, audits.
    • Annual package: $30k–$240k for full program ownership.

    6.4 Sample Seed-Stage Package (From Local Benchmarks)¶

    • Discovery + risk assessment: $7.5k–$15k one-time.
    • Monthly retainer: $3k–$7.5k for 8–15 hrs/month.
    • Equity add-on: 0.15%–0.50% to offset cash gap.

    6.5 30/60/90 Day Startup CISO Playbook¶

    First 30 days

    • Establish asset inventory and data classification.
    • Deliver top-10 risk register with owners.
    • Document current controls and posture.

    Days 31–60

    • Define roadmap and prioritized initiatives.
    • Launch baseline policies (access control, IR, vendor risk).
    • Prepare SOC 2/ISO readiness plan if required by enterprise sales.

    Days 61–90

    • Establish KPI cadence to leadership and board.
    • Execute core controls (MFA, logging, secure SDLC).
    • Align security budget with product roadmap and revenue goals.

    8. Budget Models & Diligence Scenarios¶

    8.1 Typical Diligence Budget Ranges (By Deal Size)¶

    • Under $50M enterprise value: $15k–$60k (Tier 0–1) or $60k–$150k (Tier 2).
    • $50M–$250M: $40k–$150k (Tier 1) or $150k–$350k (Tier 2).
    • $250M–$1B: $150k–$400k (Tier 1–2) or $400k–$900k (Tier 3).
    • $1B+: $400k–$1.5M+ depending on footprint and regulation.

    8.2 Budget Ranges by Diligence Depth¶

    • Rapid screen (Tier 0): $5k–$30k.
    • Standard diligence (Tier 1): $25k–$150k.
    • Deep diligence (Tier 2): $100k–$500k.
    • Full-scope diligence (Tier 3): $250k–$1M+.

    8.3 Industry Complexity Adjustments¶

    • SaaS / B2B software: baseline ranges.
    • Fintech / payments / banking: +20–60% for regulatory review.
    • Healthcare / life sciences: +20–70% for PHI and compliance checks.
    • Critical infrastructure / OT: +30–100% for site visits and OT controls.

    8.4 Budget Estimation Quick Calculator¶

    1. Select base tier (Tier 0, 1, 2, or 3).
    2. Add complexity modifiers (multi-cloud, regulated industry, global footprint).
    3. Add testing components (pen test, code review).
    4. Apply blended rate (lead + analyst mix).
    5. Add contingency (10–20%).

    Formula:

    Estimated hours = Base tier hours * (1 + complexity modifiers) + testing hours
Project cost = Estimated hours * blended rate + fixed testing add-ons

    9.3 Equity vs Cash Trade-Off Logic¶

    • Anchor on market rate ($200–$300/hr typical for fractional CISO).
    • Quantify cash gap and offset with equity value.
    • Use realistic exit probabilities (equity is long-tail upside).
    • Tie equity to explicit deliverables and time commitments.

    9.4 Vesting & Term Structure Norms¶

    • Vesting: 2–4 years, monthly or quarterly.
    • Cliff: 3–12 months (advisors often shorter than employees).
    • Acceleration: negotiable on change-of-control.
    • Scope lock: equity tied to defined deliverables.

    9.5 When to Prefer Cash Over Equity¶

    • High time commitment (20+ hrs/month) with executive accountability.
    • Regulatory exposure or breach liability.
    • Short runway (<9 months) with uncertain fundraising.
    • High operational workload (incident response, audits, customer escalations).

    • Critical infrastructure / OT (site visits, segmentation reviews).
    • SaaS platforms with enterprise sales cycles.

    Board advisory VC/PE Portfolio Oversight, governance reviews
    Expert network calls VC/PE Any Market validation, benchmarks

    11. Go-To-Market & Deal Sourcing Channels¶

    11.1 Referral Conversion Benchmarks¶

    • Direct referrals: ~60% conversion rate.
    • Partner handoffs (MSP / law firm): ~40% conversion rate.
    • Conference inbound: ~15–25% conversion rate.
    • Cold outbound: <2% conversion rate for high-value engagements.

    11.2 Partner Channel Structures (From Networking Guide)¶

    • Referral only: 10–15% of Year 1 retainer to MSP partners.
    • White-label: wholesale rate to MSP, MSP retails at markup.
    • Law firm partnerships: reciprocal referrals (cash rarely exchanged).

    11.3 Law Firm Due Diligence Pitch (Template)¶

    "When your clients buy a company, who checks the code/network for ticking time bombs? I offer a fixed-fee technical due diligence pack for M&A."

    11.4 Expert Network Channel Strategy¶

    • Maintain profiles on premium networks (GLG, AlphaSights, Guidepoint).
    • Set initial rate competitively, then raise after positive feedback.
    • Respond quickly to invitations (24–48 hour window).
    • Avoid disclosing MNPI; maintain compliance training records.

    11.5 VC/PE Partner Target List¶

    • Corporate development teams seeking bolt-on acquisitions.
    • PE operating partners with portfolio oversight mandates.
    • VC platform teams supporting portfolio security readiness.
    • Law firms serving M&A and securities compliance.

    12. Scoping Checklists & Evidence Requests¶

    12.1 Buyer-Side Scoping Questions¶

    • What is the deal type (asset, stock, carve-out) and expected close date?
    • What is the target’s revenue, headcount, and data sensitivity profile?
    • Which systems or products drive valuation?
    • Which regulatory regimes apply (PCI, HIPAA, GDPR, SOX)?
    • What level of technical testing is authorized?
    • What depth tier (0–3) is required and what is the budget?
    • What integration priorities and tool standards exist?
    • Are there known incidents, regulatory actions, or ongoing investigations?
    • What is the tolerance for remediation commitments or closing conditions?

    12.2 Data Request List by Workstream¶

    Governance & Organization

    • Security strategy and governance charters.
    • Org chart, staffing levels, and budget summary.
    • Risk assessment history and board reporting cadence.

    Asset Inventory & Architecture

    • Network diagrams and data flow maps.
    • Cloud account inventory and key services list.
    • CMDB exports and asset classification.

    Identity & Access Management

    • IAM policies and MFA coverage metrics.
    • Privileged access management documentation.
    • Access review evidence (last 12 months).

    Vulnerability & Patch Management

    • Vulnerability scan summaries and remediation SLA metrics.
    • Patch management process and exception reports.
    • External attack surface monitoring outputs (if available).

    Application Security

    • SDLC policy, code review standards, and pipeline controls.
    • SAST/DAST results and secure coding training records.
    • Third-party dependency management practices.

    Data Protection & Privacy

    • Data classification policy and encryption standards.
    • DLP controls, key management, and privacy impact assessments.
    • Data retention and deletion policies.

    Incident Response & Resilience

    • IR plan, playbooks, and tabletop exercise summaries.
    • Incident history for the last 24–36 months.
    • Backup and recovery test evidence.

    Third-Party Risk

    • Critical vendor list and due diligence outcomes.
    • Vendor contracts with security clauses.
    • SOC reports or equivalent assurance for key vendors.

    Compliance Evidence

    • SOC 2 or ISO 27001 reports (if applicable).
    • PCI/HIPAA/GxP evidence and audit results.
    • Regulatory correspondence or enforcement notices.

    12.3 Startup Advisory Due Diligence Checklist¶

    • Cap table, option pool size, and 409A valuation.
    • Fundraising history and next round timeline.
    • Existing compliance commitments (SOC 2, ISO, HIPAA).
    • Incident response ownership and escalation paths.
    • Customer security questionnaire backlog.
    • Existing vendor contracts with security clauses.
    • Roadmap alignment with product and revenue goals.

    12.4 Diligence Report Outline (Suggested)¶

    1. Executive summary and red flags.
    2. Deal context and scope assumptions.
    3. Diligence methodology and data sources.
    4. Findings by workstream with severity ratings.
    5. Remediation cost estimates and timeline.
    6. Integration roadmap and governance recommendations.
    7. Appendices with evidence inventory and interviews.

    13. KPIs & Reporting Cadence¶

    13.1 Core Startup Security KPIs¶

    • % of critical assets covered by MFA and logging.
    • % of SOC 2 / ISO controls implemented.
    • Time to detect/contain incidents; playbook coverage.
    • % of repos with CI/CD security checks.
    • Vulnerability backlog and mean time to remediate.
    • % of critical vendors assessed and contractually secured.

    13.2 Diligence Program KPIs (VC/PE)¶

    • % of deals with Tier 0 screening completed pre-LOI.
    • Average diligence cycle time (days).
    • % of deals with quantified remediation cost estimates.
    • % of portfolio companies with annual security roadmap updates.
    • Number of material cyber findings affecting valuation.

    14. Governance, Risk, and Guardrails¶

    14.1 Board & Advisory Guardrails¶

    • Ensure independence requirements are met for board roles.
    • Avoid conflicts where advisory services overlap with fiduciary duties.
    • Document scope boundaries between advisory and management roles.
    • Maintain confidentiality and MNPI compliance.

    14.2 Diligence Pitfalls to Avoid¶

    • Unclear testing permissions or scope creep.
    • Data room delays without minimum evidence package.
    • Over-scoped requests unrelated to investment thesis.
    • No remediation ownership or integration plan.
    • Failure to translate findings into deal terms.

    14.3 Risk Escalation Triggers¶

    • Unresolved security incidents or breach disclosures.
    • Lack of MFA for privileged or remote access.
    • No vulnerability management or patching cadence.
    • Incomplete asset inventory or cloud visibility.
    • Active regulatory investigation or enforcement.
