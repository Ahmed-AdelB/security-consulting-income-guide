    CODEX Blind Salary 10K: Security Engineer, CISO, Security Consultant TC (2025)¶

    Status & Access Limits¶

    This report is compiled from existing research files in this repository. Direct Blind app access is not available in this environment (network restricted), so Blind-specific data is limited to Blind-referenced anecdotes already captured internally. Non-Blind sources are included to establish baseline compensation ranges and total compensation (TC) patterns for comparison.

    Scope¶

    • Roles: Security Engineer (including Application Security), Security Consultant, CISO/vCISO
    • Compensation focus: Base, bonus, equity, and total compensation (TC)
    • Company focus: Big Tech, security consulting firms, and tech-adjacent security vendors

    Executive Summary¶

    • Security Engineer baselines: PayScale averages cluster around $103k-$106k; Glassdoor’s Cyber Security Engineer data shows higher averages around $158k with a $126k-$200k range.
    • Big Tech TC: Levels.fyi data shows $276k-$434k average TC for security engineers at major tech companies, with senior roles reaching $400k-$450k+.
    • AppSec premium: Application Security Engineer TC averages ~$219k, with senior ranges frequently above $250k.
    • CISO TC variance: CISO total comp spans $150k-$900k+, driven by company size, equity, and industry risk profile.
    • Security Consultant TC: Total comp centers around $128k with a $96k-$233k range; contractor rates average ~$145/hr and can exceed $250/hr for specialized engagements.
    • Blind signals are anecdotal: Blind data in this repo is qualitative, highlighting rate negotiation and equity upside rather than statistically representative salary bands.

    Total Compensation Structure (Base vs. TC)¶

    From internal analysis of cybersecurity compensation:

    • Base salary: 70-80% of total compensation for most full-time roles.
    • Annual bonus: Typically 10-20% of base.
    • Equity/stock: 5-15% of total comp for non-executives; much higher in Big Tech and high-growth startups.
    • Benefits value: Often 25-30% of salary cost when accounting for healthcare, 401(k) match, and training.

    Implication: A base salary of $150k often maps to a $180k-$210k total comp package when bonuses, equity, and benefits are included.

    Location & Industry Premiums¶

    • Top tech hubs (SF Bay Area, NYC, Seattle): +20-30% compensation premium over national averages.
    • Regulated industries (finance, healthcare, defense): +15-40% premium for security roles.
    • Clearance premium: +$20k-$30k on base salary in cleared markets (DC/NoVA).
