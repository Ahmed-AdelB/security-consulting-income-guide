    Platform Overview¶

    Platform Snapshot¶

    • Pivot Point Security delivers vCISO-led security programs for growth-stage and regulated organizations.
    • The platform blends advisory leadership, compliance expertise, and execution support.
    • Engagements are outcome-driven, aligning security priorities with business goals.
    • Services are delivered through a mix of strategic guidance and hands-on implementation.
    • The operating model is modular, enabling rapid onboarding and flexible scaling.

    Mission and Positioning¶

    • Mission: enable organizations to achieve trustworthy security without heavy overhead.
    • Vision: make enterprise-grade security leadership accessible to every growth company.
    • Positioning: a specialized vCISO partner for compliance and security execution.
    • Brand promise: clear guidance, measurable outcomes, and compliance-ready documentation.

    Core Problems Solved¶

    • Lack of internal security leadership or bandwidth.
    • Unclear compliance requirements and audit readiness gaps.
    • Fragmented security tooling with no cohesive program.
    • Difficulty translating security needs into engineering priorities.
    • Unstructured incident response and lack of executive reporting.

    Value Drivers¶

    • Accelerates compliance readiness and audit success.
    • Reduces security risk with prioritized, pragmatic control rollouts.
    • Improves stakeholder confidence through structured reporting.
    • Aligns security with product delivery and revenue goals.
    • Provides access to seasoned security leadership at fractional cost.

    Service Pillars¶

    • Security strategy and governance.
    • Compliance readiness and certification support.
    • Security program implementation and control design.
    • Risk management and vendor risk oversight.
    • Incident response planning and tabletop exercises.
    • Security awareness and culture building.

    Delivery Model¶

    • Phase 1: Discovery and baseline assessment.
    • Phase 2: Risk and compliance roadmap creation.
    • Phase 3: Execution support and control implementation.
    • Phase 4: Audit preparation and evidence management.
    • Phase 5: Continuous improvement and executive reporting.

    Engagement Cadence¶

    • Weekly operating meetings for tactical execution.
    • Monthly steering meetings for program governance.
    • Quarterly business reviews for strategic alignment.
    • Ad-hoc executive briefings for critical decisions.

    Stakeholder Alignment¶

    • Founders and executive leadership.
    • Engineering and infrastructure leadership.
    • Compliance and legal teams.
    • Customer success and sales stakeholders.
    • External auditors and assessors.

    Operating Principles¶

    • Focus on risk reduction over checkbox compliance.
    • Align security milestones with business initiatives.
    • Keep documentation audit-ready and living.
    • Prioritize automation and sustainable controls.
    • Build a security culture that scales with growth.

    Differentiators¶

    • Hands-on vCISO leadership with practical delivery support.
    • Deep experience in compliance frameworks for SaaS and fintech.
    • Structured templates and playbooks for faster execution.
    • Emphasis on continuous readiness rather than audit sprints.
    • Transparent metrics and executive reporting.

    Program Outcomes¶

    • Reduced audit cycle time and fewer evidence gaps.
    • Clear prioritization of security investments.
    • Higher confidence for customers, investors, and partners.
    • Improved incident readiness and response consistency.
    • Mature governance and accountability across teams.

    Success Metrics¶

    • Time to compliance baseline completion.
    • Percentage of controls implemented and validated.
    • Mean time to evidence collection.
    • Risk register trend and remediation velocity.
    • Audit findings closed within targeted timeframes.

    Typical Engagement Timeline¶

    • Week 1: kickoff, access setup, stakeholder alignment.
    • Week 2: documentation review, system mapping, gap analysis.
    • Week 3: risk scoring, compliance priorities, roadmap draft.
    • Week 4: roadmap approval, execution plan, KPI baselines.
    • Month 2+: execution sprints, evidence capture, training.

    Team Structure¶

    • Lead vCISO as primary executive sponsor.
    • Security program manager for tracking and coordination.
    • Compliance specialist for framework mapping.
    • Security engineer or consultant for control implementation.
    • Optional privacy or legal advisor for regulatory support.

    Communication Channels¶

    • Shared project workspace and ticketing system.
    • Document repository with versioned policies.
    • Dashboard for real-time KPI reporting.
    • Executive summaries with decisions and blockers.

    Service Boundaries¶

    • Pivot Point Security does not replace internal legal counsel.
    • Engineering changes are implemented by client teams or jointly.
    • Tool procurement decisions are guided but client-owned.
    • Final audit opinions remain with independent auditors.

    Typical Inputs¶

    • Asset inventory and system diagrams.
    • Existing policies, procedures, and risk registers.
    • Access to security tooling and logs.
    • Prior audit reports or questionnaires.
    • Product roadmap and strategic initiatives.

    Typical Outputs¶

    • Security program roadmap and milestone plan.
    • Compliance matrix aligned to frameworks.
    • Evidence repository and audit-ready artifacts.
    • Incident response plan and tabletop results.
    • Board-ready security posture updates.

    Compliance Focus¶

    Compliance Strategy¶

    • Compliance is treated as a structured risk management program.
    • Controls are prioritized based on business impact and audit deadlines.
    • Evidence is collected continuously to avoid audit crunches.
    • Policies are practical, implementable, and aligned with operations.

    Framework Coverage¶

    • SOC 2 Type I and Type II readiness.
    • ISO 27001 and ISO 27002 alignment.
    • NIST CSF and NIST 800-53 mapping.
    • PCI DSS for payment data environments.
    • HIPAA security rule and privacy rule alignment.
    • GDPR readiness and data protection controls.
    • CCPA and CPRA privacy considerations.
    • GLBA safeguards for financial services.
    • FedRAMP readiness for federal SaaS alignment.
    • DFARS and CMMC for defense supply chain.
    • NYDFS cybersecurity regulation alignment.
    • SEC cybersecurity disclosure readiness.

    Compliance Program Phases¶

    • Scope definition and boundary identification.
    • Control mapping and gap analysis.
    • Remediation planning and ownership assignment.
    • Evidence collection and validation.
    • Audit coordination and response support.

    Control Mapping Approach¶

    • Create a unified control library across frameworks.
    • Map each control to business processes and systems.
    • Assign control owners and define success criteria.
    • Establish test procedures and evidence types.
    • Track control maturity using a tiered scale.

    Evidence Management¶

    • Centralized evidence repository with version control.
    • Evidence requests mapped to controls and owners.
    • Automated collection where possible.
    • Evidence freshness tracked with expiration dates.
    • Audit-ready packaging and delivery workflow.

    Audit Readiness Support¶

    • Pre-audit readiness assessment.
    • Auditor selection guidance and scope validation.
    • Mock interviews and walkthrough preparation.
    • Audit response coordination and remediation tracking.
    • Post-audit lessons learned and program updates.

    Privacy and Data Protection¶

    • Data mapping and classification.
    • Privacy impact assessments for new features.
    • Data retention and deletion policy design.
    • DSR workflow design for access and deletion requests.
    • Vendor data processing addendum support.

    Vendor Risk Management¶

    • Tiered vendor classification and risk scoring.
    • Security questionnaires and evidence review.
    • Contractual security clause guidance.
    • Ongoing vendor monitoring and reassessment.
    • Offboarding and data return procedures.

    Policy Management¶

    • Policy architecture aligned to frameworks.
    • Policy lifecycle management and approvals.
    • Procedure documentation for operational controls.
    • Employee acknowledgement tracking.
    • Annual review and update cycles.

    Security Architecture Alignment¶

    • Align controls with cloud and infrastructure patterns.
    • Incorporate zero trust principles.
    • Document boundary diagrams and data flows.
    • Validate encryption and key management practices.
    • Ensure logging and monitoring coverage.

    Compliance by Design¶

    • Integrate compliance checks into SDLC.
    • Automate access reviews and change approvals.
    • Use infrastructure-as-code for consistent controls.
    • Embed privacy review in product planning.
    • Align release management with audit controls.

    Regulatory Change Management¶

    • Monitor regulatory updates for applicable regions.
    • Review changes quarterly with stakeholders.
    • Update policies and controls proactively.
    • Communicate updates to customers when required.

    Typical Compliance Deliverables¶

    • Control mapping matrix by framework.
    • Compliance roadmap with milestones.
    • Evidence collection schedule.
    • Audit-ready policy library.
    • Compliance metrics dashboard.

    Common Compliance Risks¶

    • Incomplete asset and data inventories.
    • Unclear control ownership and accountability.
    • Manual processes without evidence trails.
    • Lack of continuous monitoring.
    • Security policies not aligned to actual practices.

    Compliance Readiness Metrics¶

    • Percentage of controls with documented owners.
    • Percentage of controls with validated evidence.
    • Number of open audit items by priority.
    • Evidence collection cycle time.
    • Policy acceptance rate across the organization.

    Retainer Models¶

    Retainer Philosophy¶

    • Retainers are structured to match company stage and risk.
    • Every model includes strategic leadership and execution support.
    • Retainers are outcome-focused and periodically recalibrated.
    • Flexibility is built in through service credits and add-ons.

    Tier 1: Advisory Essentials¶

    • Target: early-stage teams seeking foundational guidance.
    • Cadence: monthly strategy sessions plus office hours.
    • Deliverables: risk register, control baseline, policy starter pack.
    • Support: compliance navigation and investor diligence prep.
    • Add-ons: tabletop exercise, vendor risk template pack.

    Tier 2: Growth Program¶

    • Target: scaling SaaS organizations preparing for SOC 2 or ISO 27001.
    • Cadence: biweekly execution sessions and monthly executive review.
    • Deliverables: compliance roadmap, evidence plan, audit readiness.
    • Support: security training, policy maturation, tooling guidance.
    • Add-ons: secure SDLC integration, privacy program design.

    Tier 3: Enterprise Readiness¶

    • Target: multi-team organizations with multiple products.
    • Cadence: weekly operating meetings and monthly steering committee.
    • Deliverables: multi-framework mapping, program KPIs, vendor oversight.
    • Support: dedicated program manager and compliance specialist.
    • Add-ons: business continuity and disaster recovery program.

    Tier 4: Regulated Industries¶

    • Target: fintech, healthcare, and critical infrastructure.
    • Cadence: weekly execution and quarterly board reporting.
    • Deliverables: regulatory mapping, risk quantification, audit coordination.
    • Support: privacy expertise, data governance, and compliance tracking.
    • Add-ons: third-party penetration testing coordination.

    Tier 5: Incident-Ready Partner¶

    • Target: organizations seeking rapid response readiness.
    • Cadence: monthly readiness drills and on-call advisory support.
    • Deliverables: incident response plan, playbooks, and response training.
    • Support: forensic preparation and communications guidance.
    • Add-ons: crisis simulation and executive media coaching.

    Service Credits¶

    • Credits cover ad-hoc needs like policy reviews and vendor assessments.
    • Unused credits roll within the retainer period, not beyond renewal.
    • Credits can be applied to specialized security engineering support.

    Retainer Customization¶

    • Scope adjusted based on asset complexity and compliance timelines.
    • Multi-framework compliance requires expanded evidence tracking.
    • Multi-region operations include privacy and localization workstreams.
    • Mergers or acquisitions add risk integration support.

    Contract Terms¶

    • Standard term lengths range from 6 to 12 months.
    • Early termination options include transition support.
    • Renewal includes a program maturity review and updated roadmap.

    Service-Level Expectations¶

    • Defined response times for executive inquiries.
    • Priority handling for audit-related requests.
    • Escalation path for incident support.
    • Clear ownership of deliverables and approvals.

    Optional Add-Ons¶

    • Secure architecture reviews for new products.
    • Penetration testing coordination and remediation tracking.
    • Supplier risk management deep dives.
    • Privacy impact assessments for high-risk features.
    • Security tooling optimization and automation.

    Transition and Offboarding¶

    • Knowledge transfer documentation and artifacts.
    • Final program maturity assessment.
    • Recommendations for internal handoff or new partner.
    • Archive of evidence and audit-ready documentation.

    • Series B: need formal SOC 2 or ISO 27001 readiness.
    • Series C+: need multi-framework and multi-region compliance.
    • Pre-IPO: need mature governance, audit history, and board reporting.

    • Enterprise deal requiring SOC 2 report.

    • Expansion into regulated markets.

    • Board request for formal security program.

    Engagement Priorities¶

    • Build a compliance-ready evidence base.

    Success Strategies¶

    Strategy Alignment¶

    • Tie security milestones to revenue-impacting goals.
    • Define security success metrics with executive sponsorship.
    • Document security posture narratives for stakeholders.
    • Align security roadmap with product development cycles.

    Discovery and Baseline¶

    • Conduct interviews with leadership and technical owners.
    • Inventory critical systems, data flows, and vendors.
    • Review existing policies, tools, and controls.
    • Identify top risks and compliance deadlines.
    • Establish baseline metrics and maturity scoring.

    Roadmap Development¶

    • Prioritize controls based on risk reduction and audit needs.
    • Define quarterly milestones with clear ownership.
    • Align resource requirements with staffing and budgets.
    • Track dependencies and cross-team collaboration needs.
    • Communicate roadmap in executive-friendly terms.

    Governance and Reporting¶

    • Create a security steering committee cadence.
    • Define reporting formats for executives and boards.
    • Track policy exceptions and risk acceptances.
    • Establish decision-making criteria for security investments.
    • Maintain meeting notes and action tracking.

    Control Implementation¶

    • Translate controls into actionable tasks.
    • Provide templates for procedures and evidence.
    • Enable automation for access reviews and logging.
    • Support infrastructure changes with design reviews.
    • Validate control efficacy with testing.

    Security Culture Enablement¶

    • Develop role-based training with measurable outcomes.
    • Reinforce security ownership across teams.
    • Include security checkpoints in onboarding.
    • Promote security champions within engineering teams.
    • Use incident learnings as training inputs.

    Incident Readiness¶

    • Define incident response roles and escalation.
    • Create playbooks for common incident types.
    • Run tabletop exercises quarterly.
    • Establish communications protocols for customers and regulators.
    • Align legal and PR support for crisis response.

    Risk Management¶

    • Maintain a living risk register with scoring methodology.
    • Track remediation progress and due dates.
    • Apply compensating controls where needed.
    • Reassess risk quarterly or after major changes.
    • Integrate risk updates into executive reporting.

    Customer Trust Enablement¶

    • Create standardized security questionnaire responses.
    • Develop security posture documentation for sales teams.
    • Provide customer-facing security and privacy statements.
    • Support RFP responses with evidence references.
    • Align SLAs and security obligations with contracts.

    Continuous Improvement¶

    • Measure control performance and adapt accordingly.
    • Conduct post-audit retrospectives.
    • Refresh policies annually or after major changes.
    • Update training content based on incidents.
    • Expand program scope as business grows.

    Budgeting and Resourcing¶

    • Forecast security investment needs by quarter.
    • Prioritize high-impact controls within budget constraints.
    • Identify cost-saving automation opportunities.
    • Justify security spend with risk reduction metrics.
    • Plan for external audits and certifications.

    30/60/90 Day Plan¶

    First 30 Days¶

    • Kickoff meeting and stakeholder alignment.
    • Access to systems, policies, and evidence repositories.
    • Initial security and compliance gap assessment.
    • Risk register creation and initial scoring.
    • Draft roadmap with quick wins identified.
    • Establish meeting cadence and reporting templates.

    Days 31-60¶

    • Finalize compliance scope and framework mapping.
    • Implement priority controls and document procedures.
    • Launch policy updates and staff acknowledgements.
    • Begin evidence collection and validation.
    • Conduct first tabletop exercise or readiness drill.
    • Provide executive update with milestones and risks.

    Days 61-90¶

    • Validate controls against audit requirements.
    • Close critical gaps and confirm remediation.
    • Build customer trust artifacts and questionnaires.
    • Prepare for auditor selection or readiness review.
    • Mature metrics dashboard and KPI reporting.
    • Review roadmap and adjust for next quarter.

    Risk Scoring Model¶

    Scoring Inputs¶

    • Likelihood based on threat activity and control maturity.
    • Impact based on data sensitivity and operational criticality.
    • Regulatory exposure based on data type and jurisdiction.
    • Customer impact based on contract requirements.

    Scoring Scale¶

    • Likelihood scale: 1 (rare) to 5 (frequent).
    • Impact scale: 1 (minimal) to 5 (severe).
    • Risk score: likelihood x impact.

    Risk Categories¶

    • Low: 1-5.
    • Medium: 6-12.
    • High: 13-19.
    • Critical: 20-25.

    Remediation Expectations¶

    • Critical risks remediated within 30 days.
    • High risks remediated within 60 days.
    • Medium risks remediated within 90 days.
    • Low risks addressed during quarterly planning.

    Responsibilities¶

    • Security strategy: vCISO (R), Executive Sponsor (A).
    • Compliance roadmap: vCISO (R), Compliance Owner (A).

    • Evidence collection: Compliance Owner (R), vCISO (C).
    • Incident response: Security Operations (R), vCISO (A).
    • Training and awareness: HR (R), vCISO (C).

    Glossary¶

    Key Terms¶

    • vCISO: a virtual Chief Information Security Officer providing fractional leadership.
    • Control: a policy, process, or technology mitigating risk.
    • Evidence: artifacts proving controls operate effectively.
    • Gap assessment: analysis of current state against required controls.
    • Readiness: the state of being prepared for audit or assessment.

    Compliance Terms¶

    • SOC 2: a standard for service organizations covering trust principles.
    • ISO 27001: an information security management system standard.
    • NIST CSF: a framework for identifying and managing cybersecurity risk.
    • PCI DSS: a standard for protecting cardholder data.
    • HIPAA: healthcare privacy and security regulation in the United States.

    Operational Terms¶

    • Risk register: a repository tracking identified risks and remediation.
    • Tabletop exercise: a simulated incident to test response plans.
    • Evidence window: the time range evidence must cover for audits.
    • Control owner: the person accountable for implementing a control.

    Appendix: Control Maturity Levels¶

    Level 1: Initial¶

    • Ad-hoc processes with minimal documentation.
    • Limited accountability for controls.

    Level 2: Managed¶

    • Controls documented with assigned owners.
    • Evidence collected inconsistently.

    Level 3: Defined¶

    • Controls standardized and consistently executed.
    • Evidence collection scheduled and tracked.

    Level 4: Measured¶

    • Control performance measured and reported.
    • Automation used for evidence collection.

    Level 5: Optimized¶

    • Continuous improvement and proactive risk reduction.
    • Controls integrated deeply into operations.
