    CODEX: RED TEAM & ADVERSARY SIMULATION INCOME GUIDE 2025¶

    Status: CONFIDENTIAL Date: December 2025 Scope: Red Team, Adversary Simulation, Purple Team Consulting Rates Version: 1.0

    1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY¶

    The market for Offensive Security consulting has matured significantly in 2025. While traditional penetration testing remains a commodity, Red Teaming and Adversary Simulation have solidified as premium, high-margin services. Organizations are no longer satisfied with simple vulnerability scans; they require holistic "stress tests" of their people, processes, and technologies against realistic threat actors.

    Key Findings: * Premium Rates: Senior Freelance Red Team Operators can command $200-$350+ per hour for specialized engagements. * Project Values: Comprehensive Adversary Simulation engagements often range from $60,000 to $150,000+ per project. * Purple Team Growth: Collaborative "Purple Team" engagements are seeing the fastest growth, with daily rates for consultants reaching $2,500 - $3,150.

    2. FREELANCE RED TEAM OPERATOR RATES (2025)¶

    Unlike general IT consulting, Red Teaming requires a rare blend of engineering, social engineering, and evasion skills. This scarcity drives high hourly and daily rates.

    Hourly Rate Breakdown¶

    Experience Level Role Hourly Rate (USD) Hourly Rate (EUR) Notes
    Junior / Mid Red Team Operator $80 - $130 €75 - €120 often sub-contracted by larger firms
    Senior Sr. Operator / Lead $150 - $250 €140 - €220 Independent consultant billing direct to client
    Expert / Niche Exploit Dev / AI Red Team $300 - $500+ €280 - €450+ Specialized (e.g., Mainframe, SCADA, LLM attacks)

    Daily Rate Benchmarks¶

    • Standard Commercial: $1,200 - $2,000 per day.
    • Senior / Specialist: $2,200 - $3,500 per day.
    • UK/Europe (G-Cloud/TIBER): £1,750 - £3,000 (~$2,200 - $3,800) per day.

    Insight: Many senior consultants prefer Day Rates over hourly billing to avoid micromanagement of hours and to account for "thinking time" and research that doesn't look like active typing.

    3. ADVERSARY SIMULATION CONSULTING PACKAGES¶

    Adversary Simulation is sold differently than penetration testing. It is outcome-based and typically follows a campaign structure.

    Pricing Models¶

    1. The "Campaign" Model (Project-Based)¶

    • Description: A fixed-scope engagement lasting 4-12 weeks. Mimics a specific threat actor (e.g., APT29).
    • Price Range: $60,000 - $150,000+ per campaign.
    • Includes:
      • OSINT & Reconnaissance (2-3 weeks)
      • Initial Access & Persistence (2-4 weeks)
      • Lateral Movement & Objective Execution (2-4 weeks)
      • Reporting & Debrief (1-2 weeks)

    2. The "Retainer" Model (CaaS - Cybercrime as a Service)¶

    • Description: Continuous red teaming capabilities. The client pays a monthly subscription for a set number of "attacks" or "objectives" per quarter.
    • Price Range: $15,000 - $40,000 per month.
    • Benefit: Provides steady cash flow for the consultant and continuous assurance for the client.

    3. Breach & Attack Simulation (BAS) Management¶

    • Description: Managing and tuning automated BAS tools (like Cortex XSOAR, Cymulate, etc.) for a client.
    • Price Range: $7,000 - $15,000 per month (excluding software costs).

    4. PURPLE TEAM SERVICES INCOME¶

    Purple Teaming (Red + Blue collaboration) is highly profitable because it is interactive and educational. Clients perceive high value because their internal teams are trained during the process.

    Engagement Structures & Pricing¶

    • Workshops (3-5 Days):

      • Intensive, onsite or remote sessions executing specific TTPs.
      • Rate: $15,000 - $25,000 per workshop.
      • High Margin: Once the playbooks are developed, delivery cost is low.

    • Per-User / Per-Endpoint Pricing:

      • Base fee (e.g., $10k) + per-node fee.
      • Example: Base $10k + $75/user for the first 250 users.

    • Government / Defense (Simulated Attack & Response):

      • These contracts are often strictly time-and-materials.
      • Rate: £1,750 - £2,500 ($2,200 - $3,150) per day per consultant.

    5. SALARY VS. FREELANCE COMPARISON¶

    Is it worth going independent?

    Metric Senior Employee (W2) Independent Consultant (1099)
    Annual Income $140k - $220k $250k - $450k+ (Gross)
    Hourly Equivalent ~$70 - $110/hr $150 - $300+/hr
    Stability High Variable (Feast/Famine)
    Benefits Paid by Employer Paid by You
    Overhead None Marketing, Insurance, Tools, Legal

    The Multiplier Rule: To maintain the same standard of living and risk profile, an independent consultant needs to charge 2.5x to 3x their desired W2 hourly wage. * Goal: $150k Salary -> Target Rate: ~$180 - $215/hr

    6. NICHE OPPORTUNITIES FOR 2025¶

    To command the highest rates (>$300/hr), specialize in these emerging areas:

    1. AI Red Teaming: Testing LLMs for jailbreaks, prompt injection, and data leakage. Rates are currently inflated due to scarcity. ($300-$500/hr)
    2. Cloud-Native Adversary Sim: Specializing purely in AWS/Azure/GCP attack paths, bypassing traditional AD-focused red teams.
    3. OT/SCADA Red Teaming: Manufacturing and critical infrastructure. extremely high barrier to entry, extremely high rates.
    4. TIBER-EU / CBEST Compliance: Regulatory-driven red teaming for financial institutions in Europe. Requires certification but guarantees high-budget projects.
