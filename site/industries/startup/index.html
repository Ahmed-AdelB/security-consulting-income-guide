    MASSIVE 10K RESOURCE RESEARCH: Cybersecurity Startup Consulting (Security Advisor Equity + Startup CISO Compensation)¶

    Compiled: 2025-12-24
    Scope: Startup security consulting economics, advisor equity frameworks, and startup CISO compensation benchmarks.
    Data note: This guide is synthesized from local research files in this repo; no live web browsing was performed. Local sources emphasize equity importance but rarely quantify advisor equity percentages, so equity bands below are heuristic ranges meant for planning and negotiation.

    1. Executive Summary¶

    • Startup CISO base range: Early-stage startup CISOs typically sit around $160k–$210k base + equity, while mid-market to growth-stage companies trend $230k–$330k cash with higher total comp potential.
    • Total comp by revenue (IANS benchmarks): Under $50M revenue $260k total ($230k cash), $50M–$200M $330k total ($280k cash), $200M–$600M $365k total ($310k cash), $600M–$1B+ $415k+ total ($350k+ cash).
    • Compensation tiers (Levels-style): Tier 3 SMBs $150k–$250k, Tier 2 mid‑market/unicorns $300k–$500k, Tier 1 big tech $500k–$900k+ total comp with heavy equity weight.
    • Equity impact: Local salary research notes equity can double base salary in top-tier packages and that equity/LTI should be ~20%–40% of total comp for executive‑level security leaders.
    • Full-time vs fractional: vCISO cost savings are consistently 60%–75% vs full‑time CISO total compensation. Typical vCISO pricing spans $150–$500/hr and $2k–$25k/month retainers, making fractional leadership the default for many startups.
    • Advisor equity reality: Advisors are rarely paid market cash; equity is used to close the gap. Equity is most defensible when paired with concrete deliverables (security roadmap, risk ownership, compliance readiness).
    • Negotiation anchor: Treat the full‑time CISO total cost and market consulting rates as anchors, then back into a cash + equity mix that reflects time, risk, and accountability.

    3. Startup Security Consulting Landscape¶

    Startups adopt security consulting earlier in their lifecycle than ever before. Primary drivers in the local research corpus include:

    • Enterprise sales pressure: SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA, PCI, and customer security questionnaires are required even at seed/Series A.
    • Board/investor scrutiny: Security posture now impacts valuation and fundraising timelines.
    • Regulatory expansion: SEC disclosure rules, state privacy laws, and industry mandates are pushing earlier security leadership hiring.
    • CISO burnout + short tenure: Average tenure is 18–26 months and 62% of CISOs report burnout, accelerating demand for fractional leadership.
    • Cost sensitivity: Startups often cannot justify $300k–$500k+ total CISO cost, so they use fractional or advisory models to defer full-time hires.

    4.4 Total Compensation Structure (Cash + Equity)¶

    • Equity dominance at the top: Local salary research notes equity can double base salary in top-tier CISO packages.
    • Executive mix target: Equity/LTI should typically represent 20%–40% of total comp for executive-level security leaders.
    • Tiered equity weights:
    • Tier 1 (big tech / mega-cap): High equity and performance bonuses.
    • Tier 2 (unicorns / mid-market tech): Base often $250k+ with meaningful upside on exit.
    • Tier 3 (SMB/startup): Lower base, higher equity sensitivity.

    4.5 Geographic & Industry Modifiers¶

    • Top markets: San Jose, San Francisco, NYC, DC, and Seattle command 30%–100% premiums.
    • Industry premiums: Finance, tech, and regulated sectors (healthcare) pay 25%–50% more.
    • Startups vs enterprises: Startups consistently trade lower base for equity upside (“lower base + equity”).

    5. Security Advisor Equity Deep Dive¶

    5.1 Advisor Role Archetypes¶

    • Lightweight Advisor (1–2 hrs/month): Occasional strategy calls, intros, security posture review.
    • Active Advisor (2–6 hrs/month): Security roadmap ownership, risk planning, compliance prep.
    • Fractional CISO (10–30 hrs/month): Ongoing leadership, board reporting, compliance ownership.
    • Board/Committee Member: Fiduciary role, governance oversight, potential liability exposure.

    5.2 Equity Instruments Commonly Used¶

    • Stock options (NSO/ISO): Most common advisor equity grant for private startups.
    • Restricted stock (early-stage): Useful pre‑revenue for favorable tax treatment.
    • RSUs (late-stage): More common as companies mature.
    • Advisor equity pools: Separate from employee option pools, often 1%–2% of fully diluted equity.

    5.4 Equity vs Cash Trade-Off Logic¶

    • Anchor on market rate: A fractional CISO at $200–$300/hr (typical market) sets the baseline cash equivalent.
    • Quantify the gap: If the startup can only afford 50% of market cash, equity should compensate for the other 50%.
    • Example conversion:
    • 8 hrs/month at $250/hr = $24,000/year cash value.
    • If cash offered is $12,000/year, the equity grant should target the remaining $12,000 in expected value.
    • Use realistic exit probabilities; equity is not guaranteed cash.

    5.5 Vesting & Term Structure (Typical Patterns)¶

    • Vesting: 2–4 years, monthly or quarterly vesting.
    • Cliff: 3–12 months; advisors often use shorter cliffs than employees.
    • Acceleration: Negotiable on acquisition or change‑of‑control.
    • Scope lock: Equity should be tied to explicit deliverables and time commitments.

    5.6 Dilution & Risk Reality¶

    • Dilution: Expect 15%–30% dilution across future fundraising rounds.
    • Option pool refreshes: Advisor equity can be diluted by pool expansions.
    • Exit risk: Most equity outcomes are $0; treat equity as long-tail upside.

    5.7 When to Prefer Cash Over Equity¶

    • High time commitment (20+ hrs/month) with executive accountability.
    • Regulatory exposure or breach liability.
    • Short runway (<9 months) with uncertain fundraising.
    • High operational workload (incident response, audits, customer escalations).

    6. Startup Consulting Compensation Models¶

    6.1 Pricing Structures (Local Benchmarks)¶

    Model Typical Range Best Use
    Hourly $150–$500/hr Short advisory, incident response
    Monthly retainer $2k–$25k/month Ongoing leadership, roadmap delivery
    Project-based $5k–$100k+ SOC 2 readiness, gap assessments
    Annual package $30k–$240k Full security program ownership

    6.2 Startup-Friendly Offer Design¶

    • Seed/Series A: Security roadmap + foundational policies + vendor risk guidance.
    • Series B: Compliance readiness (SOC 2/ISO) + incident response + security program KPIs.
    • Series C+: Security governance, board reporting, and product security scaling.

    6.3 Packaging Example (Seed-Stage)¶

    • Discovery + risk assessment: $7.5k–$15k one-time.
    • Monthly retainer: $3k–$7.5k for 8–15 hrs/month.
    • Equity add‑on: 0.15%–0.50% to offset cash gap.

    7. Scenario Models (Cash + Equity Blends)¶

    7.1 Early-Stage Fractional CISO (Seed)¶

    • Scope: 8–12 hrs/month, security roadmap, SOC 2 readiness planning.
    • Cash: $2,500–$5,000/month.
    • Equity: 0.25%–0.75% with 2–4 year vesting.
    • Rationale: Startup can’t afford $200–$300/hr full market rate; equity bridges the gap.

    7.2 Series A Full-Time CISO (Growth-Ready)¶

    • Cash base: $200k–$250k.
    • Bonus: 10%–20% tied to audit and risk milestones.
    • Equity: 0.5%–1.0% (higher if the role is the first security exec).

    7.3 Series B/C Full-Time CISO (Scale Stage)¶

    • Cash base: $250k–$320k.
    • Bonus: 15%–25% with compliance + incident KPIs.
    • Equity: 0.25%–0.75% depending on valuation and stage.

    7.4 Late-Stage / Pre‑IPO CISO¶

    • Total comp target: $350k–$500k+ with high equity weighting.
    • Equity: RSUs or options tied to IPO/exit milestones.

    7.5 Advisor Portfolio Strategy¶

    • Portfolio approach: 3–6 advisory roles at 2–4 hrs/month each.
    • Cash floor: $1.5k–$4k/month per startup.
    • Equity targets: 0.1%–0.5% per company (stage dependent).
    • Risk logic: A portfolio diversifies the low probability of a single exit.

    8. Negotiation & Due Diligence Checklist¶

    Compensation Anchors

    • Full-time CISO total cost by revenue band.
    • Market fractional rate ($200–$300/hr typical).
    • Equity/LTI target (20%–40% of total comp for executives).

    Equity Due Diligence

    • Fully diluted cap table and option pool size.
    • 409A valuation, strike price, and vesting schedule.
    • Fundraising history and next round timeline.
    • Board expectations and liability protections.

    Role Scope Clarity

    • Compliance obligations (SOC 2/ISO/HIPAA/PCI).
    • Incident response ownership and escalation paths.
    • Reporting cadence to CEO/board/investors.

    9. 30/60/90 Day Startup CISO Playbook (Advisory or Full-Time)¶

    First 30 Days

    • Establish asset inventory and data classification.
    • Run lightweight risk assessment + top 10 risk register.
    • Document current security posture and gap analysis.

    Days 31–60

    • Define security roadmap with prioritized initiatives.
    • Launch baseline policies (access control, incident response, vendor risk).
    • Prepare SOC 2/ISO readiness plan if enterprise sales require it.

    Days 61–90

    • Establish KPI reporting cadence to leadership.
    • Execute core controls (MFA, logging, secure SDLC, DR plan).
    • Align security spending with product roadmap and customer demands.

    10. Core KPIs for Startup Security Leaders¶

    • Security posture: % critical assets covered by MFA, logging, backups.
    • Compliance readiness: % of SOC 2/ISO controls implemented.
    • Incident readiness: Time to detect/contain and IR playbook coverage.
    • Engineering security: % repos with CI/CD security checks; vulnerability backlog.
    • Vendor risk: % critical vendors assessed and contractually secured.

    11. Actionable Recommendations (Fast Path)¶

    • Startups (pre‑Series B): Prioritize fractional CISO retainers over full-time hires.
    • Advisors: Anchor on market hourly rates, then convert cash gaps into equity with explicit deliverables.
    • Executives: Use total comp benchmarks (cash + equity) and insist on equity weightings aligned to 20%–40% of TC.
    • Consultants: Productize repeatable offerings (SOC 2 readiness, security roadmap) to shorten sales cycles.
