    Platform Overview¶

    Positioning Summary¶

    • FRSECURE is positioned as a security consulting and advisory firm.
    • The vCISO offering should feel like a managed leadership function.
    • The “platform” is the combined people, process, and tooling system.
    • The platform should be modular to fit small to mid-market budgets.
    • The platform should emphasize measurable risk reduction.

    Core Platform Capabilities¶

    • Security leadership and governance strategy.
    • Risk assessment and risk register management.
    • Security program roadmap development and tracking.
    • Policy and standards development and maintenance.
    • Compliance readiness guidance and audit support.
    • Vendor risk management oversight and scoring.
    • Incident response planning and tabletop facilitation.
    • Security awareness planning and training coordination.
    • Metrics and reporting for executives and boards.
    • Security architecture advice and control validation.

    Service Delivery Characteristics¶

    • Remote-first service delivery with structured cadences.
    • Flexible levels of engagement based on retainer tiers.
    • Documentation-first culture to preserve continuity.
    • Emphasis on practical, budget-aligned recommendations.
    • Clear prioritization of top risks and “quick wins.”

    Value Drivers¶

    • CISO-level insight without full-time hiring cost.
    • Accelerated compliance readiness and audit success.
    • Reduced breach likelihood through targeted controls.
    • Improved executive alignment on security priorities.
    • Shared accountability through documented decisions.

    Differentiators to Highlight¶

    • Advisory-first approach instead of tool selling.
    • Strong risk-centric storytelling for executives.
    • Modular deliverables scaled to client maturity.
    • Clear “no-surprises” reporting cadence.
    • Access to broader FRSECURE consulting expertise.

    Platform Components¶

    • Client onboarding and discovery framework.
    • Risk assessment methodology and scoring matrix.
    • Security roadmap templates and tracking system.
    • Policy library and update workflow.
    • Metrics dashboard and reporting packs.
    • Executive communications templates.
    • Incident response and crisis coordination playbooks.
    • Vendor risk due diligence checklist library.

    Verification Checklist¶

    • Confirm current FRSECURE service catalog and pricing.
    • Confirm staffing model and typical engagement size.
    • Confirm any proprietary tooling or partner platforms.
    • Confirm standard SLAs and response expectations.
    • Confirm industry focus or preferred verticals.

    vCISO Service Model¶

    Service Model Principles¶

    • Security leadership as a service, not just compliance support.
    • Focus on business outcomes and risk-informed decisions.
    • Transparent prioritization and evidence-based reporting.
    • Progressive maturity improvement with measurable milestones.
    • Consistent documentation and knowledge transfer.

    Engagement Lifecycle¶

    • Discovery and alignment with executive stakeholders.
    • Baseline risk assessment and control inventory.
    • Gap analysis mapped to prioritized risk themes.
    • Roadmap creation with phased recommendations.
    • Execution support with continuous governance.
    • Quarterly strategy review and roadmap recalibration.

    Discovery and Alignment¶

    • Identify business objectives and major revenue drivers.
    • Document critical assets, data types, and workflows.
    • Map regulatory obligations and contractual requirements.
    • Assess risk tolerance and board expectations.
    • Inventory existing security and IT resources.

    Assessment and Baseline¶

    • Conduct interviews with key stakeholders.
    • Review existing policies, standards, and SOPs.
    • Assess control coverage across people, process, and tech.
    • Build a risk register with likelihood and impact ratings.
    • Establish baseline metrics and maturity level.

    Roadmap and Planning¶

    • Translate findings into a 12-month roadmap.
    • Define phases, owners, and expected outcomes.
    • Estimate budget and time requirements per initiative.
    • Align initiatives to risk reduction and compliance goals.
    • Capture dependencies and sequencing needs.

    Execution Support¶

    • Provide guidance to internal teams and vendors.
    • Review evidence artifacts for compliance readiness.
    • Facilitate decisions on control scope and prioritization.
    • Track progress against roadmap milestones.
    • Manage exceptions and acceptance decisions.

    Governance and Reporting¶

    • Monthly operational check-ins and status reports.
    • Quarterly executive updates and risk summaries.
    • Board-ready dashboards with trend analysis.
    • Ongoing update of the risk register and roadmap.
    • Lessons learned and continuous improvement notes.

    Roles and Responsibilities¶

    • vCISO leads strategy, risk, and governance.
    • Client executive sponsor owns business alignment.
    • IT or security operations handles control execution.
    • Compliance lead manages audit evidence and artifacts.
    • Business unit leaders validate operational impacts.

    Typical Cadence¶

    • Weekly or biweekly working sessions.
    • Monthly executive status summary.
    • Quarterly roadmap review and risk update.
    • Annual program maturity assessment.

    Deliverables by Phase¶

    • Discovery summary and stakeholder map.
    • Current-state maturity assessment.
    • Risk register with prioritized risks.
    • Security roadmap with phases and budgets.
    • Policy and standard recommendations.
    • Incident response plan updates.
    • Metrics dashboard and KPI definitions.

    Tooling and Systems¶

    • Risk register tracking system.
    • Document repository for policies and evidence.
    • Project tracking for roadmap initiatives.
    • Metrics dashboard or reporting spreadsheet.
    • Secure communication channels and meetings.

    Data Handling Considerations¶

    • Define data classification boundaries early.
    • Minimize access to production data when possible.
    • Use secure file transfer for sensitive artifacts.
    • Document access approvals and revocations.
    • Align data retention with legal requirements.

    Retainer Structures ($1K-$6K per Month)¶

    Pricing Philosophy¶

    • Price for outcomes, not just hours.
    • Match tier complexity to client maturity.
    • Provide clear limits and upgrade paths.
    • Align scope with most valuable risks first.
    • Separate advisory from heavy implementation work.

    Tier 1: $1K per Month¶

    • Basic advisory check-ins and Q&A.
    • Quarterly risk review and light roadmap updates.
    • Limited policy review and gap identification.
    • Email support with defined response windows.
    • Suitable for very small firms or startups.

    Tier 2: $2K per Month¶

    • Monthly governance call and status notes.
    • Risk register initiation and maintenance.
    • Basic security roadmap with 3-6 initiatives.
    • Annual policy refresh guidance.
    • Suitable for small firms with compliance exposure.

    Tier 3: $3K per Month¶

    • Biweekly working sessions with stakeholders.
    • Expanded risk assessment and prioritization.
    • Roadmap with phased execution and budgets.
    • Vendor risk management baseline process.
    • Suitable for growing businesses with audits.

    Tier 4: $4K per Month¶

    • Weekly or biweekly execution support sessions.
    • Detailed control gap analysis and remediation plan.
    • Compliance mapping and audit readiness support.
    • Security awareness program planning.
    • Suitable for regulated or healthcare orgs.

    Tier 5: $5K per Month¶

    • Embedded vCISO presence with strategic planning.
    • Ongoing risk register updates and reporting.
    • Program metrics dashboard and board-ready reports.
    • Incident response planning and tabletop exercise.
    • Suitable for mid-market organizations.

    Tier 6: $6K per Month¶

    • Fractional CISO leadership with broad scope.
    • Comprehensive roadmap delivery management.
    • Continuous compliance and vendor risk oversight.
    • Executive coaching and board engagement.
    • Suitable for complex regulated environments.

    Optional Add-Ons¶

    • On-site workshops and executive briefings.
    • Penetration testing and red team support.
    • Security architecture and cloud review.
    • Data privacy program development.
    • Business continuity and disaster recovery planning.

    Retainer Structure Notes¶

    • Define expected hours or deliverables per tier.
    • Set a quarterly scope check to prevent drift.
    • Provide a simple upgrade path across tiers.
    • Include fixed response SLAs for each tier.
    • Clarify what is advisory versus execution.

    Sample SOW Outline¶

    • Engagement objective and success criteria.
    • Scope of work and exclusions.
    • Cadence and meeting schedule.
    • Deliverables and acceptance criteria.
    • Reporting requirements and contacts.
    • Confidentiality and data handling terms.

    Client Types and Buying Triggers¶

    Small Business and Startup¶

    • Limited budget but high compliance pressure.
    • CEO or founder is primary decision-maker.
    • Needs pragmatic policies and risk visibility.
    • Often lacks internal security leadership.
    • Values fast guidance and templates.

    Healthcare and Life Sciences¶

    • HIPAA and patient data security requirements.
    • Vendor oversight and BAAs are key concerns.
    • Breach reporting risk is a primary driver.
    • Needs incident response readiness.
    • Strong need for policy completeness.

    Financial Services and Fintech¶

    • Regulatory compliance and audit pressure.
    • High sensitivity to data security incidents.
    • Third-party risk management expectations.
    • Need for governance, metrics, and board reports.
    • Often requires formalized security controls.

    SaaS and Technology Providers¶

    • Sales enablement via SOC 2 readiness.
    • Customer security questionnaires and RFIs.
    • Need for formal policies and evidence.
    • Rapid growth drives control gaps.
    • Requires repeatable compliance workflows.

    Manufacturing and Industrial¶

    • OT and IT convergence risks.
    • Legacy system challenges and patching constraints.
    • Supply chain and vendor risk exposures.
    • Needs clear risk prioritization and resilience.
    • Focus on business continuity and safety.

    Professional Services¶

    • Client demands for security posture evidence.
    • Data privacy and confidentiality obligations.
    • Limited internal security resources.
    • Need for clear policies and training.
    • Desire to reduce cyber insurance costs.

    Buying Triggers¶

    • Failed audit or pending compliance deadline.
    • Security incident or close call.
    • New customer requiring security assurances.
    • Leadership change or growth milestone.
    • Cyber insurance renewal friction.

    Buying Committee¶

    • Executive sponsor sets budget and priority.
    • IT leader owns execution coordination.
    • Compliance or legal validates requirements.
    • Finance evaluates ROI and cost control.
    • Business leaders confirm operational impact.

    Success Strategies and Execution Playbook¶

    Provider-Side Success Strategies¶

    • Lead with risk reduction, not tool recommendations.
    • Keep deliverables highly actionable and prioritized.
    • Maintain a consistent cadence and transparency.
    • Document decisions and tradeoffs to reduce drift.
    • Use templates to shorten time-to-value.

    Client-Side Success Strategies¶

    • Assign a single executive sponsor.
    • Allocate internal execution owners.
    • Schedule recurring governance meetings.
    • Commit to evidence collection for audits.
    • Track roadmap progress publicly.

    First 30 Days¶

    • Complete stakeholder interviews.
    • Inventory critical assets and data flows.
    • Gather existing policies and evidence.
    • Establish risk register and initial scoring.
    • Identify top five risks and quick wins.

    Days 31-60¶

    • Deliver a draft security roadmap.
    • Align roadmap with budget planning.
    • Define KPI baseline and reporting format.
    • Begin policy and standard updates.
    • Start vendor risk triage for critical vendors.

    Days 61-90¶

    • Finalize roadmap and phased milestones.
    • Launch top-priority remediation tasks.
    • Run an incident response tabletop exercise.
    • Provide first executive metrics report.
    • Conduct a lessons-learned review and adjust.

    Ongoing Quarterly Motions¶

    • Refresh risk register with new findings.
    • Review roadmap progress and update timelines.
    • Provide executive summary and trend analysis.
    • Reassess business changes and new threats.
    • Adjust priorities for budget or staffing changes.

    Communication Strategies¶

    • Use plain-language risk descriptions for executives.
    • Translate security initiatives into business impact.
    • Share progress as percent complete with benefits.
    • Highlight wins to maintain stakeholder support.
    • Report exceptions and accepted risks clearly.

    Metrics and KPI Focus¶

    • Risk reduction progress over time.
    • Policy coverage and compliance evidence.
    • Mean time to remediate high risks.
    • Training completion and phishing metrics.
    • Vendor assessment coverage for critical vendors.

    Common Pitfalls and Mitigation¶

    • Scope creep without budget alignment.
    • Lack of internal owners for execution.
    • Over-indexing on compliance over real risks.
    • Poor visibility into third-party dependencies.
    • Infrequent communication with executives.

    Comparison with SideChannel¶

    SideChannel Overview (High-Level)¶

    • SideChannel is positioned as a vCISO-as-a-service provider.
    • Emphasizes a subscription-based security leadership model.
    • Often highlights standardized playbooks and tooling.
    • Targets mid-market and growth-stage companies.
    • Provides security leadership without full-time hiring.

    Comparison Dimensions¶

    Dimension FRSECURE vCISO SideChannel vCISO
    Delivery model Advisory-led with flexible consulting depth Subscription-led with standardized playbooks
    Engagement style Tailored to client maturity and needs Often productized with repeatable workflows
    Tooling May leverage client tooling or partner stack Promotes a platform-centric delivery model
    Target segment Broad SMB to mid-market SMB to mid-market with scale focus
    Differentiator Deep consulting bench and custom strategy Repeatable playbooks and rapid onboarding
    Executive reporting Customized board-ready materials Standardized dashboards and reporting

    Positioning Guidance vs SideChannel¶

    • Emphasize FRSECURE’s consultative depth for complex risk.
    • Highlight bespoke roadmap planning over generic templates.
    • Highlight integration with existing client processes.
    • Emphasize hands-on strategic leadership engagement.
    • Use SideChannel as a benchmark for operational efficiency.

    When to Choose FRSECURE¶

    • Client needs customized advisory and complex risk analysis.
    • Client requires deep compliance mapping and audit support.
    • Client has unique regulatory constraints or legacy systems.
    • Client needs intensive executive coaching.
    • Client wants flexible retainer structure and scope.

    When SideChannel May Be a Fit¶

    • Client wants a productized and fast-start model.
    • Client prefers standardized playbooks over custom advisory.
    • Client is early maturity with limited scope.
    • Client values a platform dashboard over bespoke reports.
    • Client seeks predictable, fixed-scope subscription.

    Sales Enablement and Messaging¶

    Value Proposition Statements¶

    • “Security leadership without the full-time cost.”
    • “Risk-informed roadmap aligned to business priorities.”
    • “Audit-ready documentation and evidence collection.”
    • “Executive-ready reporting that enables decisions.”
    • “Scalable program maturity with clear milestones.”

    Discovery Questions¶

    • What are your top business objectives this year.
    • Which compliance frameworks are in scope.
    • What has blocked security progress in the past.
    • Which risks keep leadership up at night.
    • How do you currently track remediation progress.

    Objection Handling¶

    • “We already have IT” → Focus on leadership and governance gap.
    • “Budget is tight” → Emphasize phased roadmap and quick wins.
    • “We only need compliance” → Link compliance to business risk.
    • “We need hands-on execution” → Offer add-ons and partners.
    • “We’re not big enough” → Emphasize right-sized controls.

    • Reduced audit failures and remediation costs.
    • Lower breach likelihood and incident impact.
    • Improved cyber insurance terms.
    • Faster sales cycles through security readiness.
