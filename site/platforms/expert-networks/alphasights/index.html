    2. AlphaSights Snapshot¶

    • Official name: AlphaSights.
    • Market position: Top 3 globally (The "Big Three": GLG, AlphaSights, Guidepoint).
    • Headquarters: London / New York (strong Euro/US presence).
    • Reputation: "White glove" service, premium clients, aggressive recruiting.
    • Primary client groups: Private Equity (PE), Strategy Consulting (MBB), Hedge Funds.
    • Service types: Expert calls (primary), B2B surveys, strategy projects.
    • Scheduling speed: Extremely fast (often < 24 hours).
    • Reported average rate: $500 - $1,000+/hr (premium over generalists).
    • Typical realized rates: $300 - $800/hr depending on negotiation.
    • Payment reliability: Excellent (monthly/bi-monthly cycles).
    • Trustpilot/Glassdoor sentiment: High for experts, mixed for employees (high pressure).
    • Best fit: Experts who can handle aggressive vetting for high-paying PE clients.

    3. Market Position and Network Size¶

    • Consistently ranked #2 or #3 expert network globally by revenue and volume.
    • Focuses on "custom recruiting" rather than just database mining.
    • Unlike GLG (database first), AlphaSights associates actively headhunt for specific projects.
    • Known for the "AlphaSights Academy" training for associates, resulting in a very specific, polished, but pushy communication style.
    • Network size is vast but they prioritize "fresh" experts over "stale" database entries.
    • Dominates the Private Equity due diligence market segment.
    • Competes directly with GLG and Dialectica; often considered "higher touch" than GLG.
    • Global footprint with major hubs in London, NYC, Hamburg, Dubai, Hong Kong, San Francisco.

    4. Client Base and Use Cases¶

    • Private Equity (PE): The core engine. PE firms use AlphaSights for "commercial due diligence" (CDD) before buying companies. These calls are high-stakes, high-rate, and require rapid turnaround.
    • Strategy Consulting: McKinsey, Bain, BCG (MBB) are heavy users. They need experts to validate hypotheses for their end-clients.
    • Hedge Funds / Asset Management: Use experts for public market signal checks.
    • Corporate Strategy: Growing segment, but less dominant than PE/Consulting.
    • Use Case: "deal flow" research. Experts are often called when a client is about to spend $100M+ on an acquisition and needs to verify technical or market claims in 48 hours.

    5. Engagement Types and Workflow¶

    • 1:1 Phone Consultations: 90% of the volume. Standard 60-minute calls.
    • Project-Based Work: Occasional longer-term advisory, but rare compared to calls.
    • Surveys: Growing, but less spammy than GLG/Guidepoint. Targeted B2B surveys.
    • Workflow:
      1. Associate finds you (LinkedIn/Database).
      2. Vetting Call: A 10-15 min phone call with the Associate (NOT the client) to "screen" you. Critical Step.
      3. Profile submission to Client.
      4. Client selection.
      5. Scheduling & Compliance.
      6. The Call.
      7. Payment.

    6. Registration and Onboarding Realities¶

    • Inbound vs. Outbound: You can register on their website, but 80%+ of projects start with them finding you (Outbound).
    • The "Vetting Call": unlike GLG (often automated), AlphaSights almost always requires a quick chat with a junior associate to verify your background before presenting you to the client.
    • Registration: https://www.alphasights.com/experts/
    • LinkedIn is Key: Their associates live on LinkedIn Recruiter. Your LinkedIn profile is your application.
    • No Cost: Free to join.

    7. Rate Intelligence (All Reported Ranges)¶

    7.1 Reported Rate Ranges¶

    • Standard Offer: Associates often suggest $150-$300/hr initially to test your floor.
    • Realized "Pro" Rate: Experienced experts typically land $500 - $800/hr.
    • Executive/Niche Rate: C-level or rare technical niche (e.g., specific drug development, obscure industrial control systems) can command $1,000 - $1,500/hr.
    • Repo Data Points:
      • REDDIT_ANALYSIS_SUMMARY_2025.md: "AlphaSights associates have a budget, they try to keep the spread. Push for $500."
      • CODEX_MEDIUM_10K.md: "Don't get out of bed for less than $400 on AlphaSights."

    7.2 The "Margin" Reality¶

    • AlphaSights charges clients heavily ($1,000 - $2,000+ per call).
    • Associates are incentivized on margins in some contexts, or at least on "successful delivery" within budget.
    • There is usually $300-$500/hr of "slack" in their initial offer.

    8. Rate Negotiation Playbook¶

    • The "Anchor" Move: When an associate reaches out, they often ask "What is your standard hourly rate?".
    • Response: "My standard commercial consulting rate is $800/hr." (Adjust based on seniority, but aim high).
    • The Pushback: Associate: "Oh, the budget for this project is tighter, we usually see $300."
    • The Counter: "I understand. However, for this specific topic where I have direct operational experience, I can't go below $500/hr. I can offer a 30-min pro-rated cap if the client isn't satisfied."
    • Leverage: Speed. "I can take the call tomorrow morning at $500."
    • Don't Blink: If you are the right expert, they will pay. They sell access, not price.
    • Retainer/Bulk: Rare for single calls, but applicable if they want 5+ hours.

    9. Payment Reliability and Timing¶

    • Reliability: 10/10. Very corporate, very reliable.
    • Method: Direct Deposit / Wire Transfer.
    • Timing: Monthly or bi-monthly cycles. Usually net 15-30 days after month-end.
    • Currency: Can pay in USD, GBP, EUR, etc.
    • Dashboard: Clear visibility into pending and paid invoices.

    10. Volume and Demand Signals¶

    • High Volume: Top 3 global volume. If you are in a hot industry (Tech, Healthcare, Energy), expect frequent outreach.
    • Project Type: Due diligence is cyclical. End of quarters and end of year are busy for PE deals.
    • Reactive: You can't really "browse" projects. You must wait to be contacted.
    • "Hot" Topics 2025: AI Infrastructure, GLP-1 Drugs, Private Credit, Defense Tech, Cybersecurity (Zero Trust/SASE).

    11. Project Matching Algorithm (The "Human-in-the-Loop" Reality)¶

    • Unlike algorithms that match keywords, AlphaSights relies on Associates (typically young, smart, hungry grads).
    • The Algorithm is a Person: A 24-year-old Associate has a quota to fill 3 expert slots for a McKinsey team by 5 PM.
    • Implication: You need to be responsive and personable to the associate.
    • The "Vetting" Script: They will ask specific screening questions. Answer confidently. If you waffle, they move to the next name on the list.
    • Keyword Matching: They search LinkedIn. Ensure your profile has the specific keywords (e.g., "Kubernetes," "Revenue Cycle Management," "Polymer manufacturing").

    12. Screening Questionnaire Strategy¶

    • AlphaSights often does phone screening rather than just text forms.
    • Tip: Treat the Associate like a client. Sell your expertise to them. They are the gatekeeper.
    • Text Screeners: If sent a written screener, keep it high-level.
      • Bad: "I increased revenue by 12% by changing X, Y, Z specific vendors." (You just gave the answer for free).
      • Good: "I led a revenue transformation project involving vendor consolidation. I can discuss the specific strategic levers and vendor landscape during the call."

    13. Success Strategies: Profile Positioning¶

    • LinkedIn Headline: Must be descriptive. "Former VP of Sales at Snowflake" is better than "Sales Leader."
    • Summary: List specific software, machinery, regulations, or markets you know.
    • "Former" Roles: Extremely valuable. PE firms want to talk to former employees to get the "real dirt" (within compliance limits) on a target company.
    • Consultant Profile: If you have an AlphaSights profile, keep your bio updated with your latest projects.

    14. Success Strategies: Responsiveness and Outreach¶

    • Speed is King: AlphaSights promises clients "experts within hours."
    • The 15-Minute Rule: Reply to an email/InMail within 15 minutes to increase your win rate by 50%.
    • Mobile Friendly: Be ready to schedule calls from your phone.
    • Associate Relationship: If you deliver a great call, the associate will tag you as a "Gold Star" expert and call you first next time.

    15. Success Strategies: Rate and Commercial Discipline¶

    • Set Your Floor: Decide "I won't do calls for less than $X." Stick to it.
    • Raise Rates Annually: Increase your rate by $50-$100 every year or every 5 calls.
    • Contextual Pricing: Charge more for "Niche/Hard" topics vs "General Industry" chat.
    • Cancellation Fees: AlphaSights is usually good about paying if a client cancels < 24 hours (ask for it).

    16. Success Strategies: Call Preparation¶

    • No Deck Needed: 99% of calls are "conversation only." No slides.
    • Mental Prep: Review the 3-4 screening questions. That's the agenda.
    • Environment: Quiet room, good headset. PE clients get annoyed by background noise.
    • Compliance Check: Remind yourself what you cannot say (MNPI, trade secrets).

    17. Success Strategies: Call Execution¶

    • The "Answer First" Pyramid: Start with the conclusion, then explain. Strategy consultants love this.
    • Be Honest about "I Don't Know": It builds credibility. "I can't speak to the Asian market, but in North America..."
    • Guidance: Don't lecture. Let the client drive the questions.
    • Time Management: If the call hits 60 mins, say "I can continue if you need more time, or we can wrap up." (Gets you paid for overage).

    18. Success Strategies: Post-Call and Scaling¶

    • Feedback: Ask the associate "How did the client find the call?"
    • Referrals: "I know someone else who knows about [X], would you like an intro?" (Referral fees are $100-$300).
    • Billing: Submit invoice immediately via their portal.

    19. Compliance and Risk Management¶

    • Strict: AlphaSights has a very robust compliance framework.
    • Training: You must pass their compliance tutorial.
    • MNPI (Material Non-Public Information): strict prohibition.
    • Competitors: You cannot consult on your current employer.
    • Public Companies: Extra scrutiny if you work for a publicly traded firm.

    20. Red Flags and Common Complaints¶

    • "Bait and Switch": Associate offers $500, then comes back saying "Client budget is $300." (Hold firm).
    • Last Minute Cancellations: Happens in M&A deals. Deal dies, call dies.
    • Aggressive Associates: Some can be pushy about scheduling. Be firm with your boundaries.
    • Unpaid Vetting: You don't get paid for the 15-min vetting call with the associate.

    22. Role-Based Playbooks¶

    • The Executive (VP/C-Level): Command $800+. Focus on strategy and market trends. Don't get bogged down in technical weeds unless asked.
    • The Technical Expert (Engineer/Dev): Command $500+. Focus on "implementation reality" vs "sales brochure promises." Clients love hearing why a tech stack failed.
    • The Customer (Procurement/User): Value is in "Why we bought X vs Y." Rate $300-$600.
    • The Competitor (Former Employee): High value. "What is the culture? Sales incentive structure?" (Keep it compliant).

    24. Benchmarks and Metrics¶

    • Win Rate: Aim to convert 30-50% of vetting calls to paid projects.
    • Rate: Aim for top 25% of your industry peer group.
    • Volume: 1-3 calls/month is healthy. 5+ is "Power User."

    25. 30/60/90-Day Action Plan¶

    • Day 1-30: Update LinkedIn. Add keywords. Register on AlphaSights site. Accept first call at offered rate (if >$200) to get "in the system."
    • Day 30-60: deliver 5-star calls. Ask associates for feedback. Raise rate to $400-$500.
    • Day 60-90: Be selective. Turn down low-ball offers. Focus on PE clients. Establish "go-to" status with 2-3 specific associates.

    26. Templates and Scripts¶

    • Rate Negotiation Script: > "Thanks for reaching out. This project aligns perfectly with my experience at [Company]. Given the specialized nature of the request and the tight turnaround, my rate for this engagement is $600/hr. I can prioritize a slot tomorrow morning at 10 AM ET. Does that work for the client?"

    • Vetting Call Script: > "Yes, I managed the P&L for that division. We faced exactly the challenge you mentioned regarding supply chain. I can walk the client through the 3 key mistakes we made and how we fixed them. I'm available Tuesday."

    29. Key Takeaways¶

    • Premium Tier: AlphaSights is a "Tier 1" network. Treat it as such.
    • Negotiate: Do not accept the first rate.
    • Associate Alliance: Your relationship is with the Associate. Make them look good to their client, and they will feed you endless work.
    • Compliance is Key: One slip up and you are banned. Stay safe.
    • Speed Wins: fast replies = money.
