## 4. 2025 High-Value Case Types

To command the highest rates, position yourself as a specialist in these trending areas:

1.  **Data Breach Class Actions (Negligence):**
    *   *The Question:* "Did the defendant implement 'reasonable' security measures (MFA, EDR, Patching) prior to the breach?"
    *   *Your Role:* Compare their security posture to industry standards (NIST, CIS Controls) at the time of the incident.

2.  **Pixel / Tracker Litigation (CIPA, VPPA):**
    *   *The Trend:* Lawsuits against companies using Meta Pixel, TikTok trackers, or chatbots that allegedly "wiretap" user communications.
    *   *Your Role:* Technical forensic analysis of web traffic (HAR files) to prove *what* data was sent, *when*, and to *whom*.

3.  **Biometric Privacy (BIPA):**
    *   *The Trend:* Disputes over facial recognition, fingerprint scanning, and voiceprints.
    *   *Your Role:* Explain how the technology captures, stores, and hashes biometric data.

4.  **AI & Copyright/Training Data:**
    *   *The Future:* Disputes over whether proprietary data was used to train LLMs.
    *   *Your Role:* Data provenance analysis and explaining machine learning pipelines to a judge.

## 5. The "Golden Standard" Expert Report (Template Structure)

Attorneys pay for *clarity*, not just technical prowess. Your income depends on your ability to write a defensible report.

**Structure of a $10,000+ Report:**
1.  **Assignment:** "I was asked to determine if [Company X] followed industry standard practices regarding [Issue]."
2.  **Executive Summary:** A 1-page summary of your opinion in plain English.
3.  **Methodology:** "I utilized the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (v2.0) and CIS Controls (v8) as my benchmarks."
4.  **Analysis:**
    *   *Fact:* "The server was running Windows Server 2012 R2."
    *   *Standard:* "Microsoft ended support for this OS in Oct 2023."
    *   *Opinion:* "Running end-of-life software constituted a failure to maintain reasonable security."
5.  **Conclusion:** Definitive, confident statements based on the evidence.

## 7. Critical Warnings

*   **Conflicts:** Always run a conflict check (Plaintiff vs. Defendant, past vendors/employers) *before* accepting a case.
*   **Discovery:** Anything you write (emails, notes, drafts) is potentially discoverable by the opposing side. **Never write anything you wouldn't want read in court.**
*   **The "Hired Gun" Trap:** Never alter your professional opinion just to please the client. Your reputation is your only asset. If the facts are bad for your client, tell them early (verbally).
