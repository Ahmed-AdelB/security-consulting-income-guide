## 1. Executive Summary: The 2025 Security Compensation Landscape

In 2025, cybersecurity remains one of the highest-paid engineering specializations. Compensation packages have stabilized following the volatility of previous years, with "Total Compensation" (TC) heavily weighted towards stock grants (RSUs) at top-tier tech companies.

**Key Findings:**
*   **Top Tier Standard:** A Senior Security Engineer at a FAANG company now commands a TC of **$400k - $450k+**.
*   **AppSec Premium:** Application Security Engineers continue to see a premium due to the shift-left movement, with senior individual contributors (ICs) clearing **$250k** easily.
*   **CISO Variance:** The CISO role has the highest variance, ranging from **$150k** at smaller firms to **$900k+** at large enterprises.

## 3. Application Security (AppSec) Compensation

AppSec is a critical niche. These engineers bridge the gap between DevOps and Security.

*   **Average TC (All Levels):** ~$219,000
*   **Entry-Level AppSec:** ~$132,000 - $160,000
*   **Senior AppSec Engineer:** ~$259,000 (Range: $185k - $719k)
*   **Top 1% Percentile:** ~$756,000 (Principal/Distinguished level at top tech firms)

**Why the premium?**
AppSec engineers must know how to *code* as well as they know how to *break* code. This dual skillset is rarer than general infrastructure security skills.

## 4. Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) Compensation

CISO compensation is unstructured and highly negotiated. It depends heavily on company size, industry risk profile, and location.

### Compensation Tiers
*   **Tier 1 (Fortune 500 / Big Tech):**
    *   **Total Comp:** $500,000 - $900,000+
    *   **Structure:** High equity component, performance bonuses based on security posture and incident avoidance.
*   **Tier 2 (Mid-Market / Tech Unicorns):**
    *   **Total Comp:** $300,000 - $500,000
    *   **Structure:** Competitive base ($250k+) with significant upside if the company exits (IPO/Acquisition).
*   **Tier 3 (Small/Medium Enterprise):**
    *   **Total Comp:** $150,000 - $250,000
    *   **Structure:** Mostly base salary with standard benefits.

### Influencing Factors
*   **Location:** San Jose and SF Bay Area CISOs earn ~30-50% more than the national average.
*   **Sector:** Finance (JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs) and Tech (Google, Microsoft) pay the most. Retail and Healthcare generally pay less, though this is shifting due to ransomware risks.

## 6. Negotiation Tips for 2025

*   **Focus on TC, not Base:** For L5+ roles, stock is 50% of your money. Negotiate the grant.
*   **Competing Offers:** You *must* have a competing offer to move the needle at FAANG.
*   **Sign-on Bonuses:** Use these to bridge the gap in Year 1 if stock vesting is back-loaded (like Amazon).
