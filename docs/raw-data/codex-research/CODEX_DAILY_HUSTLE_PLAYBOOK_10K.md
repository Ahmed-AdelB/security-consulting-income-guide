## Mission and revenue math
- Primary goal: deliver repeatable, high-trust security outcomes.
- Revenue target: $10,000 per month.
- Baseline pricing: $2,500 per retainer or $200 per hour.
- Capacity target: 12 to 15 billable hours per week.
- Lead target: 6 warm leads per week.
- Conversion target: 2 discovery calls per week.
- Active clients: 3 to 4 at any time.
- Core deliverables: security assessments, controls reviews, advisory.
- Secondary deliverables: training, policy drafting, executive briefings.

## Operating principles
- Guard deep work as the highest-leverage block.
- Communicate in predictable windows to reduce churn.
- Default to written updates and ticketed requests.
- Capture every request into the queue within 10 minutes.
- Say no to scope creep within the same day.
- Ship small, clear deliverables daily.
- Tie every action to a documented risk or outcome.
- Maintain a visible backlog for every client.
- Keep energy high by protecting sleep and movement.

## Client profile and scope boundaries
- Ideal client: SaaS founder or CTO with 10–80 employees.
- Typical needs: security roadmap, cloud hardening, audit readiness.
- Exclusions: 24/7 SOC coverage, malware cleanup, incident forensics.
- Engagement length: 4–12 weeks with weekly milestones.
- Communication: async-first, two live touchpoints per week.
- SLAs: response within 1 business day, priority window for incidents.

## Client communication windows
### Window 1 — 07:00–08:00 (inbound triage)
- Goal: respond to all client messages.
- Rule: never start deep work until inbox is clear.
- Triage tags: urgent, today, this week, backlog.
- Reply template: acknowledge, confirm, timebox.
- Escalation: if urgent, move into 10:00 block.
- Meeting scheduling: book only Tue–Thu 10:30–16:30.
- Status cadence: daily update for active projects.
- Closed-loop: confirm next action in every response.
- SLA: response within 1 business day.
- Scorecard: zero unread by 08:00.

### Window 2 — 16:00–17:00 (status updates)
- Goal: send proactive updates.
- Rule: no new scope added after 16:30.
- Update format: progress, risks, next steps.
- Checkpoint: verify client priorities.
- Deliverable tracking: link to artifacts.
- Calendar hygiene: confirm upcoming calls.
- Escalations: move blockers into next morning.
- Scorecard: all active clients receive update.

### Window 3 — Optional 11:30–12:00 (sales responses)
- Goal: respond to leads and partners.
- Rule: prioritize warm leads over cold.
- Template: quick value statement + call link.
- SLA: respond same day for warm leads.
- Scorecard: 3 touches per day.

## Templates and scripts

- Subject: Daily update — [Client] — [Date]
- Progress: [What moved today]
- Risks/blockers: [Anything slowing progress]
- Next steps: [What happens next]
- Owner: [Who owns next step]
- ETA: [Date/time]

### Quick triage response
- Thanks for the note — I’ve got this queued.
- I’ll review and reply with next steps by [time].
- If this is urgent, please confirm the impact and deadline.

### Scope change response
- This request looks outside our current scope.
- I can add it as a new task with estimate by [time].
- Do you want me to proceed with a separate quote?
