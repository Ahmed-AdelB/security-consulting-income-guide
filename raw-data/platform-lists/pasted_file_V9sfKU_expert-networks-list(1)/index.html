    Comprehensive List of Expert Network Platforms (2025)¶

    Overview¶

    This is a complete list of expert network platforms similar to GLG, Arbolus, and Guidepoint. These platforms connect professionals like you with clients (investors, consulting firms, corporations) who need expert insights.

    Top 10 Major Expert Networks¶

    1. GLG (Gerson Lehrman Group)¶

    • Website: www.glg.com
    • Founded: 1998
    • Network Size: 800,000+ freelance consultants
    • Key Features: Largest expert network globally, pay-as-you-go model, $1,200/hour average rates
    • Registration: Apply directly on website or through LinkedIn outreach
    • Service Types: Phone calls, virtual meetings, surveys, research projects
    • Ideal For: Investors, Fortune 500 companies, consultants

    2. Guidepoint¶

    • Website: www.guidepoint.com
    • Founded: 2009
    • Network Size: 600,000+ advisors from 150+ industries
    • Key Features: Flexible rates, quick scheduling (often within hours), $1,000/hour average rates
    • Registration: Direct application on website
    • Service Types: Expert calls, surveys, panel events
    • Ideal For: Investment professionals, corporate decision-makers

    3. Third Bridge¶

    • Website: www.thirdbridge.com
    • Network Size: Invite-only expert network
    • Key Features: Proprietary expert network, video insights (Canopy), data feeds
    • Registration: Mostly invite-only; can submit interest form at help.thirdbridge.com
    • Service Types: Expert calls, library access, surveys, advisory services
    • Ideal For: Investors and business leaders

    4. AlphaSights¶

    • Website: www.alphasights.com
    • Network Size: 400,000+ verified experts
    • Key Features: Global reach, advanced technology, machine learning matching
    • Registration: Direct application on website
    • Service Types: Expert interviews, surveys, consulting projects
    • Ideal For: Consulting firms, investment banks, corporations

    5. Arbolus¶

    • Website: www.arbolus.com
    • Founded: 2018 (by former AlphaSights managers)
    • Network Size: Growing global network
    • Key Features: AI-driven screening, Canopy video insights, proprietary ArbolusGPT
    • Registration: Direct application on website
    • Service Types: 1-on-1 expert calls, video Q&A, surveys
    • Ideal For: Strategy consultants, private equity, corporate strategy teams

    6. Tegus (AlphaSense Expert Insights)¶

    • Website: www.tegus.com
    • Network Size: 200,000+ transcripts, growing database
    • Key Features: Transparent pricing, self-service model, transcript library (200K+)
    • Registration: Submit request through Contact Us page
    • Service Types: Expert calls, transcript library access
    • Ideal For: Experienced researchers, investment firms

    7. Zintro¶

    • Website: www.zintro.com
    • Network Size: Large global network
    • Key Features: Market research agency, expert network integration
    • Registration: Direct application on website
    • Service Types: Expert consultations, market research
    • Ideal For: Companies needing market research and expert insights

    8. Inex One¶

    • Website: www.inex.one
    • Key Features: Aggregates multiple expert networks in one dashboard, pay-as-you-go, AI transcripts included
    • Registration: Direct sign-up on website
    • Service Types: Expert calls across multiple networks
    • Ideal For: Organizations wanting flexible, on-demand access without retainers

    9. NewtonX¶

    • Website: www.newtonx.com
    • Network Size: 1.1+ billion professionals (via proprietary Knowledge Graph)
    • Key Features: AI-powered expert matching, hyper-targeted recruiting, transcripts included
    • Registration: Create profile at www.newtonx.com/for-professionals/
    • Service Types: Expert calls, research studies, surveys
    • Ideal For: Companies needing niche expert targeting

    10. Capvision¶

    • Website: www.capvision.com

    • Key Features: Global network, strong in Asia, $30,000+ annual for top experts

    How to Register: General Process¶

    Most expert networks follow these steps:

    Step 1: Prepare Your Profile¶

    • Update LinkedIn profile with current role, expertise, and achievements
    • Prepare a professional bio (2-3 sentences)
    • Gather resume/CV
    • List 3-5 areas of expertise
    • Identify industries and companies you know

    Step 2: Apply to Networks¶

    Visit each platform's website and look for: - "Join as Expert" button - "Become an Expert" link - "Apply Now" option - Contact form for expert applications

    Step 3: Complete Application¶

    Typically includes: - Email address - Full name and background - Professional experience - Areas of expertise - CV/resume upload - LinkedIn profile link

    Step 4: Screening Process¶

    • Phone or video interview (15-30 minutes)
    • Discuss your expertise and availability
    • Confirm your hourly rate
    • Answer screening questions

    Step 5: Onboarding¶

    • Review compliance agreements
    • Understand confidentiality requirements
    • Set up your expert profile
    • Indicate availability and hourly rates
    • Complete any training modules

    Step 6: Start Receiving Opportunities¶

    • Check your inbox for project invitations
    • Respond quickly to match with clients
    • Complete project details
    • Schedule calls with clients

    Tips for Registration Success¶

    1. Complete Your Profile: The more detailed, the better. Include:
    2. Specific companies you've worked for
    3. Exact products/services you know
    4. Competitors you understand

    5. Current role and responsibilities

    6. Set Realistic Rates: Typical ranges:

    7. Entry-level/junior: $100-$200/hour
    8. Mid-level/managers: $200-$400/hour

    9. Senior executives: $400-$1,000+/hour

    10. Be Responsive: Networks prioritize experts who respond within hours to opportunities

    11. Keep Profile Updated: Update with new companies, products, roles as your career changes

    12. Join Multiple Networks: There's no penalty for joining multiple platforms simultaneously

    13. Optimize LinkedIn: Networks use LinkedIn heavily to find experts

    14. Use specific keywords in headline
    15. Detail tools/software you know
    16. Add publications and presentations

    17. Get recommendations from colleagues

    18. Be Proactive: If not contacted, reach out to networks via their contact forms

    Important Notes¶

    1. Multiple Network Membership: You can and should join multiple expert networks simultaneously
    2. No Fees: There are no fees to join or maintain expert status
    3. Invite-Only: Some networks are invite-only (like Third Bridge), but you can express interest
    4. Exclusivity: Most networks (except Capvision historically) don't prevent you from joining competitors
    5. Compliance: All require compliance training on confidentiality and insider trading rules
    6. Payment Methods: Vary by network (bank transfer, PayPal, WeChat, Alipay, etc.)

    Next Steps¶

    1. Priority Registration: Start with GLG, Guidepoint, AlphaSights, and Arbolus
    2. Secondary Registration: Add Tegus, Zintro, NewtonX, Inex One
    3. Specialized Networks: Based on your industry, add Capvision (Asia), Coleman (Finance), Experfy (Tech/AI)
    4. Monitor: Check email for project invitations and respond quickly
    5. Rate Negotiation: After 5 calls with positive feedback, request rate increase
