    SOCIAL ENGINEERING CONSULTING GUIDE 2025¶

    Income, Rates, and Market Strategy¶

    1. Executive Summary: The Human Element Market¶

    In 2025, social engineering remains the #1 attack vector for breaches, with 97.5% of attacks relying on some form of human manipulation. As AI-driven phishing and deepfake vishing become commoditized, organizations are shifting budget from purely technical controls to "human patching" and rigorous testing.

    Key Market Stat: The global market for Social Engineering Attack Defense Solutions is projected to reach $9.8 Billion by 2032, growing at a CAGR of 16.5%.

    2. Service Rate Card 2025¶

    Based on 2024-2025 market data for US-based consulting.

    A. Phishing Assessment Services¶

    • Standard Campaign (1-Time): $1,000 - $3,500
      • Includes: 1 template, up to 100 targets, basic reporting (clicks/creds).
    • Advanced Spear-Phishing (1-Time): $3,500 - $8,000
      • Includes: OSINT recon, custom payloads, domain spoofing, executive targeting (Whaling).
    • Annual Managed Phishing (Quarterly): $12,000 - $25,000/year
      • Includes: 4 campaigns/year, progressive difficulty, remediation training integration.
    • Platform Subscription (Reseller Model): $1 - $3 per user/month
      • Note: Consultants often mark up SaaS licenses (e.g., KnowBe4, GoPhish management) by 20-30%.

    B. Vishing (Voice Phishing) Services¶

    • Standard Vishing Campaign: $3,000 - $7,500
      • Scope: 5-10 specific targets (e.g., Help Desk, HR).
      • Deliverable: Call recordings (redacted), outcome report.
    • "Vishing as a Service" (VaaS): $15,000+ Annual Retainer
      • Scope: Continuous testing of new hires and support staff.
    • AI-Enhanced Vishing Test: $5,000+ per engagement
      • New for 2025: Testing employee resilience against deepfake audio/voice cloning.

    C. Comprehensive Social Engineering Pentest¶

    • Remote Only (Email/Phone/SMS): $5,000 - $15,000
    • Full Scope (Physical + Remote): $15,000 - $45,000+
      • Physical: Tailgating, badge cloning, "left media" drops.
      • Remote: Phishing, Vishing, Smishing (SMS).
    • Red Team Operation (Adversary Emulation): $45,000 - $100,000+
      • Scope: Full-spectrum attack simulating a specific threat actor (APT).

    4. 2025 Market Drivers¶

    1. AI & Deepfakes: The barrier to entry for attackers has lowered. "Script kiddies" can now generate perfect English emails and clone voices. Companies are terrified and paying premiums for tests that include these vectors.
    2. Remote/Hybrid Work: The "perimeter" is now the employee's home. Vishing attacks targeting VPN credentials and 2FA bypass are surging.
    3. Regulatory Pressure:
      • SEC Rules: Public companies must disclose material incidents, driving proactive testing.
      • Insurance: Cyber insurance carriers are mandating social engineering tests as a condition for coverage.
    4. Ransomware Initial Access: Most ransomware starts with a phish. Preventing the initial foothold is high value.

    5. Packaging Your Services (The "Bundle" Strategy)¶

    Don't sell "a phishing test." Sell a "Human Risk Reduction Program."

    The "Human Firewall" Package ($25k - $40k/year) 1. Baseline Assessment: Initial phish/vish to establish risk level. 2. Quarterly Simulations: 4 Phishing + 2 Vishing campaigns. 3. OSINT Report: "What can hackers find about your executives online?" 4. Training Workshops: Live or recorded sessions based on assessment failures. 5. Policy Review: Updating the "Acceptable Use" and "Verification" policies.

    6. Tools of the Trade (Low Overhead, High Margin)¶

    • Phishing Frameworks: GoPhish (Free/Open Source), Evilginx2 (MFA Bypass).
    • Vishing: SpoofCard, VoIP setups, Voice Changers (AI-based).
    • OSINT: Maltego, Recon-ng, Shodan, LinkedIn Sales Navigator.
    • Physical: Badge cloners (Proxmark3), Lockpicks, Under-door tools.

    Conclusion: Social Engineering consulting offers one of the highest profit margins in cybersecurity because it relies heavily on skill and creativity rather than expensive enterprise software. A single $15,000 engagement might only require $50 in infrastructure costs.
