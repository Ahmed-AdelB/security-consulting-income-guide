    Executive Summary¶

    • RealCISO is a purpose-built marketplace and delivery platform for virtual CISO services.
    • It connects organizations with vetted security leaders and provides tooling to run engagements.
    • The platform combines discovery, matching, scoping, delivery management, and reporting.
    • Differentiation comes from a data-driven matching engine and outcome-oriented playbooks.
    • Revenue is driven by marketplace fees, subscriptions, and optional managed service layers.
    • Success hinges on trust, measurable security outcomes, and repeatable delivery workflows.

    Platform Overview¶

    Mission¶

    • Make executive-grade security leadership accessible to organizations of any size.
    • Reduce buyer uncertainty through transparent expertise, pricing, and outcomes.
    • Standardize delivery so vCISO services are efficient, repeatable, and scalable.

    Vision¶

    • Become the most trusted marketplace for fractional security leadership.
    • Provide a full-stack operating layer for vCISOs and their clients.
    • Enable resilient security programs for the underserved mid-market.

    Value Proposition¶

    • Clients gain immediate access to seasoned leaders without long-term hiring risk.
    • Providers get qualified demand, reduced sales friction, and delivery infrastructure.
    • Partners gain a scalable channel to bundle security leadership with their offerings.

    Platform Pillars¶

    • Marketplace: discovery, profiles, and vetting of vCISO providers.
    • Matching: data-driven alignment of client needs to provider expertise.
    • Delivery: program management, assessment tools, and reporting templates.
    • Insights: benchmarks, maturity scoring, and risk posture tracking.
    • Commerce: proposals, contracts, billing, and compliance documentation.

    Positioning Statement¶

    • RealCISO is the operating system for modern, fractional security leadership.
    • It blends marketplace matchmaking with robust delivery workflows and governance.
    • It emphasizes measurable outcomes over vanity assessments.

    Primary Customer Segments¶

    • SMB and mid-market organizations lacking full-time security leadership.
    • Regulated startups preparing for compliance or fundraising events.
    • MSPs and technology partners expanding their security portfolios.
    • High-growth firms needing interim leadership during hiring transitions.

    Core Outcomes Delivered¶

    • Faster security program initialization and maturity improvements.
    • Reduced audit and compliance friction through structured deliverables.
    • Improved risk prioritization and board-level visibility.
    • Stronger security governance without hiring a full-time CISO.

    Market Context and Problem Space¶

    Why vCISO Demand Is Rising¶

    • Full-time CISO talent is scarce and expensive for smaller organizations.
    • Regulatory pressure and customer security requirements are increasing.
    • Fundraising and enterprise sales cycles require credible security leadership.
    • Security incidents demand leadership beyond basic IT management.

    Buyer Pain Points¶

    • Difficulty validating vendor expertise and fit.
    • Lack of clarity on what a vCISO engagement delivers.
    • Uncertain pricing and value alignment.
    • Fragmented tooling and ad hoc reporting.

    Provider Pain Points¶

    • High cost of customer acquisition and long sales cycles.
    • Inconsistent client readiness and unclear scope.
    • Administrative overhead for proposals, reporting, and billing.
    • Limited access to industry benchmarks and playbooks.

    Competitive Landscape¶

    • Traditional security consultancies offer expensive project engagements.
    • MSSPs focus on tooling and monitoring rather than leadership.
    • Freelancer marketplaces lack governance and delivery infrastructure.
    • Boutique vCISO firms scale slowly without matching engines or automation.

    Target Personas and Stakeholders¶

    Client Personas¶

    • Founder or CEO seeking executive security guidance.
    • COO or Head of Operations needing compliance support.
    • VP Engineering tasked with security without strategic leadership.
    • CFO or board member demanding risk visibility and cost control.

    Provider Personas¶

    • Former CISOs or security executives offering fractional services.
    • Security leaders building a consulting practice.
    • Boutique firms seeking additional demand channels.
    • Compliance specialists expanding into strategic advisory services.

    Internal Platform Stakeholders¶

    • Marketplace operations team handling vetting and quality assurance.
    • Product team managing workflows, templates, and analytics.
    • Revenue team managing pricing, packaging, and partnerships.
    • Support team ensuring smooth onboarding and delivery success.

    Core Platform Capabilities¶

    Discovery and Marketplace¶

    • Searchable provider catalog with structured profiles and certifications.
    • Filters for industry, company size, frameworks, and service focus.
    • Portfolio highlights, case studies, and outcome summaries.

    Assessment and Intake¶

    • Guided intake for client needs and current security posture.
    • Lightweight maturity survey to pre-score risk and urgency.
    • Document capture for policies, audit artifacts, and incident history.

    Matching and Recommendation¶

    • Algorithmic scoring to shortlist best-fit providers.
    • Human-assisted verification to ensure qualitative fit.
    • Transparent match rationale shown to clients and providers.

    Engagement Management¶

    • Scoped engagements, milestones, and deliverables tracked in-platform.
    • Task boards for security initiatives and policy updates.
    • Communication channels for client-provider collaboration.

    Reporting and Governance¶

    • Executive dashboards with risk heatmaps and KPIs.
    • Quarterly business review templates and board summaries.
    • Compliance mapping to common frameworks and standards.

    Commerce and Billing¶

    • Proposal workflow with scope, timeline, and pricing options.
    • E-signature and contract management support.
    • Subscription management and automated invoicing.

    Analytics and Insights¶

    • Benchmarking against industry peers and maturity levels.
    • Trend analysis across incidents, risk reduction, and coverage.
    • Provider performance metrics and client satisfaction tracking.

    vCISO Marketplace Deep Dive¶

    Provider Types Supported¶

    • Independent fractional CISOs.
    • Boutique advisory firms with multi-consultant delivery.
    • Compliance-focused advisors with governance expertise.
    • Technical security leaders offering strategic roadmapping.

    Provider Profile Structure¶

    • Executive summary and positioning statement.
    • Industry verticals and company size focus.
    • Certifications and frameworks supported.
    • Services offered and engagement models.
    • Delivery approach and communication cadence.
    • Sample deliverables and anonymized artifacts.
    • Availability, time zones, and language coverage.
    • Pricing ranges and minimum engagement terms.

    Verification and Vetting¶

    • Identity verification and background checks.
    • Review of certifications and relevant employment history.
    • Reference checks and sample work evaluation.
    • Code of conduct and service-level expectations.

    Service Catalog Taxonomy¶

    • Security program assessment and roadmap.
    • Policy creation and governance framework.
    • Risk management and third-party risk oversight.
    • Incident response planning and tabletop exercises.
    • Compliance readiness for SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA, and PCI.
    • Security awareness and training programs.
    • Executive reporting and board communication.

    Marketplace Listing Example¶

    • Provider Name: A. Patel, Fractional CISO.
    • Vertical Focus: SaaS, Healthcare, FinTech.
    • Certifications: CISSP, CISM, ISO 27001 Lead Auditor.
    • Engagements: 6 to 18 month retainer, 10 to 20 hours monthly.
    • Typical Outcomes: Compliance readiness, security maturity lift.
    • Starting Rate: $4,000 monthly retainer.

    Ratings and Reputation¶

    • Client feedback after each milestone.
    • Outcome scoring based on delivered roadmap progress.
    • Platform-verified badges for timely delivery.
    • Ongoing monitoring to ensure consistent quality.

    Governance and Enforcement¶

    • Standardized scope templates to avoid mismatch.
    • Complaint resolution workflow with mediation options.
    • Removal policy for providers with repeated issues.
    • Annual re-verification and credential updates.

    Client Matching and Intake Engine¶

    Intake Workflow¶

    • Client selects engagement goals and urgency level.
    • Platform captures organization size, industry, and tech stack.
    • Risk profile gathered via maturity survey and incident history.
    • Budget range and engagement timeline collected.
    • Preferred communication cadence and leadership style indicated.

    Matching Inputs¶

    • Industry fit and regulatory exposure.
    • Desired frameworks and compliance targets.
    • Required leadership experience and board communication skills.
    • Geographic and time zone alignment.
    • Budget compatibility and minimum engagement thresholds.

    Matching Methodology¶

    • Weighted scoring across expertise, fit, and availability.
    • Rule-based exclusions for conflicts of interest.
    • Dynamic adjustments based on provider workload and response time.
    • Human review for high-complexity or regulated clients.

    Match Output¶

    • Shortlist of 3 to 5 providers with rationale.
    • Comparative view of pricing, experience, and focus areas.
    • Suggested engagement model and expected timeline.
    • Option to schedule introductory calls directly in-platform.

    Client Matching Experience¶

    • Transparent explanation of why each provider fits.
    • Ability to adjust preferences and re-run match logic.
    • Guardrails to prevent under-scoping or unrealistic timelines.

    Provider Matching Experience¶

    • New lead summary with priority indicators.
    • Suggested discovery questions based on intake responses.
    • Proposal templates tailored to the clients context.

    Pricing Models and Monetization¶

    Pricing Model Overview¶

    • Subscription retainers for ongoing leadership and governance.
    • Project-based engagements for assessments or compliance readiness.
    • Hourly consulting for advisory or emergency response.
    • Outcome-based pricing for defined deliverables or compliance milestones.

    Subscription Retainer Tiers¶

    • Essentials: 5 to 10 hours per month, lightweight governance.
    • Growth: 10 to 20 hours per month, policy and roadmap support.
    • Regulated: 20 to 40 hours per month, compliance and audit prep.
    • Enterprise: custom scope with executive reporting and program buildout.

    Typical Retainer Range¶

    • Essentials: $2,500 to $5,000 monthly.
    • Growth: $5,000 to $10,000 monthly.
    • Regulated: $10,000 to $20,000 monthly.
    • Enterprise: $20,000+ monthly.

    Project-Based Pricing¶

    • Security maturity assessment: $7,500 to $20,000.
    • Compliance readiness sprint: $15,000 to $40,000.
    • Incident response planning: $10,000 to $25,000.
    • Vendor risk program setup: $12,000 to $30,000.

    Outcome-Based Pricing¶

    • SOC 2 Type I readiness with milestones and evidence collection.
    • ISO 27001 stage 1 readiness and internal audit support.
    • Board-level risk program delivered with defined reporting cadence.

    Platform Monetization¶

    • Marketplace success fee on closed engagements.
    • Subscription fee for premium discovery or analytics tools.
    • Paid add-ons for templates, policy libraries, and risk scoring.
    • Enterprise partnerships with MSPs and compliance vendors.

    Pricing Transparency Principles¶

    • Clear scope boundaries and included deliverables.
    • Visible assumptions, dependencies, and client responsibilities.
    • Optional add-ons clearly priced and scheduled.

    Growth 10 to 20 Governance, vendor risk, training $5k to $10k

    Client Experience Journey¶

    Phase 1: Discovery¶

    • Client completes intake and maturity survey.
    • Platform explains recommended engagement models.
    • Client receives shortlist and provider comparisons.

    Phase 2: Matching¶

    • Introductory calls scheduled with top providers.
    • Provider proposes scope, timeline, and success criteria.
    • Client selects final provider and signs engagement.

    Phase 3: Assessment¶

    • Provider performs risk and compliance assessment.
    • Baseline maturity score and critical gaps documented.
    • Initial roadmap and executive summary delivered.

    Phase 4: Program Build¶

    • Policies, governance structures, and priorities established.
    • Risk register created and ownership assigned.
    • Security roadmap milestones tracked.

    Phase 5: Ongoing Leadership¶

    • Monthly or quarterly reporting for executives and boards.
    • Continuous risk management and roadmap adjustments.
    • Support for audits, vendor security reviews, and incident response.

    Phase 6: Renewal or Transition¶

    • Engagement success metrics reviewed.
    • Options for renewal, transition to full-time CISO, or downgrade.
    • Offboarding checklist and documentation handoff.

    vCISO Experience Journey¶

    Phase 1: Onboarding¶

    • Provider creates profile and submits verification materials.
    • Platform reviews credentials, references, and deliverables.
    • Provider completes tooling and template orientation.

    Phase 2: Lead Intake¶

    • Provider receives matched lead notifications.
    • Intake includes risk profile, goals, and budget range.
    • Provider schedules discovery call and logs notes.

    Phase 3: Proposal and Scope¶

    • Proposal generated using standardized scope templates.
    • Deliverables and milestones agreed with the client.
    • Contract, invoice schedule, and kickoff plan finalized.

    Phase 4: Delivery Management¶

    • Roadmap tasks created and tracked in the platform.
    • Evidence library used for policies and compliance artifacts.
    • Communication cadence and reporting maintained.

    Phase 5: Renewal and Growth¶

    • Provider measures outcomes and presents QBR reports.
    • Upsell opportunities identified based on maturity gains.
    • Referral prompts and case study opportunities offered.

    Marketplace Operations and Governance¶

    Quality Assurance¶

    • Quarterly performance reviews based on client feedback.
    • Delivery milestone audits for consistency.
    • Outcome tracking for program progress and compliance readiness.

    Support and Escalations¶

    • Multi-tier support with response time targets.
    • Dispute resolution workflow with mediation guidance.
    • Escalation playbook for missed deliverables or misalignment.

    Trust and Safety¶

    • Conflict of interest disclosures required for providers.
    • Security standards applied to platform data and communications.
    • Privacy controls for sensitive assessments and evidence.

    Financial Operations¶

    • Payment processing with milestone gating.
    • Refund policy tied to scope completion and timelines.
    • Tax and invoicing workflows for international providers.

    Security, Privacy, and Compliance¶

    Platform Security Controls¶

    • Role-based access control for clients and providers.
    • Least-privilege permissions for sensitive artifacts.
    • Audit logging for access to policies and evidence.
    • Encryption at rest and in transit for stored data.

    Privacy Considerations¶

    • Data minimization in intake forms.
    • Client-controlled sharing of evidence and reports.
    • Optional data deletion and retention policies.

    Compliance Alignment¶

    • Internal mapping to SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA, and PCI.
    • Standardized policy templates and control mappings.
    • Secure record retention and evidence tracking.

    Success Strategies for Providers¶

    Profile Optimization¶

    • Lead with outcomes and tangible metrics.
    • Highlight industry-specific experience and compliance wins.
    • Provide sample deliverables to reduce buyer uncertainty.

    Discovery and Scoping¶

    • Use the intake summary to target key pain points quickly.
    • Align scope with the clients current maturity and budget.
    • Define success metrics in the proposal and repeat them in QBRs.

    Delivery Excellence¶

    • Maintain consistent cadence for updates and reports.
    • Track roadmap progress and remove blockers rapidly.
    • Educate stakeholders on why each control or policy matters.

    Retention and Expansion¶

    • Show measurable improvements in risk posture and readiness.
    • Offer add-on engagements tied to compliance deadlines.
    • Encourage referrals with clear client success stories.

    Success Strategies for Clients¶

    Prepare for Engagement¶

    • Assign an internal security owner to coordinate with the vCISO.
    • Collect existing policies, asset inventories, and audit artifacts.
    • Define business goals and critical data flows up front.

    Drive Outcomes¶

    • Participate in roadmap prioritization and risk tradeoffs.
    • Commit to implementation resources for high-priority items.
    • Use QBRs to align leadership and funding.

    Measure Success¶

    • Track maturity scores and policy adoption milestones.
    • Monitor audit readiness and customer security requirements.
    • Benchmark improvements against industry peers.

    Success Strategies for Platform Operator¶

    Marketplace Liquidity¶

    • Balance supply and demand across industries and regions.
    • Encourage providers to publish availability and minimum engagement size.
    • Promote quick response times for matched leads.

    Quality and Trust¶

    • Enforce standardized deliverables and reporting cadence.
    • Publish anonymized benchmarks to show platform performance.
    • Maintain transparent vetting and verification policies.

    Revenue Optimization¶

    • Bundle premium templates with marketplace subscriptions.
    • Offer white-label options for MSPs and consultancies.
    • Incentivize long-term retainers and annual contracts.

    Product Growth¶

    • Use matching outcomes to refine scoring logic.
    • Launch capability badges for specialty compliance areas.
    • Introduce automation for evidence collection and policy tracking.

    Metrics, KPIs, and Health Signals¶

    Marketplace KPIs¶

    • Provider activation rate and profile completeness.
    • Lead response time and acceptance rate.
    • Match-to-contract conversion rate.
    • Average deal size and retention duration.

    Client KPIs¶

    • Time to first deliverable and roadmap completion rate.
    • Client satisfaction score and net promoter score.
    • Compliance readiness improvement over baseline.

    Provider KPIs¶

    • On-time delivery rate for milestones.
    • Average client retention length.
    • Engagement utilization and hours delivered.

    Platform Health Signals¶

    • Supply-demand balance by industry and region.
    • Dispute rate and average resolution time.
    • Churn rate by pricing tier.

    Go-To-Market Strategy¶

    Ideal Customer Profile¶

    • 50 to 500 employee companies in regulated or high-growth sectors.
    • Startups preparing for SOC 2 or enterprise sales requirements.
    • Organizations lacking security leadership but with clear risk exposure.

    Primary Channels¶

    • Partner referrals from MSPs, compliance consultants, and auditors.
    • Content marketing focused on security leadership and compliance readiness.
    • Sales outreach to founders, COOs, and compliance managers.

    Sales Motion¶

    • Product-led matching for fast discovery.
    • Assisted onboarding for higher-value or regulated clients.
    • Success-based pricing to reduce buyer risk.

    Marketing Assets¶

    • Maturity assessment tools and calculators.
    • Security readiness checklists and board templates.
    • Case studies highlighting measurable outcomes.

    Risks, Challenges, and Mitigations¶

    Risk: Scope Creep¶

    • Mitigation: standardized scopes and milestone-based billing.
    • Mitigation: explicit change request workflow in the platform.

    Risk: Provider Quality Variability¶

    • Mitigation: strict vetting and reference checks.
    • Mitigation: ongoing quality audits and client feedback loops.

    Risk: Client Readiness Gaps¶

    • Mitigation: readiness assessment and onboarding education.
    • Mitigation: highlight internal resource requirements up front.

    Risk: Pricing Compression¶

    • Mitigation: emphasize outcomes and value-based pricing.
    • Mitigation: differentiate with compliance expertise and tooling.

    Roadmap and Future Enhancements¶

    0 to 3 Months¶

    • Expand provider vetting automation and credential verification.
    • Launch improved intake survey and matching transparency.
    • Add standardized proposal templates by industry.

    3 to 6 Months¶

    • Release compliance evidence repository with control mapping.
    • Add benchmarking and maturity scoring dashboards.
    • Introduce team-based delivery for larger engagements.

    6 to 12 Months¶

    • Integrate with GRC tools and ticketing systems.
    • Launch automated policy generation and version tracking.
    • Develop AI-assisted reporting and executive summaries.

    • What is your budget range for vCISO services?
    • What is your target timeline for key outcomes?

    Appendix B: Sample Deliverables Checklist¶

    • Security program charter and scope.
    • Executive risk dashboard and reporting cadence.
    • Policy library aligned to required frameworks.
    • Risk register with prioritized remediation plan.
    • Incident response and business continuity plans.
    • Security awareness training schedule and materials.

    Appendix C: Sample Statement of Work Outline¶

    • Objectives and success metrics.
    • Engagement scope and out-of-scope items.
    • Milestones, deliverables, and timeline.
    • Client responsibilities and dependencies.
    • Fees, payment terms, and termination conditions.

    Appendix D: Example RACI for Security Program¶

    • Responsible: vCISO, IT Security Lead.
    • Accountable: CEO or COO.
