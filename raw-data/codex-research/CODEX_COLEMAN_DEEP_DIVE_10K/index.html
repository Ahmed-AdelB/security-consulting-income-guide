    COLEMAN RESEARCH DEEP DIVE GUIDE¶

    Practical, field-tested guidance for experts who want to earn income by advising investors, consultants, and corporates through the Coleman Research platform. This guide is informational, not legal advice, and uses illustrative examples. Always follow Coleman’s compliance rules and your own employer’s policies.

    Platform Overview¶

    Coleman Research is a global expert network that connects subject matter experts with clients who need fast, targeted insights. The platform is used across investment research, consulting engagements, corporate strategy, and market intelligence. Experts are typically paid hourly for phone calls or virtual consultations, with rates set by the expert and approved by the client.

    Key highlights:

    • Operates in multiple regions and time zones.
    • Focuses on short, high-impact calls rather than long-term consulting.
    • Emphasizes compliance, confidentiality, and avoidance of material non-public information.
    • Enables experts to monetize specialized knowledge without long engagements.
    • Offers a mix of inbound requests and project-based recruiting.

    Who uses it:

    • Hedge funds and asset managers seeking market signals.
    • Management consulting firms needing rapid domain context.
    • Corporate development and strategy teams.
    • Market research firms supporting product or category analysis.

    What experts deliver:

    • Industry context and market dynamics.
    • Competitive landscape and go-to-market practices.
    • Customer behavior, procurement, or channel insights.
    • Operational realities, pricing, and adoption patterns.
    • Technology architecture and vendor selection criteria.

    How Coleman Fits the Expert Network Ecosystem¶

    Coleman Research is one of several expert networks that broker conversations between experts and clients. The model centers on rapid discovery and time-boxed consultations. Compared to traditional consulting, expert networks are:

    • Faster to engage (days instead of weeks).
    • Narrower in scope (one or two questions per call).
    • Focused on current practitioners rather than full project teams.
    • Lower risk when compliance protocols are followed.

    Where Coleman typically sits:

    • Mid-to-large network with strong buy-side coverage.
    • Frequent demand for technology, SaaS, and enterprise themes.
    • Competitive in North America and Europe for expert supply.

    Common alternatives experts may also use:

    • GLG, AlphaSights, Guidepoint, Third Bridge, Dialectica, NewtonX.
    • Many experts participate in more than one network.
    • Rate expectations can vary across networks.

    Expert Network Structure¶

    Core Participants¶

    • Experts: Operators, executives, engineers, product managers, or sales leaders with real-world experience.
    • Clients: Investors, consultants, or corporate teams who request insights.
    • Client Service Teams: Coordinate matches, manage compliance, and schedule calls.
    • Compliance Teams: Enforce policies around MNPI, conflicts, and confidentiality.
    • Project Managers: Run larger research projects with multiple calls.

    How Engagements Work¶

    1. Client submits a request with specific criteria.
    2. Coleman screens expert profiles and outreach starts.
    3. Expert expresses interest and completes a compliance check.
    4. Client approves the expert and confirms rate.
    5. Call is scheduled and completed.
    6. Expert is paid after call approval.

    Typical Engagement Types¶

    • 30–60 minute consultations (most common).
    • Multi-call series for a single project.
    • One-off written responses or short surveys (less common).
    • Advisory boards or longer arrangements (rare).

    Compliance Framework¶

    • Experts must avoid material non-public information (MNPI).
    • Experts must avoid confidential employer data or trade secrets.
    • Experts should not discuss future earnings, undisclosed deals, or internal forecasts.
    • Experts should stick to public information and personal experience.

    Screening and Onboarding¶

    • Initial profile review and verification.
    • Role and tenure validation.
    • Conflict checks against current employers or clients.
    • Agreement to compliance and confidentiality terms.

    Hourly Rates and Compensation¶

    Rate Basics¶

    • Experts set an hourly rate in their profile.
    • Clients can accept or negotiate the rate.
    • Coleman typically pays after the client approves the call.
    • Payment terms vary by region and method.

    Illustrative Rate Ranges (Not Guarantees)¶

    These ranges are broad examples drawn from public anecdotes and industry norms. Actual rates vary significantly by role, demand, and topic.

    • Emerging professionals or niche specialists: $100–$250/hr
    • Mid-level managers or senior ICs: $200–$450/hr
    • Directors and VPs: $300–$700/hr
    • Senior executives and former C-suite: $500–$1,200+/hr

    Factors That Influence Rates¶

    • Scarcity of expertise in a specific niche.
    • Seniority and current vs. former role.
    • Recency of experience (last 12–24 months matters).
    • Complexity and urgency of the project.
    • Geographic location and language needs.
    • Risk profile and compliance restrictions.

    Rate Positioning Tips¶

    • Start with a rate you are comfortable defending.
    • Align to your seniority and uniqueness.
    • Adjust based on demand signals and call volume.
    • Consider time cost: prep + call + admin time.
    • Use a tiered approach for long projects if allowed.

    Payment and Taxes¶

    • Payments are typically made via ACH, PayPal, or wire.
    • Experts may receive 1099 or equivalent forms depending on region.
    • Track income for tax reporting and deductions.

    Example Rate Calculator (Illustrative)¶

    • Target monthly income: $3,000
    • Planned calls per month: 6
    • Average call length: 45 minutes
    • Required hourly rate: $3,000 / (6 * 0.75) = $666/hr

    Use this to back into a rate that meets your goals.

    Tech Sector Focus¶

    Coleman has strong demand for technology sector insights, especially in software, infrastructure, and cloud services. Clients look for practical, recent experience rather than theory.

    High-Demand Sub-Sectors¶

    • Cloud infrastructure and hyperscale services.
    • Enterprise SaaS (CRM, ERP, HRIS, finance).
    • Cybersecurity (endpoint, identity, SIEM, SOC).
    • Data engineering and analytics platforms.
    • Developer tools and DevOps platforms.
    • AI/ML tooling, MLOps, and applied AI workflows.
    • Fintech infrastructure and payments.
    • Healthtech and digital health systems.
    • E-commerce enablement and logistics tech.

    Typical Client Questions¶

    • How do buyers evaluate vendors in this category?
    • What drives product adoption and churn?
    • How do enterprise procurement cycles work?
    • What pricing models are standard and why?
    • Which features are table stakes vs. differentiators?
    • What is the competitive landscape and road map?

    Example Call Topics¶

    • “SaaS renewals in mid-market finance teams.”
    • “Security buyer behavior after a breach.”
    • “Cloud cost optimization and FinOps adoption.”
    • “Migration from legacy ERP to cloud-native.”
    • “Developer toolchain consolidation strategies.”

    Roles That Perform Well¶

    • Product managers with ownership of pricing or roadmap.
    • Sales leaders who know procurement and objections.
    • Engineers or architects with hands-on implementation.
    • Customer success leaders with churn and adoption data.
    • Former executives who can discuss market shifts.

    Demonstrating Relevance¶

    • Highlight recent experience in your last two roles.
    • Use measurable outcomes (ARR growth, adoption rates).
    • List target customers or segments served.
    • Include a focused keywords section in your profile.

    Fast Onboarding Process¶

    Coleman’s onboarding is designed to move quickly. Most experts can go from signup to first call in a few days if their profile is complete.

    Step-by-Step Timeline (Example)¶

    Day 0: Sign up, complete profile, set rate. Day 1: Verification outreach and compliance agreement. Day 2: Initial project invite arrives. Day 3: Accept invite, pass conflict check. Day 4: First call scheduled and completed. Day 7–14: Payment processed after call approval.

    Fast Onboarding Checklist¶

    • Full legal name and accurate contact info.
    • Clear role history with dates and companies.
    • Concise expertise summary (3–5 bullets).
    • Keywords list focused on your sector.
    • Updated resume or LinkedIn if requested.
    • Preferred scheduling windows and time zone.
    • Bank or payment setup ready to go.

    What Slows Experts Down¶

    • Vague or incomplete profile descriptions.
    • Missing compliance documentation.
    • No rate set or unrealistic rate without rationale.
    • Slow responses to project invitations.

    Immediate Wins¶

    • Add concrete numbers (e.g., “managed $30M ARR portfolio”).
    • Call out procurement or buyer-side exposure.
    • Emphasize current or very recent work.

    Success Strategies¶

    Profile Optimization¶

    • Lead with the most in-demand expertise.
    • Use clear, specific keywords for search matching.
    • Describe buyer persona knowledge (CIO, CISO, CFO).
    • List notable projects without confidential details.
    • Keep your summary to 5–7 lines.

    Responsiveness¶

    • Reply within a few hours when possible.
    • Even if you decline, provide a reason.
    • Fast replies increase future invitations.

    Call Preparation¶

    • Re-read the client’s prompt before the call.
    • Prepare 5–8 bullet points of insights.
    • Avoid any confidential or MNPI details.
    • Be ready to provide context, not speculation.

    During the Call¶

    • Start with a 60-second role overview.
    • Ask what the client is trying to decide.
    • Use structured answers: market, buyer, vendor.
    • Offer examples from your experience.
    • Confirm if any topics are off-limits.

    After the Call¶

    • Log notes for your own tracking.
    • Mark follow-up availability if applicable.
    • Update profile keywords based on new topics.

    Building Repeat Business¶

    • Offer to discuss adjacent topics in future calls.
    • Mention if you can introduce another expert.
    • Stay consistent in quality and professionalism.

    Real Earnings Scenarios¶

    These scenarios are illustrative, based on typical call volume and publicly discussed rate ranges. Actual earnings depend on demand, compliance, and availability.

    Scenario A: Part-Time Specialist¶

    • Role: Senior engineer in cybersecurity.
    • Rate: $350/hr.
    • Calls per month: 4.
    • Average length: 45 minutes.
    • Monthly earnings: 4 * 0.75 * $350 = $1,050.
    • Annualized (steady state): ~$12,600.

    Scenario B: High-Demand Director¶

    • Role: Director of product at a SaaS company.
    • Rate: $600/hr.
    • Calls per month: 8.
    • Average length: 60 minutes.
    • Monthly earnings: 8 * 1.0 * $600 = $4,800.
    • Annualized: ~$57,600.

    Scenario C: Former Executive¶

    • Role: Former VP of sales for cloud infrastructure.
    • Rate: $1,000/hr.
    • Calls per month: 6.
    • Average length: 60 minutes.
    • Monthly earnings: $6,000.
    • Annualized: ~$72,000.

    Earnings Reality Check¶

    • Call volume can be lumpy and seasonal.
    • Rate increases often require proven demand.
    • Declining irrelevant projects preserves credibility.
    • Time spent on prep and scheduling reduces net hourly rate.

    How to Increase Earnings¶

    • Build a differentiated profile in a niche.
    • Accept early calls to build history and ratings.
    • Gradually raise your rate after 3–5 calls.
    • Expand expertise list in adjacent areas.

    Compliance and Risk Management¶

    Key Compliance Principles¶

    • Do not disclose MNPI about public companies.
    • Avoid confidential employer information.
    • Use public sources if referencing data.
    • Stick to observations from your role.

    Topics to Avoid¶

    • Non-public revenue or margin details.
    • Undisclosed product roadmaps.
    • Pending acquisitions or partnerships.
    • Internal forecasts or pipeline data.

    Safe Topics¶

    • General market trends and buyer behavior.
    • Public product positioning and features.
    • Common procurement frameworks.
    • Implementation challenges and timelines.

    Red Flags During Calls¶

    • Client asks for “numbers you cannot share.”
    • Client pushes for internal forecasts.
    • Client references confidential documents.

    How to Respond¶

    • State that you cannot discuss confidential data.
    • Redirect to public or generalized examples.
    • Ask to move on to another topic.

    Employer Conflicts¶

    • Check your employer policy on outside consulting.
    • Avoid calls that conflict with current clients.
    • Decline if compliance is unclear.

    Evaluating Invitations¶

    Quick Fit Checklist¶

    • Is the topic within your core expertise?
    • Is your experience recent enough?
    • Any conflicts with your employer or clients?
    • Does the time window work?
    • Is the rate acceptable?

    When to Decline¶

    • The request is too broad or outside your role.
    • Compliance risk is high.
    • The call would overlap with your work hours.
    • You cannot deliver meaningful insights.

    How to Decline Professionally¶

    • Respond quickly.
    • Provide a brief reason.
    • Offer a referral if possible.

    When to Ask for Clarification¶

    • If the request is vague, ask for focus questions.
    • If the company list is missing, ask for categories.
    • If the goal is unclear, ask what decision is being made.

    Negotiation and Rate Positioning¶

    When to Negotiate¶

    • The client requests a lower rate.
    • The project is highly specialized.
    • The call is scheduled outside your normal hours.

    How to Respond¶

    • Emphasize the uniqueness of your expertise.
    • Offer a small discount for multiple calls.
    • Stay within a range that reflects your value.

    Rate Increase Strategy¶

    • Raise rate after consistent demand.
    • Increase in 10–20% steps.
    • Keep a record of acceptance rates.

    Multi-Call Projects¶

    • Consider a package rate if allowed.
    • Ask if a series of calls is planned.
    • Clarify the scope of each session.

    Scheduling and Call Flow¶

    Scheduling Best Practices¶

    • Offer 2–3 time windows in your reply.
    • Include time zone explicitly.
    • Avoid back-to-back calls without breaks.
    • Confirm call duration and any prep needs.

    Pre-Call Checklist¶

    • Confirm compliance agreement.
    • Review the client prompt carefully.
    • Prepare structured notes.
    • Ensure a quiet environment.

    Recommended Call Structure¶

    1. Brief introduction and role summary.
    2. Client goal clarification.
    3. Deep dive into 3–5 key topics.
    4. Summary and key takeaways.
    5. Offer follow-up availability.

    Post-Call Actions¶

    • Track call details and topics.
    • Note rate, length, and any feedback.
    • Update profile if new topics emerge.

    FAQ¶

    How do I get more invitations?¶

    • Improve profile keywords and clarity.
    • Respond quickly to all outreach.
    • Take early calls to build history.

    Can I participate if I am currently employed?¶

    • Many experts do, but check your employer policies.
    • Decline any requests that involve confidential info.

    How long until I get paid?¶

    • Typically after call approval; timelines vary by region.

    Can I refuse to answer a question during a call?¶

    • Yes. You should refuse any confidential or MNPI topics.

    Is there a minimum number of calls?¶

    • No. Participation is optional and flexible.

    Glossary¶

    • Expert Network: A platform matching experts with clients for short consultations.
    • MNPI: Material Non-Public Information.
    • Client: The organization paying for the call.
    • Project: A group of calls around a theme.
    • Screening: Compliance and fit validation before a call.
    • Rate: The hourly fee set by the expert.

    • [Day, Time Range, Time Zone]
    • [Day, Time Range, Time Zone]

    Hi [Name],

    Thanks for considering me. The topic is outside my current expertise, so I’ll pass. If helpful, I can suggest another expert with relevant experience.

    Best, [Your Name]

    I’m happy to discuss market dynamics and my experience, but I can’t share confidential or non-public information. Let’s focus on publicly known data and general practices.

    • Rate increases every 6–10 calls when demand is strong.

    Common Client Personas¶

    • Buy-side analyst evaluating a vendor.
    • Consultant mapping the competitive landscape.
    • Corporate team benchmarking vendors.
    • Market research firm seeking adoption data.

    High-Value Insights to Share¶

    • Decision criteria in enterprise evaluations.
    • Common objections and how vendors respond.
    • Deployment timelines and resource requirements.
    • Integration challenges with legacy systems.

    Sources You Can Reference Safely¶

    • Public earnings calls and investor presentations.
    • Vendor documentation and public pricing pages.
    • Industry conferences and public webinars.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid¶

    • Overstating your role or responsibility.
    • Overpromising on niche topics without depth.
    • Sharing internal metrics or pipeline data.
    • Accepting too many calls and burning out.

    Tools and Setup¶

    Workspace Preparation¶

    • Quiet room and reliable headset.
    • Calendar with time zone support.
    • Notes template for quick capture.

    Productivity Tips¶

    • Batch calls on certain days.
    • Use a standard intro and wrap-up.
    • Maintain a topic glossary for quick reference.

    Earnings Optimization Playbook¶

    Phase 1: Establish Credibility (Calls 1–5)¶

    • Accept clear-fit projects quickly.
    • Keep rate reasonable to build history.
    • Capture positive feedback and reuse it.

    Phase 2: Increase Leverage (Calls 6–15)¶

    • Raise rate by 10–15%.
    • Decline low-fit requests.
    • Expand keyword tags with confirmed topics.

    Phase 3: Specialize (Calls 16+)¶

    • Focus on high-demand topics.
    • Build repeat client relationships.
    • Consider package or series engagements.

    Deep Dive: Expert Profile Checklist¶

    • 3–5 bullet summary of key achievements.
    • Recent roles highlighted, older roles summarized.
    • Distinct keywords for tools, markets, and roles.
    • Explicit mention of buyer-side experience.
    • Rate aligned to seniority and demand.

    • Experts who deliver crisp, structured insights are rebooked.

    • Can I set my own rate? Yes, within platform guidelines.
    • Can I decline calls? Yes, always optional.
    • How fast is payment? Varies; expect several days to weeks.

    • Avoid speculation; use experience-based answers.
    • Do not record calls unless explicitly allowed.

    • Use generalized examples when in doubt.

    Protecting Your Professional Brand¶

    • Keep your LinkedIn consistent with profile claims.
    • Avoid exaggerating your scope of responsibility.
    • Maintain confidentiality as a core value.

    • Respond quickly and professionally.
    • Suggest a more relevant expertise area.
    • Offer to revisit if the topic shifts.

    Time Management Playbook¶

    • Block expert calls on fixed calendar days.
    • Keep a 15-minute buffer before and after calls.
    • Track total prep time to evaluate net rate.

    Taxes and Administration (General)¶

    • Track gross income and payment dates.
    • Record expenses like phone, internet, and office supplies.
    • Consult a tax professional for deductions.

    • Publish public content in your domain.
    • Speak at webinars or conferences.
    • Update your profile with new roles quickly.

    Safety-First Reminders¶

    • If unsure, decline or ask for compliance guidance.
    • Do not share confidential customer names without permission.
    • Keep personal notes private and secure.

    • Add adjacent topics to grow invite volume.
    • Offer multi-call support for complex projects.
    • Increase rate if acceptance remains strong.
