    Referral Network Building Guide¶

    How to Use This Guide¶

    • Read the overview and clarify your positioning.
    • Build your referral offer and compliance policy first.
    • Choose one partner type to start with.
    • Use the email and LinkedIn templates as scripts, not spam.
    • Track every interaction and run weekly follow-ups.
    • Iterate based on response rates and referral conversions.

    Outcomes You Are Building Toward¶

    • 10 to 20 active referral partners.
    • 3 to 5 warm introductions per month.
    • 1 to 3 qualified projects per month.
    • Repeatable partner onboarding and reporting.
    • A reputation for low-friction, high-trust referrals.

    Core Principles¶

    • Trust is the product, referrals are the byproduct.
    • Make the partner look good in every interaction.
    • Reduce risk for the referrer, not just the client.
    • Consistency beats volume in relationship building.
    • Clarity beats hype in positioning and messaging.

    Target Partner Types¶

    • Law firms and practice groups.
    • VC funds and platform teams.
    • Other consultants and agencies.

    Your Core Value Proposition¶

    • One sentence describing the problem you solve.
    • One sentence describing the outcome you deliver.
    • One sentence describing who you do it for.
    • One sentence describing your differentiator.
    • A short list of proof points and case studies.

    Positioning Statement Template¶

    • I help [ICP] achieve [outcome] by [method].
    • Typical engagements are [scope] and last [duration].
    • Clients choose me because [differentiator].
    • Best fit clients are [qualities].
    • Not a fit when [exclusions].

    Referral Offer Design¶

    • Define which services are referral-ready.
    • Define who is a qualified lead.
    • Define minimum budget or retainer level.
    • Define intake criteria and red flags.
    • Define your response and follow-up SLA.

    Referral Incentive Options¶

    • No-fee reciprocity and shared content.
    • Fixed referral fee per closed engagement.
    • Percentage-based referral fee by project value.
    • Donations to charity in referrer name.
    • Co-marketing opportunities and co-selling.

    Legal and Ethical Notes¶

    • Check local bar rules for legal referrals.
    • Clarify conflict checks for law firms.
    • Avoid pay-to-play in VC relationships.
    • Use written consent for sharing confidential data.
    • Keep a clear record of introductions and fees.

    Referral Experience Standards¶

    • Reply to new intros within 24 hours.
    • Share a short confirmation to the referrer.
    • Provide a simple, two-step intake process.
    • Provide monthly status updates.
    • Close the loop with outcomes and gratitude.

    Assets to Prepare¶

    • One-page capability overview.
    • Two short case studies with results.
    • A simple intake form or Typeform.
    • A referral tracking sheet or CRM pipeline.
    • An FAQ for partners and their clients.

    One-Page Overview Structure¶

    • Headline: outcome and audience.
    • Short summary of services.
    • Three proof points or metrics.
    • Case study snapshot with results.
    • Call-to-action for intro call.

    Partner FAQ Topics¶

    • What problems do you solve.
    • Which clients are a good fit.
    • What budgets you typically handle.
    • How you run onboarding and delivery.
    • How you communicate with referrers.

    CRM Fields for Referral Tracking¶

    • Partner name and organization.
    • Partner type and practice area.
    • Source channel and warm intro source.
    • Referral date and contact details.
    • Qualified status and stage.
    • Estimated project value.
    • Outcome and date closed.
    • Notes and follow-up schedule.

    Pipeline Stages¶

    • Identify potential partner.
    • Initial outreach sent.
    • First call scheduled.
    • Relationship active and nurtured.
    • Referral received and qualified.
    • Engagement closed and delivered.
    • Relationship review and expansion.

    Weekly Relationship Cadence¶

    • Monday: new partner outreach.
    • Tuesday: follow-ups and check-ins.
    • Wednesday: send value-add content.
    • Thursday: schedule coffee chats.
    • Friday: update CRM and notes.

    Monthly Relationship Cadence¶

    • Share one insight or market update.
    • Host or attend one partner event.
    • Review pipeline metrics and conversions.
    • Send thank-you notes to active referrers.
    • Ask for feedback on client experience.

    Law Firm Referral Playbook¶

    Why Law Firms Refer¶

    • Clients need help beyond legal counsel.
    • Firms want to expand client value.
    • Legal teams avoid operational execution risk.
    • Firms want deeper client retention.

    Ideal Practice Areas¶

    • Corporate and transactional teams.
    • Mergers and acquisitions groups.
    • Employment and HR teams.
    • Data privacy and security teams.
    • IP and licensing practices.

    Common Referral Use Cases¶

    • Post-merger integration support.
    • Compliance program implementation.
    • Vendor selection and negotiation support.
    • Go-to-market or pricing strategy.
    • Operational scaling for growing clients.

    Law Firm Risk Concerns¶

    • Conflict of interest with existing clients.
    • Client dissatisfaction harming the firm.
    • Fees and value perception of consultants.
    • Confidentiality and data handling.
    • Slow response times or poor follow-through.

    How to Reduce Law Firm Risk¶

    • Offer conflict checks and clear boundaries.
    • Provide engagement summaries for clients.
    • Share a clear methodology and timeline.
    • Propose limited-scope pilots.
    • Provide clear communication guidelines.

    Law Firm Outreach Targets¶

    • Partners leading high-growth client accounts.
    • Practice group leaders and BD teams.
    • Senior associates with client exposure.
    • Alumni networks and prior clients.
    • Firm-sponsored events and webinars.

    Law Firm First Call Agenda¶

    • 5 minutes: introductions and context.
    • 10 minutes: practice group focus and priorities.
    • 10 minutes: recent client needs and gaps.
    • 5 minutes: explain your offer and cases.
    • 5 minutes: agree on next steps.

    Law Firm Value-Add Ideas¶

    • Client risk and readiness assessment.
    • Whitepaper co-authored with the firm.
    • Joint client workshop on change management.
    • Implementation playbook for a new law.
    • Executive briefing on market trends.

    VC Fund Referral Playbook¶

    Why VC Funds Refer¶

    • Portfolio companies need hands-on help.
    • Funds want higher retention and exits.
    • Partners want trusted operators in their network.
    • Platform teams need reliable service partners.

    Ideal VC Entry Points¶

    • Platform and portfolio services leaders.
    • Venture partners focused on operations.
    • Partners with hands-on portfolio engagement.
    • Fund community managers.
    • Sector-focused funds with operator support.

    Common VC Referral Use Cases¶

    • Revenue operations setup and scale.
    • Hiring, org design, or leadership coaching.
    • Pricing and packaging redesign.
    • Sales process definition and enablement.
    • Post-fundraise execution planning.

    VC Risk Concerns¶

    • Advisors who waste founder time.
    • Conflicts with existing advisors.
    • Lack of startup operating empathy.
    • Poor measurement of outcomes.
    • Too much selling or upselling.

    How to Reduce VC Risk¶

    • Offer a clear diagnostic and quick wins.
    • Provide founder-friendly engagement scopes.
    • Use startup-aware language and metrics.
    • Share pricing transparency and flexibility.
    • Deliver short monthly outcome updates.

    VC Value-Add Ideas¶

    • Monthly portfolio office hours.
    • Playbooks for common scaling problems.
    • Workshops on pricing, GTM, or hiring.
    • KPI dashboards for early-stage teams.
    • Founder peer roundtables.

    Consultant and Agency Referral Playbook¶

    Why Consultants Refer¶

    • Capacity limits or specialist needs.
    • Service gaps in their current offering.
    • Need trusted partners for overflow work.
    • Desire for reciprocal referrals.

    Ideal Consultant Targets¶

    • Adjacent service providers.
    • Complementary industry experts.
    • Boutique agencies with premium clients.
    • Fractional executives and advisors.
    • Prior colleagues in consulting roles.

    Consultant Risk Concerns¶

    • Losing the client relationship.
    • Inconsistent quality or timelines.
    • Misaligned pricing or scope.
    • Poor communication during engagement.
    • Brand and reputation risk.

    How to Reduce Consultant Risk¶

    • Offer white-label delivery options.
    • Provide clear scopes and governance.
    • Commit to regular status reporting.
    • Agree on client communication rules.
    • Offer co-delivery with shared meetings.

    Consultant Value-Add Ideas¶

    • Joint service packages and co-marketing.
    • Cross-training workshops for teams.
    • Shared templates and tools.
    • Case study co-authorship.
    • Partner referral sprints.

    Relationship Mapping¶

    • Start with your existing client list.
    • Map key stakeholders and their advisors.
    • Identify shared alumni, schools, and events.
    • Tag contacts by influence and referral potential.
    • Build a warm intro map before outreach.

    Warm Intro Ask Framework¶

    • Be specific about the target partner.
    • Provide a short context message for forwarding.
    • Explain the mutual benefit clearly.
    • Make it easy for them to decline.
    • Follow up with gratitude regardless.

    Warm Intro Request Template¶

    Subject: Quick intro request to [Partner Name] Hi [Name], I hope you are doing well. I am reconnecting with [partner type] who support [client type]. Would you be comfortable introducing me to [Partner Name] at [Firm]? I put a short blurb below if that helps. If now is not a good time, no worries at all. Thanks for considering. Best, [Your Name]

    Forwardable Blurb Hi [Partner Name], I wanted to connect you with [Your Name]. They help [ICP] achieve [outcome]. I thought this could be helpful for your clients or portfolio. If you are open, I will let you two take it from here.

    Outreach Channel Mix¶

    • Email for structured, professional outreach.
    • LinkedIn for softer relationship-building.
    • Events for social proof and trust-building.
    • Referrals for high-conversion introductions.
    • Content to maintain top-of-mind awareness.

    Email Outreach Principles¶

    • Keep the message to six sentences or fewer.
    • Lead with their context, not your biography.
    • Use a clear and specific ask.
    • Mention a relevant trigger or mutual contact.
    • Close with a simple yes or no next step.

    Email Template: Law Firm Intro¶

    Subject: Supporting [Firm] clients with [Outcome] Hi [Name], I work with [client type] on [problem] after [trigger]. I noticed your team supports clients in [practice area]. If helpful, I can offer a short diagnostic for your clients. Would you be open to a 20-minute intro call next week? Thanks, [Your Name]

    Email Template: Law Firm Follow-Up¶

    Subject: Re: Supporting [Firm] clients with [Outcome] Hi [Name], Just circling back in case this is timely. I can share a 1-page overview and two short cases. If now is not ideal, I am happy to revisit later. Is there a better person on your team to speak with? Best, [Your Name]

    Email Template: VC Platform Intro¶

    Subject: Operator support for [Fund] portfolio teams Hi [Name], I help founders improve [area] during [stage]. I saw [Fund] invests in [sector], and thought this could help. I can offer a lightweight assessment for portfolio companies. Would it be useful to schedule a brief intro? Best, [Your Name]

    Email Template: VC Platform Follow-Up¶

    Subject: Re: Operator support for [Fund] portfolio teams Hi [Name], Following up in case you want a quick overview. I can also host office hours for a few portfolio teams. If you are open, I can share suggested topics. Would next week work for a short call? Thanks, [Your Name]

    Email Template: Consultant Partnership Intro¶

    Subject: Possible partner on [adjacent service] Hi [Name], I specialize in [service] for [client type]. I noticed you support clients in [adjacent area]. I would like to explore a referral or co-delivery model. Open to a short call to compare notes? Best, [Your Name]

    Email Template: Consultant Follow-Up¶

    Subject: Re: Possible partner on [adjacent service] Hi [Name], Just checking in. I can share a brief summary of where we could collaborate. If someone else handles partnerships, I can reach out to them. Would next week work for a quick chat? Thanks, [Your Name]

    Email Template: Referral Request to Active Partner¶

    Subject: Referral check-in for [Month] Hi [Name], I wanted to share a quick update on recent client outcomes. We helped [client type] achieve [result]. If you have any clients facing [problem], I would be glad to help. No pressure, just staying connected. Best, [Your Name]

    Email Template: Intro Thank You¶

    Subject: Thank you for the intro Hi [Name], Thanks for the introduction to [Prospect]. I will keep you posted on next steps. If I can support you in return, please let me know. Appreciate it, [Your Name]

    Email Template: Closed-Won Update¶

    Subject: Update on [Client] intro Hi [Name], We moved forward with [Client], and we are starting soon. Thanks again for the introduction. I will share a short summary once we deliver outcomes. Best, [Your Name]

    Email Template: Closed-Lost Update¶

    Subject: Update on [Client] intro Hi [Name], We spoke with [Client], but timing was not right. Thanks again for the introduction. I will stay in touch if circumstances change. Best, [Your Name]

    LinkedIn Profile Optimization¶

    • Headline focused on outcome and audience.
    • About section with proof points and services.
    • Featured section with one-page overview.
    • Recent posts that demonstrate expertise.
    • Clear call-to-action in contact info.

    LinkedIn Connection Request¶

    Hi [Name], I work with [client type] on [problem]. I noticed your work at [Firm] and would like to connect. Thanks, [Your Name]

    LinkedIn Follow-Up DM 1¶

    Hi [Name], Thanks for connecting. I help [client type] with [outcome]. If you ever need support for clients in that area, I am happy to help. No rush, just wanted to introduce myself.

    LinkedIn Follow-Up DM 2¶

    Hi [Name], Quick follow-up with a relevant resource on [topic]. Here is a short summary: [one sentence]. Happy to chat if it is useful.

    LinkedIn Follow-Up DM 3¶

    Hi [Name], I am hosting a short session on [topic] for [audience]. If you have clients or portfolio teams who would benefit, happy to share details. No pressure either way.

    LinkedIn Content Ideas¶

    • Short case studies with outcomes.
    • Lessons learned from recent projects.
    • Market insights for partner audiences.
    • Templates or checklists that save time.
    • Founder or executive pitfalls and fixes.

    Relationship First Call Script¶

    • Start with appreciation for their time.
    • Ask about their client or portfolio focus.
    • Share a relevant, concise case study.
    • Offer a low-risk pilot or diagnostic.
    • Agree on the next mutual action.

    Discovery Questions for Partners¶

    • What are the most common client challenges you see?
    • Where do your clients get stuck after your work?
    • What is a successful referral experience for you?
    • What makes a partner unreliable in your view?
    • How would you like updates on referred work?

    Partner Nurture Touchpoints¶

    • Share quarterly insights and trends.
    • Congratulate them on firm or fund wins.
    • Provide introductions to helpful contacts.
    • Invite them to events or webinars.
    • Send a handwritten note once a year.

    Event Strategy¶

    • Attend legal, VC, and industry conferences.
    • Prioritize smaller, curated events for depth.
    • Offer to speak or host workshops.
    • Follow up within 48 hours of meeting.
    • Add notes to CRM immediately.

    Simple Partnership Agreement Template¶

    • Parties and roles.
    • Scope of referrals and services.
    • Confidentiality and data handling.
    • Referral fee terms and payment timeline.
    • Conflict resolution and termination terms.

    Service Intake Checklist¶

    • Client contact details and context.
    • Primary goals and desired outcomes.
    • Budget range and timeline expectations.
    • Stakeholders and decision makers.
    • Known constraints and risks.

    Client Onboarding Workflow¶

    • Intro email to the client and referrer.
    • Discovery call scheduled within 3 days.
    • Proposal or scope document shared.
    • Kickoff call and project plan.
    • Monthly updates to referrer.

    Update Cadence to Referrers¶

    • First update after discovery call.
    • Second update after proposal delivery.
    • Monthly progress updates thereafter.
    • Final outcome summary and next steps.

    Gratitude and Reciprocity¶

    • Send a thank-you note after each intro.
    • Offer a reciprocal introduction quarterly.
    • Share one helpful resource monthly.
    • Invite them to a private briefing.

    Metrics to Track¶

    • Outreach attempts by channel.
    • Response rate by partner type.
    • Call-to-referral conversion rate.
    • Referral-to-closed conversion rate.
    • Average project value from referrals.

    Red Flags to Avoid¶

    • Partners who do not respect boundaries.
    • Introductions without clear need.
    • Requests for excessive discounts.
    • Conflicts that complicate delivery.
    • Low-trust behavior or poor follow-up.

    Law Firm Email Sequence¶

    Sequence Goal¶

    • Book an intro call.
    • Offer a client-facing diagnostic.

    Email 1¶

    Subject: Helping [Firm] clients with [Outcome] Hi [Name], I help [client type] with [problem] after [trigger]. If helpful, I can share a short diagnostic for your clients. Would you be open to a quick call next week? Thanks, [Your Name]

    Email 2¶

    Subject: Re: Helping [Firm] clients with [Outcome] Hi [Name], Sharing a one-page overview with two short case studies. If you prefer, I can send a short video walkthrough. Would you like me to send it? Best, [Your Name]

    Email 3¶

    Subject: Quick question about client support Hi [Name], If you do not handle referrals, who should I contact? I want to make sure I speak with the right person. Thanks, [Your Name]

    VC Fund Email Sequence¶

    Sequence Goal¶

    • Offer portfolio office hours.
    • Align on portfolio needs.

    Email 1¶

    Subject: Portfolio support for [Fund] Hi [Name], I help founders with [problem] at [stage]. I could host office hours for a few portfolio teams. Open to a brief intro call? Thanks, [Your Name]

    Email 2¶

    Subject: Re: Portfolio support for [Fund] Hi [Name], Here are two short examples of outcomes: - [Metric or result]. - [Metric or result]. Happy to share more if useful. Best, [Your Name]

    Email 3¶

    Subject: Should I follow up later? Hi [Name], Let me know if this is better to revisit next quarter. I can also speak with whoever manages platform partnerships. Thanks, [Your Name]

    Consultant Email Sequence¶

    Sequence Goal¶

    • Identify complementary services.
    • Propose co-delivery or referral swap.

    Email 1¶

    Subject: Exploring a referral partnership Hi [Name], I work on [service] for [client type]. I think there is a nice overlap with your work in [area]. Would you be open to a short call? Best, [Your Name]

    Email 2¶

    Subject: Re: Exploring a referral partnership Hi [Name], I can share a brief collaboration outline. If someone else handles partnerships, I can reach out to them. Thanks, [Your Name]

    Email 3¶

    Subject: Any interest in collaborating? Hi [Name], I will close the loop if now is not a priority. Happy to reconnect later if helpful. Best, [Your Name]

    Step 2: Short Value Add¶

    Hi [Name], Thanks for connecting. I recently published a short guide on [topic]. Happy to share if useful.

    Step 3: Soft Ask¶

    Hi [Name], If you ever see clients needing help with [problem], I would be glad to assist. No pressure, just staying connected.

    LinkedIn Sequence for VC Funds¶

    Step 1: Connect¶

    Hi [Name], I support founders on [topic] at [stage]. Your work at [Fund] caught my attention. Would love to connect.

    Step 2: Office Hours Invite¶

    Hi [Name], I host office hours on [topic] for a few portfolio teams. If you ever want an extra operator resource, happy to help.

    Step 3: Portfolio Resource¶

    Hi [Name], Sharing a short checklist on [topic]. Happy to tailor it for your portfolio.

    LinkedIn Sequence for Consultants¶

    Step 1: Connect¶

    Hi [Name], I do [service] for [client type]. I noticed your focus on [area] and would like to connect.

    Step 2: Collaboration Note¶

    Hi [Name], If you ever need support on [service], I can help. Likewise, happy to refer clients who need [their service].

    Step 3: Ask for Call¶

    Hi [Name], Open to a short call to compare notes on collaboration? No pressure if not.

    Objection Handling¶

    Objection: We already have partners¶

    • Ask how they choose partners.
    • Offer a backup or overflow option.
    • Provide a specific case study.

    Objection: No budget for referrals¶

    • Offer a small pilot or diagnostic.
    • Emphasize time-saving and risk reduction.
    • Provide a low-cost workshop.

    Objection: Timing is not right¶

    • Ask when to follow up.
    • Offer a quick resource now.
    • Add a reminder in your CRM.

    Referral Quality Qualification¶

    • Is the client facing a real, urgent problem?
    • Do they have budget and decision authority?
    • Is the project within your expertise?
    • Are timelines realistic and mutual?
    • Is the partner aligned on expectations?

    Delivery Playbook for Referred Clients¶

    • Prioritize referred clients for response time.
    • Over-communicate in the first two weeks.
    • Share a clear, outcome-focused plan.
    • Ask for feedback during delivery.
    • End with a crisp outcome summary.

    Outcome Summary Template¶

    Subject: Outcome summary for [Client] Hi [Partner Name], We completed [scope] for [Client]. Key outcomes: - [Outcome 1] - [Outcome 2] - [Outcome 3] Appreciate the introduction and support. Best, [Your Name]

    Co-Marketing Ideas¶

    • Joint webinar on a shared topic.
    • Guest article in a partner newsletter.
    • Co-branded checklist or toolkit.
    • Shared client panel or roundtable.
    • Joint case study with permission.

    Thought Leadership Topics by Partner Type¶

    Law Firms¶

    • Operationalizing compliance programs.
    • Post-merger integration pitfalls.
    • Managing vendor risk in regulated industries.

    VC Funds¶

    • Pricing strategy for growth-stage startups.
    • Scaling revenue operations from Series A to C.
    • Hiring plan and org design for rapid growth.

    Consultants¶

    • Integrating analytics into strategy projects.
    • Change management for digital transformations.
    • Building cross-functional execution plans.

    CRM Reminder Rules¶

    • New outreach follow-up in 4 days.
    • Post-call follow-up in 24 hours.
    • Partner nurture touchpoint every 30 days.
    • Closed-won thank-you within 48 hours.
    • Quarterly relationship review scheduling.

    Partner Relationship Score¶

    • Responsiveness and engagement.
    • Quality of referrals.
    • Reciprocity and collaboration level.
    • Strategic fit with your services.
    • Growth potential and network reach.

    Referral Fee Guidelines¶

    • Define fees only after legal review.
    • Use written agreements and invoices.
    • Tie fees to closed and paid engagements.
    • Document the referral source and date.
    • Keep receipts and payment confirmations.

    Partner Onboarding Checklist¶

    • Share your one-page overview.
    • Review referral criteria and fit.
    • Agree on communication cadence.
    • Provide case study examples.
    • Clarify confidentiality expectations.

    Examples of Referral Offers¶

    • Diagnostic assessment for their clients.
    • Short workshop for their portfolio.
    • Executive briefing on market changes.
    • Vendor selection support.
    • Growth plan or roadmap workshop.

    30-Day Plan¶

    • Finalize your positioning statement.
