    2. Retainer Fundamentals¶

    • Retainer = prepaid, recurring fee for access plus defined monthly scope.
    • Retainers are common for vCISO, expert witness, DFIR readiness, privacy DPO, and security awareness management.
    • Retainers stabilize cash flow and reduce collection risk.
    • Retainers give clients predictable availability and response windows.
    • Retainers should define included hours or deliverables.
    • Retainers should specify cadence (weekly sync, monthly report, quarterly review).
    • Retainers should specify response windows (business hours vs 24/7).
    • Retainers should separate baseline scope from surge response.
    • Evergreen retainers auto-replenish when balance dips below threshold.
    • Retainer replenishment thresholds should be explicit.
    • Unused hours policy must be clear (carry-over vs expire).
    • Overage rates must be priced in advance.
    • Retainers should include change control for scope expansion.
    • Retainers should include termination notice (30-60 days common).
    • Retainers should include payment timing (Net 15/Net 30).
    • Retainers should include data handling and confidentiality terms.
    • Retainers are not ideal for one-off, fully defined deliverables.

    3. Engagement Models and Fit¶

    3.1 Common Engagement Models¶

    Model Best For Pricing Style Notes
    Hourly / T&M Open-ended advisory Hourly rate Cap hours or set weekly max
    Fixed-Fee Project Defined deliverable Milestone fee Use change orders for new scope
    Retainer Ongoing governance Monthly fee Define hours, cadence, availability
    Hybrid Retainer + Surge Readiness + emergency response Monthly + premium hourly Ideal for DFIR
    Subscription Pentest Continuous testing Quarterly/monthly fee Include retest window

    3.2 Fit Signals for Retainers¶

    • Client has recurring compliance deadlines (SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR).
    • Client needs leadership guidance but not a full-time CISO.
    • Work is cyclical (quarterly testing, monthly training).
    • Client values guaranteed availability and fast response.
    • Consultant wants predictable utilization and reduced sales overhead.

    3.3 Fit Signals for Projects Instead¶

    • Scope is discrete with a single deliverable.
    • Client cannot commit to a monthly cadence.
    • Deliverables are mandatory before any ongoing work.
    • Client expects one-time procurement approval.

    4. Pricing Benchmarks by Service Line¶

    4.1 vCISO / Security Program Retainers (Internal Benchmarks)¶

    Tier Monthly Range Typical Scope
    vCISO Lite $1,600-$5,000 Monthly advisory + risk review
    vCISO Standard $5,000-$12,000 Governance + compliance oversight
    vCISO Premium $12,000-$20,000 Executive reporting + audit readiness
    SMB Range $1,000-$5,000 Light governance and policy review
    Mid-Market Range $5,000-$10,000 Governance, roadmap, vendor oversight
    Enterprise Range $10,000-$20,000+ Board reporting, audit readiness
    Fractional CISO (light) $6,000-$15,000 1-2 days/month
    Fractional CISO (standard) $12,000-$35,000 3-6 days/month
    Fractional CISO (heavy) $25,000-$70,000 6-12 days/month
    Strategic Advisory Retainer $10,000-$30,000 Architecture + risk reviews
    - Typical hourly bands: $200-$300/hr common; $500+/hr premium.
    - Retainer scope should separate strategic advisory from execution work.

    4.2 Expert Witness Retainers (Litigation)¶

    Item Benchmark
    Initial retainer $2,000-$5,000 upfront (3-4 hours standard)
    Retainer minimum 3-4 hours to schedule work
    Deposition minimum 4 hours per day
    Trial minimum Half-day (4 hours)
    Payment terms Net 15 or Net 30
    Travel time billing 50% of hourly rate
    - Case review rates often start $350-$600/hour for new experts.
    - Depositions commonly $450-$800/hour as experience grows.
    - Trial testimony commonly $500-$1,000/hour at premium tier.
    - Evergreen retainers are used to avoid payment gaps.

    4.3 DFIR / Incident Response Retainers¶

    • Retainer models are used for response readiness and guaranteed response windows.
    • Some providers offer 2-hour response time retainers.
    • Retainer + surge model keeps baseline predictable and surge premium.
    • Hourly bands for IR surge work commonly $250-$700/hour in internal benchmarks.

    4.4 Penetration Testing Retainers¶

    • Quarterly or monthly testing cycles with bundled retesting.
    • PTaaS baseline for community platforms: $85-$200/hour.
    • Enterprise/API testing: $200-$500/hour.
    • Red/purple team surge work: $250-$600/hour equivalents.
    • Retest cycles are often included once within 30-60 days.

    4.5 Security Awareness Program Retainers¶

    Role Benchmark
    Program manager retainer (SMB) $1,000-$3,000/month
    Example management fee $1,500/month on 500-user client
    Per-user recurring model $2-$5 per user/month
    - Retainer scope includes campaign scheduling, reporting, and remedial training.

    4.6 Privacy / DPO Retainers (Illustrative)¶

    Organization Size Monthly Retainer Range Notes
    Startup / Small $2,000-$6,000 Light governance and policy review
    Mid-Market $6,000-$20,000 DSAR oversight + vendor reviews
    Enterprise $20,000-$60,000+ Program ownership and audits
    - Outsourced DPO hourly ranges: $250-$500/hour in privacy benchmarks.

    4.7 Physical Security Retainers¶

    • GSA schedule maintenance retainer: $1,250-$3,000/month.
    • CPTED advisory retainer: ~$2,500 for 8-10 hour block.
    • Advisory retainers often align to $150-$300/hour equivalents.

    4.8 Automotive / Product Security Retainers¶

    • Retainer model used for ongoing CSMS/PSMS support and vulnerability management.
    • Principal consultants may be priced daily or on retainer for ongoing oversight.

    4.9 Expert Network Call Economics (Context)¶

    • Expert network calls are hourly or per-minute, not retainer based.
    • Typical call rates: $200-$1,000/hour depending on network and specialty.
    • Payment timing varies 1-6 weeks; confirm terms before calls.

    5. Contract Stack Overview¶

    • Master Services Agreement (MSA): overarching legal relationship.
    • Statement of Work (SOW): scope, deliverables, timeline, fees.
    • Retainer Agreement: recurring scope, monthly fee, availability.
    • Rules of Engagement (ROE): pentest authorization and constraints.
    • NDA / Confidentiality Agreement: protects client data and findings.
    • Data Processing Addendum (DPA): required for regulated data handling.
    • Change Order: scope adjustments and pricing changes.
    • Engagement Letter: common for expert witness and litigation work.
    • Subcontractor Agreement: flow-down obligations for 1099/C2C work.
    • Insurance Certificate: evidence of E&O and cyber coverage.
    • Work Authorization: explicit permission to test systems.

    6. Core Retainer Terms¶

    • Scope of services (advisory vs execution).
    • In-scope systems and business units.
    • Out-of-scope exclusions.
    • Included hours or deliverables per month.
    • Billing increments (15-minute, 30-minute, or hourly).
    • Overage rate and approval threshold.
    • Response windows (business hours, after-hours).
    • On-call coverage and surge rates.
    • Cadence for status updates and reports.
    • Retainer replenishment threshold (evergreen minimum).
    • Carry-over policy for unused hours.
    • Data handling and retention timelines.
    • Confidentiality and MNPI restrictions (expert networks).
    • IP ownership and license rights for deliverables.
    • Liability cap and indemnity boundaries.
    • Term and termination notice period.
    • Payment terms and invoicing procedures.
    • Travel reimbursement policy.

    7. Payment Terms & Billing Mechanics¶

    • Net 15 or Net 30 is the default standard.
    • Net 60+ and pay-when-paid clauses are red flags.
    • 25%-50% upfront deposit is common for large projects.
    • Monthly invoices are common for retainers.
    • Expert witness engagements often require prepayment via retainer.
    • Retainer replenishment should be triggered when balance dips below threshold.
    • Late payment penalties should be written (e.g., 1.5% per month).
    • Milestone billing reduces exposure on multi-week engagements.
    • Retainer hours should be logged and reported monthly.
    • Use escrow on platforms when available (Upwork).
    • Require written approval for any retainer overage.
    • Withhold final report until invoices are paid (expert witness norm).
    • Define billing for travel time (full or 50% rate).
    • Explicitly list reimbursable expenses.

    8. Negotiation Playbook¶

    8.1 Pricing Anchors (From Internal Templates)¶

    • Scope-first anchor: tie rate to senior band and scope clarity.
    • Premium urgency anchor: price higher for surge response or executive impact.
    • Retainer + surge hybrid: baseline retainer plus premium response rate.
    • Rate review clause: adjust after first milestone if scope shifts.

    8.2 Tactics That Protect Margin¶

    • Start 15-20% above target to leave room to negotiate.
    • Tie premium pricing to compliance deadlines or urgency.
    • Offer retainer discounts only for scope stability.
    • Require change orders for any scope expansion.
    • Align payment milestones to deliverable acceptance.
    • Use minimum engagement hours for premium clients (20-40 hours).

    8.3 Common Red Flags¶

    • Unlimited liability or broad indemnity.
    • Net 60+ payment terms or pay-when-paid clauses.
    • Vague deliverables with fixed fees.
    • Unlimited retesting or remediation support.
    • Refusal to provide written rate confirmation.

    9. Retainer Conversion & Renewal¶

    • Step 1: Deliver a scoped assessment with clear findings.
    • Step 2: Present a remediation roadmap and risk register.
    • Step 3: Offer monthly governance reviews with defined cadence.
    • Step 4: Convert to retainer anchored to compliance deadlines.
    • Step 5: Review utilization after 60-90 days and adjust hours.
    • Step 6: Offer annual renewal with rate review clause.
    • Use platforms for acquisition and direct contracts for retention.
    • Document conversion path in proposals and follow-up notes.
    • Track retainer conversion rate as a core KPI.

    11. Template: vCISO Retainer Agreement¶

    11.1 Scope (Typical)¶

    • Governance oversight and security roadmap updates.
    • Risk register maintenance and quarterly review.
    • Policy and procedure review and approval support.
    • Vendor risk and third-party questionnaire support.
    • Compliance readiness guidance (SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA).
    • Executive and board reporting (monthly or quarterly).

    11.2 Cadence¶

    • Monthly executive sync.
    • Quarterly risk review and metrics report.
    • Annual program assessment or roadmap refresh.

    11.3 Retainer Structure¶

    • Monthly retainer: $5,000-$15,000 mid-market benchmark.
    • SMB range: $1,000-$5,000 for light advisory.
    • Enterprise range: $10,000-$20,000+ for board-level support.
    • Included hours: 15-20 hours typical; adjust for heavy engagements.
    • Overage rate: $200-$300/hr typical; premium $500+/hr.

    11.4 Response Windows¶

    • Business hours availability standard.
    • After-hours support billed at surge rate.

    11.5 Deliverables¶

    • Monthly KPI dashboard.
    • Risk register updates.
    • Quarterly board report.
    • Compliance readiness checklist.

    11.6 Liability & Insurance¶

    • Liability cap benchmark: 12 months of fees.
    • Cyber liability coverage: $1M-$5M aggregate.
    • Professional liability (E&O): $1M+ common.

    11.7 Termination¶

    • 30-60 day notice recommended.
    • Suspension allowed for delinquent payment.

    18. SOW Templates (Outline + Expanded)¶

    18.1 SOW Outline (Security Architecture)¶

    • Objectives and business context.
    • Scope and boundaries (systems, business units, environments).
    • Deliverables with acceptance criteria.
    • Assumptions and dependencies.
    • Roles and responsibilities (RACI).
    • Timeline and milestones.
    • Engagement model (T&M, fixed fee, retainer).
    • Change control and scope management.
    • Confidentiality and data handling requirements.
    • Success criteria and reporting cadence.

    18.2 Expanded SOW Example (Mobile Security)¶

    Objectives - Assess iOS and Android apps aligned to OWASP MASVS.

    Scope - iOS app version X.X. - Android app version Y.Y. - API endpoints in inventory.

    Out of Scope - Third-party services not controlled by client. - Load or performance testing.

    Methodology - Threat modeling and architecture review. - Static analysis of code or binaries. - Dynamic testing with instrumentation. - API testing and authorization review.

    Deliverables - Executive summary and risk overview. - Detailed findings with reproduction steps. - MASVS mapping and evidence references. - Remediation recommendations. - One retest cycle within 30 days.

    Assumptions - Client provides test builds and credentials. - Test environments are stable and accessible.

    Timeline - Week 1: Onboarding and threat model. - Week 2-4: Testing and analysis. - Week 5: Reporting and review. - Week 6: Retest and closure.

    Fees and Payment Terms - Fixed fee or T&M. - 50% upfront, 50% upon report delivery.

    20. Clause Library & Language Bank¶

    20.1 Retainer Clauses¶

    • "Invoices are due Net 30 from receipt."
    • "Late payments accrue interest at 1.5% per month."
    • "Milestone payments are due upon delivery acceptance."
    • "Expenses are reimbursed at cost with prior approval."

    20.3 Liability Clauses¶

    • "Liability shall not exceed twelve (12) months of fees."
    • "Total liability is capped at $1,000,000."
    • "Neither party is liable for consequential damages."

    20.4 Confidentiality Clauses¶

    • "Receiving party shall use confidential information solely for the engagement."
    • "Confidentiality obligations survive termination for five (5) years."
    • "Confidential information includes findings, data, and system details."

    20.5 Data Handling Clauses¶

    • "Evidence will be retained for 90 days after final report delivery."
    • "Client data will be destroyed upon written request."

    20.6 Change Control Clauses¶

    • "Scope changes require written approval and a change order."

    20.7 Authorization Clauses¶

    • "Client authorizes consultant to perform testing per ROE."

    20.8 IP Clauses¶

    • "Consultant retains ownership of pre-existing methodologies and tooling."

    20.9 Termination Clauses¶

    • "Consultant may suspend services if payment is 30 days overdue."

    21. Checklists (Pre / During / Post)¶

    21.1 Pre-Engagement Checklist¶

    • Confirm client legal entity and billing contact.
    • Confirm authorized testing approval and ROE.
    • Validate scope and exclusions in writing.
    • Obtain certificates of insurance if required.
    • Confirm payment terms and invoicing process.
    • Sign NDA and confidentiality agreements.
    • Document data retention and destruction timeline.

    21.2 In-Engagement Checklist¶

    • Track milestones and acceptance criteria.
    • Document scope changes and issue change orders.
    • Maintain secure storage for evidence and artifacts.
    • Provide status updates per contract cadence.
    • Validate any changes to timeline or access.

    21.3 Post-Engagement Checklist¶

    • Deliver final report and executive briefing.
    • Confirm payment received before releasing sensitive artifacts.
    • Provide retest results if contracted.
    • Securely destroy data per retention policy.
    • Capture lessons learned for process improvement.

    22. SLA & Escalation Definitions¶

    • Severity 1: active incident, response within 2 hours (surge rate).
    • Severity 2: high risk, response within 8 hours.
    • Severity 3: standard advisory, response within 2 business days.
    • Retainer includes business-hour availability by default.
    • After-hours support billed at premium surge rate.
    • Escalation contacts and backup contacts must be listed.
    • Stop-test triggers documented for offensive testing.

    23. Risk Controls, Insurance, and Liability¶

    • Typical liability caps for partnerships: $1M-$5M range.
    • vCISO agreements often cap liability at 12 months of fees.
    • Professional liability (E&O) coverage commonly $1M+.
    • Cyber liability coverage commonly $1M-$5M aggregate.
    • Document evidence handling and chain-of-custody in DFIR.
    • Define data retention and destruction timelines.
    • Clarify IP ownership and licensing for deliverables.

    24. Referral Fees & Partner Terms¶

    • MSP referral fees: 10-15% of year-1 retainer common.
    • White-label model: wholesale rate (e.g., $4k/mo) resold at higher rate.
    • Sales agent fee: ~20% first deal (one-time).
    • Peer consultant fee: ~10% one-time finders fee.
    • Include referral fee terms in partner agreements.

    25. Metrics & Reporting Cadence¶

    • Retainer conversion rate.
    • Retainer renewal rate.
    • Effective hourly rate (retainer revenue / hours).
    • Utilization rate vs contracted hours.
    • Retainer collection rate.
    • Days to payment (DSO).
    • Client satisfaction and review scores.
    • Report revision count per project.
    • Monthly KPI dashboard cadence.

    26. Negotiation Scripts¶

    • Scope-first anchor: "Based on the scope and deliverables, my rate aligns with the senior consultant band at $X-$Y."
    • Premium urgency anchor: "Given the urgency and executive impact, this fits my surge rate."
    • Retainer + surge hybrid: "We can set a baseline retainer for readiness and a separate surge rate for incidents."
    • Rate review clause: "Lets include a rate review after the first milestone to reset expectations."
    • Retainer discipline: "I schedule work upon receipt of the retainer."

    27. Glossary¶

    • MSA: Master Services Agreement.
    • SOW: Statement of Work.
    • ROE: Rules of Engagement (authorized testing parameters).
    • DPA: Data Processing Addendum.
    • Retainer: Prepaid recurring fee for access and defined scope.
    • Evergreen Retainer: Retainer that auto-replenishes when balance dips below threshold.
    • Surge Rate: Premium rate for urgent or after-hours response.
    • Change Order: Written modification to scope, timeline, or fees.
    • MNPI: Material Non-Public Information (expert network compliance).

    28. 30-60-90 Day Implementation Plan¶

    Days 0-30¶

    • Build standard retainer agreement template (skeleton + clause library).
    • Define rate card and retainer tiers.

    • Draft change order template.

    Days 31-60¶

    • Pilot retainer with 1-2 clients.
    • Track utilization and adjust included hours.
    • Build reporting cadence (monthly KPI dashboard).
    • Set escalation workflow and response windows.
    • Capture feedback and refine scope language.

    Days 61-90¶

    • Launch retainer conversion campaign (assessment roadmap retainer).
    • Add renewal and rate review clauses to all retainers.
    • Create partner referral terms and outreach scripts.
    • Update templates with lessons learned.
    • Formalize retention metrics and quarterly review process.
