    CODEX: THE $100K/MONTH SECURITY CONSULTANT WEEKLY ROUTINE¶

    Version: 1.0.0 Date: December 25, 2025 Author: Codex System Target Audience: Elite Security Consultants, vCISOs, Expert Network Consultants, Bug Bounty Hunters Objective: Operationalize the path to $100k/month through extreme efficiency, strategic time management, and physiological optimization.

    1. INTRODUCTION: THE ARCHITECTURE OF A 7-FIGURE SCHEDULE¶

    Reaching $100,000 per month as a solopreneur or boutique agency owner is not strictly a function of skillâ€”it is a function of time arbitrage and energy management. You cannot bill 400 hours a month. To hit this revenue target, your schedule must prioritize high-leverage activities (sales, strategic consulting, content assets) over low-leverage toil (email, scheduling, basic troubleshooting).

    This guide provides the blueprint for a week that balances aggressive revenue generation with sustainable health and deep work. It is not designed for the 9-to-5 worker; it is designed for the high-performance athlete of the cybersecurity world.

    1.1 The Mathematics of Time¶

    To earn $100k/month ($1.2M/year), assuming a 40-hour work week (approx. 160 hours/month): * Hourly Value: ~$625/hour. * Reality: You cannot bill every hour. You likely bill 40-50% of your time. * Effective Billable Rate: You must generate ~$1,250 - $1,500 in value for every hour spent on client work to cover admin, sales, and downtime. * Implication: If a task can be outsourced for less than $100/hour, you should not be doing it.

    1.2 The Four Quadrants of Consultant Time¶

    1. Deep Work (The Money Maker): Security assessments, report writing, code review, content creation.
    2. Live Work (The Trust Builder): Client calls, expert network consultations, sales meetings, speaking engagements.
    3. Shallow Work (The Maintainer): Email, Slack, invoicing, scheduling.
    4. Growth Work (The Multiplier): Networking, learning new tech, refining offers, business strategy.

    2. CORE PRINCIPLES OF THE ROUTINE¶

    2.1 Context Switching is the Enemy¶

    Every time you switch from a technical audit to a client call, you lose 20 minutes of cognitive capacity. * Rule: Batch similar tasks. Do not check email during Deep Work blocks. * Strategy: "Theme Days" (e.g., Maker vs. Manager days).

    2.2 Energy Management > Time Management¶

    • Chronotype Alignment: If you are a night owl, do not force 5 AM starts. Build the schedule around your peak cognitive windows.
    • The 90-Minute Wave: Work in 90-minute ultradian cycles followed by 15-20 minute breaks.

    2.3 The "No-Fly" Zones¶

    • Morning Protocol: No phone/email for the first 60 minutes.
    • Deep Work Protection: 4 hours/day of uninterrupted creative/technical output.
    • Hard Stop: A specific time each day where work ends to allow recovery.

    3. THE WEEKLY TEMPLATE: THEME DAYS¶

    To minimize cognitive load, we assign a primary "Theme" to each day of the week.

    Day Theme Focus Areas
    Monday CEO & Strategy Pipeline review, Business Dev, Team syncs, Weekly Planning.
    Tuesday Deep Execution Core client deliverables, Audits, vCISO strategy docs.
    Wednesday Mid-Week Sprint Client meetings, Expert Network calls, Consultations.
    Thursday Marketing & Content LinkedIn, Articles, Webinars, Podcast recording.
    Friday Finance & Wrap-up Invoicing, Expense reports, Closing loops, Next week prep.
    Saturday Deep Learning (Optional) Research, Certifications, Lab time.
    Sunday Rest & Reset Zero work, Family, Bio-recovery.

    4. DETAILED DAILY SCHEDULES¶

    MONDAY: The CEO Day (Strategy & Sales)¶

    Focus: Setting the trajectory for the week and filling the pipeline.

    • 06:00 - 07:00: Wake up, Hydrate, Light Movement, Cold Exposure. NO PHONE.
    • 07:00 - 08:00: Deep Breakfast & Review Annual Goals.
    • 08:00 - 09:30: Block 1: Pipeline Review.
      • Check CRM (HubSpot/Salesforce).
      • Follow up on open proposals.
      • Send 10 personalized outreach messages (LinkedIn/Email).
    • 09:30 - 10:00: Break & Walk.
    • 10:00 - 12:00: Block 2: Discovery Calls.
      • Schedule initial prospect meetings here.
      • Review potential Expert Network matches.
    • 12:00 - 13:00: Lunch & Nutritional Reset.
    • 13:00 - 15:00: Block 3: Proposal Writing.
      • Drafting SOWs (Statements of Work).
      • Pricing strategy for new leads.
    • 15:00 - 15:30: Admin Blitz (Email/Slack triage - limit to 30 mins).
    • 15:30 - 17:00: Block 4: Team/Delegation.
      • Sync with VA, junior analysts, or subcontractors.
      • Assign tasks for the week.
    • 17:00 - 18:00: Workout (Strength Training).
    • 18:00+: Shutdown ritual. Evening relaxation.

    TUESDAY: Deep Execution (The Maker Day)¶

    Focus: High-value deliverables. No meetings allowed.

    • 06:00 - 07:00: Morning Routine.
    • 07:00 - 08:00: Commute/Prep (or Read).
    • 08:00 - 12:00: MEGA BLOCK: Deep Technical Work.
      • Phone in Airplane Mode.
      • Pentesting / Code Review.
      • Developing Security Policies.
      • Architecture Risk Analysis.
    • 12:00 - 13:00: Lunch & Mental Break (No screens).
    • 13:00 - 16:00: Block 2: Secondary Technical Work.
      • Drafting reports for the morning's work.
      • Reviewing automated scan results.
    • 16:00 - 17:00: Admin Triage (Only urgent items).
    • 17:00 - 18:30: Workout (Cardio/Endurance).
    • 18:30+: Dinner & Leisure.

    WEDNESDAY: The Consultant (Meetings & Calls)¶

    Focus: External facing interaction. Stack all calls here.

    • 06:00 - 07:00: Morning Routine.
    • 07:00 - 08:00: Prep for client calls (Review agendas).
    • 08:00 - 12:00: Block 1: Client Retainer Meetings (vCISO).
      • Monthly steering committee meetings.
      • Status updates with IT Directors/CISOs.
    • 12:00 - 12:30: Quick Lunch.
    • 12:30 - 15:30: Block 2: Expert Network Calls.
      • GLG/AlphaSights/Guidepoint calls (1-hour slots).
      • Target: 3 calls x $500-$1000/hr.
    • 15:30 - 17:00: Block 3: Ad-hoc Client Support.
      • Responding to client urgent requests.
      • Troubleshooting sessions.
    • 17:00 - 18:00: Decompress / Light exercise (Yoga/Stretch).
    • 18:00+: Social evening or Networking event.

    THURSDAY: The Thought Leader (Marketing & Content)¶

    Focus: Building the brand that drives inbound leads.

    • 06:00 - 07:00: Morning Routine.
    • 07:00 - 08:00: Consume Industry News (HackerNews, Feedly, Twitter).
    • 08:00 - 11:00: Block 1: Content Creation (Writing).
      • Write 1 long-form article (Blog/Substack).
      • Draft 5 LinkedIn posts for the next week.
      • Write case study from Tuesday's work (anonymized).
    • 11:00 - 12:30: Block 2: Media/Recording.
      • Record YouTube video or Podcast episode.
      • Guest on other podcasts.
    • 12:30 - 13:30: Lunch.
    • 13:30 - 15:30: Block 3: Distribution & Engagement.
      • Engage with comments on LinkedIn.
      • Comment on target prospect's posts.
      • Newsletter formatting and scheduling.
    • 15:30 - 17:00: Learning/Research.
      • Read whitepapers.
      • Test a new security tool.
    • 17:00 - 18:00: Workout.
    • 18:00+: Family/Personal time.

    FRIDAY: The Operator (Finance & Admin)¶

    Focus: Closing the loop. Don't carry baggage into the weekend.

    • 07:00 - 08:00: Morning Routine.
    • 08:00 - 10:00: Block 1: Finance.
      • Send Invoices.
      • Follow up on overdue payments.
      • Update P&L spreadsheet.
      • Review business expenses.
    • 10:00 - 12:00: Block 2: Project Management.
      • Update project trackers.
      • Send "Weekly Status Reports" to clients (Automate this if possible).
    • 12:00 - 13:30: Long Lunch (Networking Lunch with peer or prospect).
    • 13:30 - 15:00: Block 3: The "Loose Ends" Sweep.
      • Clear Inbox to Zero.
      • File digital paperwork.
      • Clean up desktop/downloads folder.
    • 15:00 - 16:00: Block 4: Weekly Review.
      • What went well?
      • What failed?
      • Plan next week's calendar.
    • 16:00: HARD STOP. Start Weekend.

    SATURDAY: The Student (Deep Learning - Optional)¶

    Focus: Staying ahead of the curve. Low pressure.

    • 08:00 - 10:00: Block 1: Deep Study.
      • Study for next cert (CISSP/OSCP/CISM).
      • Read technical books.
    • 10:00 - 12:00: Block 2: Labs.
      • HackTheBox / TryHackMe.
      • Building home lab infrastructure.
      • Experimenting with new AI tools.
    • Afternoon: OFF. Hobbies, Family, Outdoors.

    SUNDAY: The Human (Rest)¶

    Focus: Total recovery.

    • Activities:
      • Digital Detox (No screens until evening).
      • Nature exposure.
      • Meal prep for the week.
      • Sauna / Massage / Recovery.
    • Evening (20:00 - 20:30): "Sunday Scaries" Killer.
      • 5-minute review of Monday's calendar.
      • Lay out clothes.
      • Set intent for the week.

    5. TIME BLOCKING STRATEGIES¶

    5.1 The Pomodoro Upgrade (50/10)¶

    Standard Pomodoro (25/5) is often too short for deep technical work. * Strategy: Work 50 minutes, Break 10 minutes. * Cycle: After 3 cycles (3 hours), take a 60-minute break.

    5.2 The "Eat the Frog" Method¶

    • Schedule your most difficult/dreaded task (e.g., cold calling, complex report writing) as the very first thing in your first Deep Work block.
    • Do not check email before eating the frog.

    5.3 Buffer Blocks¶

    • Always leave 30 minutes of "white space" between meetings.
    • Why? Meetings run over. Bio breaks. Notes processing.
    • Rule: Never schedule back-to-back-to-back calls unless it's a dedicated "Call Block" where you have prepped specifically for rapid switching.

    6. CLIENT CALL SCHEDULING PROTOCOLS¶

    6.1 The Calendar Fortress¶

    Use a scheduling tool (Calendly/Cal.com) to enforce your rules. Do not ask "When are you free?" * Settings: * Only allow bookings on Tue/Wed/Thu. * Minimum notice: 24 hours. * Maximum 4 calls per day. * Buffer: 15 mins before/after.

    6.2 Call Types & Durations¶

    • Discovery Call: 15 Minutes (Strict). Qualify or disqualify.
    • Strategy Session: 45 Minutes. (Billable or High-Value Prospect).
    • Project Update: 20 Minutes. (Focus on blockers/approvals).
    • Expert Network Consultation: 60 Minutes (Hard stop).

    6.3 Pre-Call Ritual¶

    1. Review CRM notes (2 mins).
    2. Open relevant docs/tabs (1 min).
    3. Fill water bottle.
    4. Breathe.

    7. BUSINESS DEVELOPMENT (BD) INTEGRATION¶

    BD is not an event; it is a habit. Even when fully booked, you must market.

    7.1 The "Power Hour" (Daily)¶

    Dedicate 1 hour daily (preferably Monday morning) to: 1. Outreach: 5 new contacts. 2. Follow-up: 5 existing warm leads. 3. Referrals: Ask 1 current client for a referral.

    7.2 Networking Activities¶

    • Weekly: Attend 1 virtual webinar or industry meetup.
    • Monthly: Attend 1 in-person local event (ISSA, ISACA, OWASP).
    • Quarterly: Attend/Speak at a major conference.

    8. HEALTH & WELLNESS INTEGRATION (Biohacking)¶

    You are a cognitive athlete. Your brain is your asset.

    8.1 Sleep Hygiene¶

    • Goal: 7.5 - 8 hours.
    • Protocol: No blue light 2 hours before bed. Cool room (65-68Â°F). Magnesium supplementation.
    • Why? Sleep deprivation reduces IQ and emotional regulation (critical for negotiations).

    8.2 Ergonomics¶

    • Desk: Sit/Stand desk. Switch every 60 minutes.
    • Eyes: 20-20-20 Rule (Every 20 mins, look 20 feet away for 20 seconds). Blue light blocking glasses.
    • Air: CO2 monitor in the office. High CO2 (>1000ppm) causes brain fog. Open windows.

    8.3 Nutrition for Focus¶

    • Intermittent Fasting: Skip breakfast or have just coffee/fats (Bulletproof style) to prolong morning focus spike.
    • Lunch: Low carb/Low glycemic. Avoid the "pasta coma" in the afternoon.
    • Hydration: 3-4 Liters of water daily.

    8.4 Physical Activity¶

    • Minimum: 3x Strength training sessions per week (45 mins).
    • Daily: 10k steps. Walking meetings for internal/informal calls.
    • Recovery: Sauna usage (heat shock proteins) or cold plunge (dopamine spike) if available.

    9. PRODUCTIVITY TOOLS & TECH STACK¶

    Streamline the routine with the right software.

    9.1 Essential Stack¶

    • Calendar: Google Calendar / Outlook (The source of truth).
    • Task Manager: Todoist / OmniFocus / Notion.
    • Note Taking: Obsidian / Notion (Second Brain).
    • Scheduling: Calendly / Cal.com.
    • Time Tracking: Toggl (Track billable vs. non-billable).
    • Blocking: Freedom / Cold Turkey (Block social media during Deep Work).

    9.2 Automation (Zapier/Make)¶

    • Trigger: New Lead in Calendly -> Action: Create Trello Card & Draft Email Draft.
    • Trigger: Client pays invoice -> Action: Send Thank You email & Slack notification.

    10. HANDLING DISRUPTIONS (Crisis Management)¶

    10.1 The Incident Response Plan for Your Schedule¶

    When a client has a breach or urgent crisis: 1. Assess Severity: Is this a true P1 (Business Stopping) or a P3 (Annoying)? 2. Activate Protocol: * If P1: Cancel all internal meetings. Reschedule non-urgent client work. Notify family of late night. * If P3: "I acknowledge receipt. I will review this during my 3 PM admin block." 3. The "Crisis Rate": Ensure your contract specifies a higher hourly rate for emergency, immediate-response work (e.g., 2x standard rate). This filters false emergencies.

    11. WORK-LIFE BALANCE & BOUNDARIES¶

    11.1 The "After Hours" Auto-Responder¶

    Set expectations. If you reply at 10 PM, they will expect replies at 10 PM. * Email Signature: "I check email at 09:00 and 16:00 ET. For urgent matters, call [Number]."

    11.2 Vacation Protocols¶

    • Quarterly: Take 1 long weekend (Fri-Mon off).
    • Annually: Take 2 full weeks off.
    • The Deputy: Have a trusted peer you can refer emergencies to while you are away (reciprocal arrangement).

    12. CHECKLISTS FOR SUCCESS¶

    12.1 Daily Shutdown Ritual (15 Mins)¶

    • Review today's open tasks.
    • Move unfinished tasks to a specific future slot (don't just "push to tomorrow").
    • Clear physical desktop.
    • Close all browser tabs.
    • Check tomorrow's calendar: Do I have early meetings?
    • Visualize the first task of tomorrow.

    12.2 Weekly Review Protocol (Friday PM)¶

    • Inbox Zero: Email, Desktop, Downloads, Physical mail.
    • Calendar Audit: Did I stick to the plan? Where did I leak time?
    • Finance Check: What was billed? What was collected?
    • Outreach Check: Did I hit my BD targets?
    • Celebrate: Identify one "Win" of the week.

    12.3 Monthly Strategic Review (Last Friday of Month)¶

    • Review P&L. Am I on track for $100k/month run rate?
    • Review Pipeline value.
    • Check progress on Quarterly Goals (Rocks).
    • Update Rate Card (if demand exceeds supply).

    • Content/Course Sales: Passive/Semi-Passive = $10,000
      • Time Cost: 10 hours (maintenance/marketing).
    • TOTAL: $100,000 / Month
