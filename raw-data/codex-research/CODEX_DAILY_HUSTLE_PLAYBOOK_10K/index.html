    Version: 1.0 Last updated: 2024-10-05 Target: $10K/month revenue from security consulting Style: deep work + structured communication + weekly cadence Timezone: local

    Mission and revenue math¶

    • Primary goal: deliver repeatable, high-trust security outcomes.
    • Revenue target: $10,000 per month.
    • Baseline pricing: $2,500 per retainer or $200 per hour.
    • Capacity target: 12 to 15 billable hours per week.
    • Lead target: 6 warm leads per week.
    • Conversion target: 2 discovery calls per week.
    • Active clients: 3 to 4 at any time.
    • Core deliverables: security assessments, controls reviews, advisory.
    • Secondary deliverables: training, policy drafting, executive briefings.

    Operating principles¶

    • Guard deep work as the highest-leverage block.
    • Communicate in predictable windows to reduce churn.
    • Default to written updates and ticketed requests.
    • Capture every request into the queue within 10 minutes.
    • Say no to scope creep within the same day.
    • Ship small, clear deliverables daily.
    • Tie every action to a documented risk or outcome.
    • Maintain a visible backlog for every client.
    • Keep energy high by protecting sleep and movement.

    Client profile and scope boundaries¶

    • Ideal client: SaaS founder or CTO with 10–80 employees.
    • Typical needs: security roadmap, cloud hardening, audit readiness.
    • Exclusions: 24/7 SOC coverage, malware cleanup, incident forensics.
    • Engagement length: 4–12 weeks with weekly milestones.
    • Communication: async-first, two live touchpoints per week.
    • SLAs: response within 1 business day, priority window for incidents.

    Hour-by-hour deep dive¶

    00:00–01:00 — Sleep block¶

    • Objective: continuous sleep.
    • Primary tasks: none.
    • Output: recovery.
    • Metrics: sleep duration.
    • Boundaries: devices off.
    • Tools: sleep tracker.
    • Transition: no alarms except primary.

    01:00–02:00 — Sleep block¶

    • Objective: deep sleep.
    • Primary tasks: none.
    • Output: hormonal recovery.
    • Metrics: restfulness.
    • Boundaries: no screens.
    • Tools: blackout curtains.
    • Transition: maintain temperature.

    02:00–03:00 — Sleep block¶

    • Objective: deep sleep.
    • Primary tasks: none.
    • Output: restored focus.
    • Metrics: heart rate dips.
    • Boundaries: no interruptions.
    • Tools: white noise.
    • Transition: keep room cool.

    03:00–04:00 — Sleep block¶

    • Objective: continuous sleep.
    • Primary tasks: none.
    • Output: cognitive recovery.
    • Metrics: sleep score.
    • Boundaries: no notifications.
    • Tools: do-not-disturb.
    • Transition: slow wake prep.

    04:00–05:00 — Sleep block¶

    • Objective: complete final cycle.
    • Primary tasks: none.
    • Output: full recovery.
    • Metrics: sleep length.
    • Boundaries: no phone.
    • Tools: alarm for 05:00.
    • Transition: gentle wake.

    05:00–06:00 — Wake + prime¶

    • Objective: switch into execution mode.
    • Primary tasks: hydration, mobility, breathwork.
    • Output: calm, alert state.
    • Metrics: energy 7/10 or higher.
    • Boundaries: no email.
    • Tools: water, timer, journal.
    • Transition: move to planning desk.

    06:00–07:00 — Plan the day¶

    • Objective: set the top 3 priorities.
    • Primary tasks: review backlog, choose deliverables.
    • Output: locked daily plan.
    • Metrics: 3 outcomes defined.
    • Boundaries: no inbound tasks.
    • Tools: task manager, calendar.
    • Transition: open comms window.

    07:00–08:00 — Client comms window 1¶

    • Objective: clear inbound messages.
    • Primary tasks: reply, triage, schedule.
    • Output: inbox zero for clients.
    • Metrics: responses sent.
    • Boundaries: no deep work.
    • Tools: email, Slack, CRM.
    • Transition: launch research block.

    08:00–09:00 — Research block A1¶

    • Objective: gather evidence and context.
    • Primary tasks: review logs, configs, code.
    • Output: notes and risk hypotheses.
    • Metrics: 3 insights captured.
    • Boundaries: no meetings.
    • Tools: notes, terminal, docs.
    • Transition: continue analysis.

    09:00–10:00 — Research block A2¶

    • Objective: validate findings.
    • Primary tasks: test assumptions, map risks.
    • Output: validated issues list.
    • Metrics: top 3 risks defined.
    • Boundaries: no comms.
    • Tools: sandbox, scanner, docs.
    • Transition: move to delivery block.

    10:00–11:00 — Client delivery block¶

    • Objective: convert research into artifacts.
    • Primary tasks: draft report sections, diagrams.
    • Output: client-ready deliverables.
    • Metrics: 1 section shipped.
    • Boundaries: no distractions.
    • Tools: docs, diagrams, templates.
    • Transition: quick buffer.

    11:00–12:00 — Sales/prospecting¶

    • Objective: keep pipeline warm.
    • Primary tasks: outreach, referrals, follow-ups.
    • Output: 3 new touches.
    • Metrics: 1 call booked.
    • Boundaries: no deep work.
    • Tools: CRM, LinkedIn, email.
    • Transition: lunch reset.

    12:00–13:00 — Lunch + reset¶

    • Objective: recovery and light reset.
    • Primary tasks: meal, short walk.
    • Output: energy restored.
    • Metrics: mood check.
    • Boundaries: no meetings.
    • Tools: timer, hydration.
    • Transition: prepare next block.

    13:00–14:00 — Research block B1¶

    • Objective: secondary analysis.
    • Primary tasks: map controls to frameworks.
    • Output: mapped controls list.
    • Metrics: 5 controls mapped.
    • Boundaries: no inbound tasks.
    • Tools: framework docs.
    • Transition: continue analysis.

    14:00–15:00 — Research block B2¶

    • Objective: finalize findings.
    • Primary tasks: refine recommendations.
    • Output: prioritized remediation list.
    • Metrics: 5 recommendations.
    • Boundaries: no comms.
    • Tools: notes, checklists.
    • Transition: admin block.

    15:00–16:00 — Admin time¶

    • Objective: keep business clean.
    • Primary tasks: invoicing, updates, file hygiene.
    • Output: clear admin queue.
    • Metrics: zero overdue items.
    • Boundaries: no client calls.
    • Tools: billing, docs.
    • Transition: comms window 2.

    16:00–17:00 — Client comms window 2¶

    • Objective: share status and close loops.
    • Primary tasks: send updates, book calls.
    • Output: status sent to all clients.
    • Metrics: 100% updates.
    • Boundaries: no deep work.
    • Tools: email, Slack, calendar.
    • Transition: delivery block.

    17:00–18:00 — Client delivery block¶

    • Objective: finish the day’s deliverables.
    • Primary tasks: finalize reports, send assets.
    • Output: shipped artifacts.
    • Metrics: 1–2 deliverables shipped.
    • Boundaries: no new scope.
    • Tools: templates, docs.
    • Transition: marketing.

    18:00–19:00 — Marketing/content¶

    • Objective: build credibility.
    • Primary tasks: publish insights, update portfolio.
    • Output: 1 short post or update.
    • Metrics: 1 asset shared.
    • Boundaries: no heavy research.
    • Tools: social, blog, portfolio.
    • Transition: wrap-up.

    19:00–20:00 — Wrap-up¶

    • Objective: clear loose ends.
    • Primary tasks: update backlog, plan tomorrow.
    • Output: clean task list.
    • Metrics: top 3 priorities set.
    • Boundaries: no new work.
    • Tools: task manager.
    • Transition: shutdown routine.

    20:00–21:00 — Shutdown routine¶

    • Objective: end work cleanly.
    • Primary tasks: review wins, set boundaries.
    • Output: mental off-ramp.
    • Metrics: stress ≤ 3/10.
    • Boundaries: no messages.
    • Tools: journal, calendar.
    • Transition: personal time.

    21:00–22:00 — Wind down¶

    • Objective: calm nervous system.
    • Primary tasks: light reading, stretch.
    • Output: relaxed state.
    • Metrics: sleep readiness.
    • Boundaries: no screens if possible.
    • Tools: book, tea.
    • Transition: bedtime.

    22:00–23:00 — Wind down¶

    • Objective: avoid cognitive load.
    • Primary tasks: prep for sleep.
    • Output: quiet mind.
    • Metrics: low stimulation.
    • Boundaries: no work talk.
    • Tools: bedtime routine.
    • Transition: sleep.

    23:00–00:00 — Sleep prep¶

    • Objective: fall asleep quickly.
    • Primary tasks: lights out, breathing.
    • Output: sleep onset.
    • Metrics: sleep latency < 20 minutes.
    • Boundaries: no phone.
    • Tools: alarm, white noise.
    • Transition: sleep.

    Morning routine (05:00–06:15)¶

    • 05:00 Wake, drink 12 oz water.
    • 05:02 Open blinds, natural light exposure.
    • 05:05 5 minutes nasal breathing.
    • 05:10 10 minutes mobility (neck, hips, shoulders).
    • 05:20 10 bodyweight reps each: squats, pushups, rows.
    • 05:30 5-minute cold rinse or brisk face wash.
    • 05:35 3-minute gratitude journal.
    • 05:38 Review the day’s top outcomes.
    • 05:40 Make the bed, tidy workspace.
    • 05:45 Prepare coffee/tea and protein snack.
    • 05:50 Quick environment scan (notifications off).
    • 05:55 Review calendar constraints.
    • 06:00 Open backlog, confirm top 3 deliverables.
    • 06:05 Identify 1 risk to reduce today.
    • 06:10 Identify 1 revenue action to take today.
    • 06:15 Start daily planning block.

    Client communication windows¶

    Window 1 — 07:00–08:00 (inbound triage)¶

    • Goal: respond to all client messages.
    • Rule: never start deep work until inbox is clear.
    • Triage tags: urgent, today, this week, backlog.
    • Reply template: acknowledge, confirm, timebox.
    • Escalation: if urgent, move into 10:00 block.
    • Meeting scheduling: book only Tue–Thu 10:30–16:30.
    • Status cadence: daily update for active projects.
    • Closed-loop: confirm next action in every response.
    • SLA: response within 1 business day.
    • Scorecard: zero unread by 08:00.

    Window 2 — 16:00–17:00 (status updates)¶

    • Goal: send proactive updates.
    • Rule: no new scope added after 16:30.
    • Update format: progress, risks, next steps.
    • Checkpoint: verify client priorities.
    • Deliverable tracking: link to artifacts.
    • Calendar hygiene: confirm upcoming calls.
    • Escalations: move blockers into next morning.
    • Scorecard: all active clients receive update.

    Window 3 — Optional 11:30–12:00 (sales responses)¶

    • Goal: respond to leads and partners.
    • Rule: prioritize warm leads over cold.
    • Template: quick value statement + call link.
    • SLA: respond same day for warm leads.
    • Scorecard: 3 touches per day.

    Research blocks¶

    Research block A (08:00–10:00)¶

    • Purpose: gather evidence and diagnose issues.
    • Inputs: logs, cloud configs, code review notes.
    • Output: 3 validated findings.
    • Checklist: confirm scope boundaries.
    • Checklist: note environment assumptions.
    • Checklist: identify quick wins.
    • Checklist: map findings to controls.
    • Checklist: capture screenshots or evidence.
    • Checklist: store artifacts in client repo.
    • Checklist: update risk register.
    • Checklist: prepare questions for client.

    Research block B (13:00–15:00)¶

    • Purpose: craft remediation guidance.
    • Inputs: validated findings list.
    • Output: recommendations ranked by impact.
    • Checklist: define risk severity.
    • Checklist: outline business impact.
    • Checklist: propose minimal fixes first.
    • Checklist: provide implementation steps.
    • Checklist: include verification steps.
    • Checklist: note dependencies and owners.
    • Checklist: check against frameworks.
    • Checklist: add references and links.
    • Checklist: prep summary for update.

    Admin time¶

    Daily admin (15:00–16:00)¶

    • Process invoices and payment status.
    • Log billable time blocks.
    • Update project trackers.
    • File and name new documents.
    • Clean up meeting notes.
    • Update CRM with lead statuses.
    • Check contract milestones.
    • Review upcoming renewals.
    • Confirm next-day calendar.
    • Send gentle nudges for blockers.

    Weekly admin (Friday 15:00–16:00)¶

    • Reconcile expenses.
    • Review revenue vs target.
    • Update pipeline forecast.
    • Draft next week’s focus.
    • Archive completed projects.
    • Refresh portfolio artifacts.
    • Audit access keys and tokens.
    • Update internal SOPs.

    Delivery quality checklist¶

    • Every deliverable maps to a risk or requirement.
    • Findings include evidence and reproducibility steps.
    • Recommendations include exact owner and timeline.
    • Severity includes likelihood and impact.
    • Each client artifact has a clear summary section.
    • All sensitive data is redacted.
    • Artifacts stored in versioned repo or folder.
    • Client receives a status update within 24 hours.
    • Confirm next action and deadline.

    Prospecting and pipeline¶

    • Daily target: 3 outreach touches.
    • Weekly target: 2 discovery calls.
    • Monthly target: 4 qualified proposals.
    • Sources: founder communities, referrals, LinkedIn.
    • Offer: security roadmap or audit readiness sprint.
    • Lead magnet: quick security scorecard.
    • Follow-up cadence: day 2, day 5, day 10.
    • Close criteria: budget, urgency, authority, fit.

    End-of-day shutdown¶

    • Review the top 3 wins.
    • Log any unfinished tasks into the backlog.
    • Confirm tomorrow’s top 3 priorities.
    • Clear desk and close tabs.
    • Put devices on do-not-disturb.
    • Set a hard stop for work talk.
    • Short stretch and breathwork.

    Weekly rhythm¶

    Monday¶

    • Review active client priorities.
    • Set weekly revenue and delivery goals.
    • Schedule deep work for Tue–Thu.

    Tuesday¶

    • Focus on research and analysis.
    • Prioritize deliverables for top clients.
    • Run first round of status updates.

    Wednesday¶

    • Conduct discovery calls.
    • Build or refine remediation plans.
    • Publish one thought leadership post.

    Thursday¶

    • Finalize reports and recommendations.
    • Prepare next week’s pipeline.
    • Check in on long-running tasks.

    Friday¶

    • Admin sweep and invoicing.
    • Weekly review and metrics.
    • Light marketing and networking.

    Templates and scripts¶

    Daily client update (email or Slack)¶

    • Subject: Daily update — [Client] — [Date]
    • Progress: [What moved today]
    • Risks/blockers: [Anything slowing progress]
    • Next steps: [What happens next]
    • Owner: [Who owns next step]
    • ETA: [Date/time]

    Quick triage response¶

    • Thanks for the note — I’ve got this queued.
    • I’ll review and reply with next steps by [time].
    • If this is urgent, please confirm the impact and deadline.

    Scope change response¶

    • This request looks outside our current scope.
    • I can add it as a new task with estimate by [time].
    • Do you want me to proceed with a separate quote?

    Discovery call agenda¶

    • 5 min: context and goals.
    • 10 min: current security posture.
    • 10 min: risks and priorities.
    • 5 min: engagement options and next steps.

    Post-call follow-up¶

    • Thanks for the call today.
    • Here are the top risks we discussed.
    • Here is a recommended first sprint.
    • Here is a proposed timeline and price.

    Weekly summary update¶

    • Overview of progress this week.
    • Major findings and impact.
    • Completed deliverables.
    • Planned work for next week.
    • Questions or decisions needed.

    Metrics and scorecard¶

    • Billable hours this week.
    • Revenue booked this month.
    • Active clients count.
    • Open findings by severity.
    • Average response time.
    • Deliverables shipped per week.
    • Lead touches per day.
    • Discovery calls booked per week.
    • Proposal win rate.
    • Sleep average hours.
    • Exercise sessions per week.

    Contingency playbook¶

    • If an incident appears: pause deep work and triage.
    • Confirm scope and authority before action.
    • Create an incident timeline note.
    • Notify client of initial findings within 2 hours.
    • Document every action taken.
    • After resolution, schedule a retrospective.

    Quick reference checklist¶

    • Top 3 priorities chosen by 06:30.
    • Inbound messages cleared by 08:00.
    • Research block A completed by 10:00.
    • Deliverable shipped by 11:00 or 18:00.
    • Prospecting touch completed by 12:00.
    • Admin queue cleared by 16:00.
    • Status updates sent by 17:00.
    • Shutdown routine completed by 20:00.

    • Publish one insight or lesson.

    • Review wins.

    20:00 cue¶

    • Power down devices.
    • Reflect and decompress.
    • Transition to personal time.

    Research artifact structure¶

    • Summary paragraph.
    • Scope and assumptions.
    • Evidence section with timestamps.
    • Impact analysis.
    • Recommended remediation steps.
    • Verification and testing steps.
    • Owner and due date.

    Client onboarding checklist¶

    • Signed agreement and scope.
    • Access provisioned (read-only by default).
    • Data handling expectations confirmed.
    • Workspace or shared folder created.
    • Intro call scheduled.
    • Security posture questionnaire sent.
    • Initial backlog created.

    Client offboarding checklist¶

    • Final report delivered.
    • Access revoked and confirmed.
    • Credentials rotated.
    • Retrospective notes shared.
    • Test plan for changes provided.
    • Optional follow-up call scheduled.

    Personal sustainability rules¶

    • No meetings before 10:30 unless urgent.
    • Maintain two full deep work blocks daily.
    • Hard stop at 20:00.
    • Move body at least 20 minutes daily.
    • One full day off per week.

    Micro-templates for rapid replies¶

    • Ack: “Got it — I’ll review and update by [time].”
    • Delay: “This is queued for tomorrow’s deep work block.”
    • Clarify: “Which environment or service does this affect?”
    • Confirm: “Can you confirm impact if this slips 24 hours?”
    • Close: “Delivered; please confirm receipt.”

    Notes for maintaining $10K/month pace¶

    • Protect reputation over speed.
    • Ship consistent, small wins daily.
    • Always tie work to revenue impact.
    • Keep pipeline warm even when busy.
    • Raise rates when utilization > 70%.
