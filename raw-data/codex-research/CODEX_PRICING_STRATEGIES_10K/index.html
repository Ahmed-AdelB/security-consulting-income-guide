    Executive Summary: Pricing Reality Check¶

    • Security consulting rates in repo research span roughly $40/hour (entry platform work) to $1,000+/hour (expert networks and expert witness).
    • Average freelance cybersecurity consulting rate appears near $144/hour in internal datasets.
    • U.S. employee-equivalent averages cluster around $50-$70/hour, reinforcing the need for 1099 premiums.
    • Premium expert networks commonly list $300-$1,000/hour for advisory calls.
    • vCISO models dominate recurring revenue, with $2k-$25k/month retainers depending on scope and seniority.
    • Fixed-fee compliance and pentest packages often outperform hourly billing when scope is tight.
    • Toptal, Upwork, and Fiverr show clear fee drag; net-rate math is mandatory for margin protection.
    • Retainer + project hybrid is the most stable model cited across repo guides.
    • Incident response and DFIR work command the highest urgency premiums and require strict minimums.
    • Expert witness work requires upfront retainers and minimum billing blocks.
    • Specialty premiums are consistently higher for OT/ICS, AI security, cloud, and zero trust architecture.
    • Day-rate anchors across multiple sources cluster at $1,000-$3,000/day for senior work.
    • High-end contractor signals ($200-$700/hour) often reflect emergency or expert-call engagements, not staff augmentation.
    • Rate negotiation advice repeats: start 15-20% above target, set floors, and anchor with outcome value.
    • Annual rate increases of 5-10% are recommended as a baseline; larger jumps after certifications or scarcity.
    • Platform-driven rate compression is common; direct B2B work supports $200+/hour for specialists.
    • Bundled packages and productized offers improve conversion and defend premium pricing.
    • Scope discipline and change orders are critical to preserving effective hourly rate.
    • Retainer conversion is the primary path to predictable cash flow.
    • Payment terms should target Net 15/Net 30; avoid Net 60+.

    Pricing Model Map (When to Use Which)¶

    Hourly / Time & Materials¶

    • Best for ambiguous scope, emergency response, or ongoing advisory with variable effort.
    • Typical ranges in repo data: $75-$300/hour general, $200-$500/hour for vCISO, $100-$300/hour for pentest.
    • Use minimum blocks (2-4 hours) to prevent micro-billing.
    • Protects against scope creep but caps upside on high-impact work.
    • Combine with weekly caps or sprint scopes for predictability.

    Daily / Weekly¶

    • Common in pentest, architecture, and staff augmentation engagements.
    • Day-rate anchors: $1,000-$2,500/day for senior pentest; $1,500-$3,000+ for enterprise consulting.
    • Simplifies client procurement for 1-3 week sprints.
    • Works well for on-site or workshop-heavy work.
    • Blend with fixed-fee reporting to protect margin.

    Fixed-Fee Projects¶

    • Best for discrete deliverables (pentest report, SOC 2 readiness, architecture review).
    • Typical project bands: $5k-$100k+ depending on scope, with 10-25% contingency.
    • Requires detailed scoping and explicit change-order terms.
    • Offers upside when delivery efficiency is high.
    • Add paid retest or remediation support to avoid unpaid rework.

    Retainers (Monthly / Quarterly)¶

    • Best for ongoing leadership, continuous monitoring, or program management.
    • vCISO retainers commonly $2k-$25k/month; DFIR retainers include prepaid hours.
    • Provides predictable cash flow and deeper client integration.
    • Include explicit SLA, meeting cadence, and deliverables per month.
    • Track unused hours and roll into tabletop exercises or roadmap updates.

    Value-Based / Outcome-Based¶

    • Price tied to business impact (audit readiness, risk reduction, revenue enablement).
    • Works well for compliance readiness, M&A due diligence, and transformation programs.
    • Requires quantified outcomes and a defined success metric.
    • Often paired with fixed-fee + bonus for milestone attainment.
    • Use a value-capture ratio (e.g., 10-30% of quantified value).

    Hybrid Models¶

    • Most common model in repo guidance: retainer + project add-ons.
    • Example: $7.5k/month vCISO + $15k SOC 2 readiness project.
    • Use hourly out-of-scope adders to prevent margin erosion.
    • Ideal for long-term accounts with periodic large deliverables.
    • Requires a clear boundary between retainer scope and project scope.

    Value-Based Pricing Framework¶

    Step 1: Identify the Value Driver¶

    • Compliance deadline avoidance (SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA).
    • Cost-of-breach reduction (average breach costs used as anchor).
    • Revenue enablement (closing enterprise deals or procurement clearance).
    • Operational savings (reduced manual security overhead).

    Step 2: Quantify Impact¶

    • Estimate avoided breach cost, audit delay costs, or lost deal revenue.
    • Use conservative ranges to maintain credibility.
    • Translate time savings into internal labor cost reduction.

    Step 3: Define the Outcome¶

    • Example: SOC 2 Type 1 readiness in 6 weeks.
    • Example: Reduce critical findings by 70% within 90 days.
    • Example: Zero trust architecture approved by security council.

    Step 4: Apply a Value Capture Ratio¶

    • Typical capture ratio: 10-30% of quantified value.
    • Higher ratios justified for urgent or scarce expertise.
    • Lower ratios for commoditized or repeatable work.

    Step 5: Package the Offer¶

    • Convert into a fixed-fee or hybrid proposal.
    • Include milestones with payment triggers.
    • Attach a premium retainer for ongoing governance.

    Value-Based Guardrails¶

    • Anchor with a baseline T&M estimate, then show the outcome premium.
    • Require discovery phase before committing to outcomes.
    • Use success criteria that are measurable and auditable.

    Rate Negotiation Playbook¶

    Pre-Negotiation Setup¶

    • Research specialization bands and local market ranges.
    • Determine your floor rate using 1099 premium math.
    • Define non-negotiables (minimum hours, payment terms, response SLAs).
    • Prepare 2-3 comparables (platform data, expert network bands).

    Anchoring Tactics¶

    • Start 15-20% above target to allow negotiation room.
    • Lead with outcomes and specialization scarcity.
    • Use tiered offers to prevent price-only comparison.

    Concession Strategy¶

    • Trade scope for price, not price for scope.
    • Offer schedule flexibility in exchange for rate retention.
    • Provide a limited-time discount tied to a retainer commitment.

    Defensive Pricing Moves¶

    • Add rush multipliers (1.5-2x) for urgent or weekend work.
    • Apply minimum engagement sizes (20-40 hours or 1-month retainer).
    • Use milestone payments to reduce risk and accelerate cash flow.

    Closing the Deal¶

    • Summarize value achieved, not hours delivered.
    • Reinforce that higher rates buy experienced delivery and lower risk.
    • Confirm payment terms and change-order process in writing.

    Rate Calculation & Fee Math¶

    Salary-to-Hourly Conversion¶

    • Base hourly = Annual salary / 2,080 hours.
    • Example: $150k salary $72/hour base.

    1099 Premium Formula¶

    • 1099 hourly floor = base hourly 1.3 to 1.4.
    • Rule of thumb: 173% of W-2 hourly rate.

    Utilization Adjustment¶

    • Loaded cost = base salary (1 + overhead) / 2,080.
    • Billable rate = loaded cost / utilization.
    • Target price = billable rate (1 + margin).

    Platform Net Rate Math¶

    • Net rate = Gross rate (1 - platform fee).
    • Upwork: gross $100 net $90 (10% fee).
    • Fiverr: gross $125 net $100 (20% fee).

    Retainer Pricing Math¶

    • Retainer = hourly anchor reserved hours 0.8-0.9 (commitment discount).
    • Add SLA premium for priority response.

    Risk, Urgency, and Premium Multipliers¶

    • Rush work (48-72 hour turnaround): 1.5x standard rate.
    • After-hours or weekend response: 1.5-2x standard rate.
    • Highly regulated industry (finance, healthcare): +20-50% premium.
    • Security clearance required: +15-30% premium.
    • On-site travel requirements: add travel fee + daily premium.
    • Long-term engagement discounts: 5-15% for 6+ month commitments.

    Payment Terms & Contract Guardrails¶

    • Require 25-50% deposits for project work.
    • Use milestone payments for multi-phase engagements.
    • Set Net 15 or Net 30 terms as default.
    • Add late-payment penalties and pause clauses.
    • Define scope, exclusions, and change-order rates upfront.
    • Include retainer minimums for expert witness work.

    Pricing Communication Scripts (Examples)¶

    • Our retainer includes 10 hours/month, with additional hours billed at $250/hour.

    • We can reduce scope to stay within budget, but the price is tied to deliverables.
    • Our pricing reflects the risk reduction outcome and the cost of delay.
    • We offer a fixed-fee package with a defined change-order process.

    Rate Review & Increase Schedule¶

    • Annual increase: 5-10% for retained clients.
    • Certification or specialization milestone: 10-20% rate bump.
    • Demand exceeds capacity: 20-30% increase for new clients.
    • Use new-client pricing to test higher rates before raising legacy clients.
    • Review utilization quarterly to adjust tier pricing.

    Pricing Decision Checklist¶

    • Have you anchored your rate to a validated market band?
    • Did you apply a 1099 premium to cover taxes and benefits?
    • Are platform fees accounted for in your gross rate?
    • Is the scope clearly defined with exclusions?
    • Did you include a change-order clause for out-of-scope work?
    • Are minimum engagement hours defined?
    • Do you have a retainer conversion path after project delivery?
    • Are payment terms Net 15/30 with deposit requirements?
    • Did you price for urgency, travel, or regulatory risk premiums?
    • Is there a clear success metric for value-based pricing?
