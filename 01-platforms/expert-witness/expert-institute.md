## Platform Overview
### Mission and Positioning
- Connects attorneys with vetted subject-matter experts
- Optimizes speed and quality of expert identification
- Emphasizes admissibility readiness and litigation credibility
- Operates as a managed marketplace with concierge matching
- Positions itself between traditional expert firms and DIY searching
- Supports plaintiff, defense, and neutral engagements

### Core Services
- Expert identification and shortlist delivery
- Credential verification and background checks
- Case scoping interviews and expert feasibility analysis
- Coordination for depositions, declarations, and trial testimony
- Ongoing expert support through project management
- Access to institutional knowledge and prior case learnings

### Client Segments
- AmLaw firms seeking rapid expert sourcing
- Boutique litigation firms needing specialized experts
- In-house legal teams with recurring expert demand
- Litigation finance partners assessing case merits
- Government entities with procurement requirements

### Expert Segments
- Academics with research authority
- Practitioners with applied industry experience
- Former regulators and compliance officers
- Engineers and scientists in niche fields
- Medical specialists with clinical practice history
- Economists and damages experts

### Geographic Reach
- Strongest presence in the US
- Access to Canadian and UK experts for cross-border disputes
- Select network in EU and APAC for multi-jurisdiction cases
- Remote testimony support for cost control

### Typical Engagement Types
- Advisory consults and case strategy calls
- Expert declarations and affidavits
- Deposition testimony and trial testimony
- Independent testing or analysis
- Class certification and damages modeling

### Platform Strengths
- Speed from intake to shortlist

### Platform Limitations

## Expert Witness Rates (0-2 1500/hr)
### Rate Band Overview
- 0-2 150/hr: Pro bono, junior academics, or consult-only support
- 150-2 300/hr: Early-career professionals, narrow consulting
- 300-2 500/hr: Mid-career specialists, typical consulting rate
- 500-2 750/hr: Senior practitioners, deposition-ready experts
- 750-2 1000/hr: Nationally recognized experts, high stakes
- 1000-2 1500/hr: Elite thought leaders, complex trials

### Role-Based Rates
- Consulting-only engagements typically lower than testimony roles
- Testifying experts command premium rates for credibility risk
- Non-testifying rebuttal experts sit mid-range
- Expert witnesses with MD/JD dual credentials command premiums
- Economists and damages experts trend higher

### Stage-Based Rates
- Case assessment calls billed at consulting rate
- Report drafting at standard hourly rate
- Deposition prep and testimony often higher
- Trial days priced with minimum hours or day rates

### Discipline Rate Signals
- Medical specialties vary widely by scarcity
- Highly regulated industries price above average
- Engineering and product design experts in demand
- Software and data science rates trending upward

### Regional Adjustments
- Major metros add 10-25% premiums
- Rural experts often price lower but may require travel
- Cross-border testimony includes travel and admin markups

### Minimums and Retainers
- Common minimums: 4-10 hours per month
- Retainers from $2K-20K depending on scope
- Cancellation fees for late deposition changes

### Budgeting Guidance
- Start with scope: consult vs testimony
- Add 20-230% buffer for discovery shifts
- Budget for document review time
- Include prep time for depositions and trial
- Track out-of-pocket expenses separately

### Negotiation Strategies
- Offer multi-case volume for rate concessions
- Bundle tasks into predictable milestones
- Request blended rates for varied tasks
- Secure capped fees for discrete deliverables
- Clarify billing increments and minimums

## Success Strategies for Law Firms
### Case Scoping
- Define expert role with precise questions
- Provide a clean case summary and timeline
- Identify jurisdiction-specific admissibility risks
- Clarify whether testimony will be required

### Intake Best Practices
- Provide key pleadings and allegations early
- Share discovery deadlines and trial dates
- Outline expected deliverables and format
- Identify budget ceiling and flexibility

### Expert Selection
- Prioritize clarity of communication over CV length
- Seek experts with prior testimony wins
- Avoid over-credentialing for simple issues
- Vet for conflicts and prior adverse testimony

### Managing Experts
- Align on scope and billing cadence
- Schedule check-ins for report milestones
- Prepare experts for deposition style questions
- Provide mock cross-exam if high stakes

### Daubert Readiness
- Pressure-test methodology before report
- Ensure citations are current and credible
- Keep opinions within expertise boundaries
- Document assumptions and data sources

### Budget Control
- Break scope into phases
- Use capped fees for discrete tasks
- Require monthly billing summaries
- Track travel costs separately

### Communication
- Assign a single point of contact
- Keep experts informed of case changes
- Respect expert time and scheduling needs
- Provide feedback on drafts quickly

## Success Strategies for Experts
### Positioning
- Maintain a clean, current CV
- Highlight litigation and regulatory experience
- Emphasize practical application of expertise
- Keep a list of prior testimony topics

### Engagement Readiness
- Respond quickly to initial inquiries
- Clarify scope and conflicts early
- Offer realistic timelines for deliverables
- Maintain professional liability coverage if needed

### Report Quality
- Use clear, concise language for non-technical readers
- Support opinions with peer-reviewed sources
- Document assumptions and limitations
- Avoid advocacy tone

### Deposition Preparedness
- Practice common cross-exam patterns
- Understand opposing counsel strategy
- Align on key phrases and definitions
- Stay within areas of expertise

### Business Discipline
- Track time in 0.1-hour increments
- Use consistent invoice formats
- State minimums and cancellation policies
- Maintain an engagement checklist

### Reputation Management
- Avoid overextension into unrelated fields
- Preserve independence in opinions
- Maintain a consistent ethical posture

## Red Flags and Risk Controls
- Expert insists on contingency fees
- Prior testimony conflicts with current position
- CV contains unverifiable claims
- Expert has active disciplinary action
- Expert overcommits without schedule clarity
- Reports show advocacy tone instead of analysis

## FAQs
### How fast can EI deliver a shortlist?
- Typical range is 2-2 7 days for standard matters
- Urgent cases may be expedited with premium fees

### Can EI supply multiple experts for a single matter?
- Yes, for liability, causation, and damages components
- Multi-expert packages require aligned scheduling

### What documentation is needed to start?
- A case summary, allegations, and relevant pleadings
- A list of deadlines and jurisdiction constraints

### How are conflicts managed?
- Experts complete conflict disclosures before engagement
- EI flags prior testimony that may be adverse

### Can experts be used for settlement support only?
- Yes, consulting-only roles are common
- These roles can reduce overall cost

### Are rates negotiable?
- Often negotiable based on volume and scope
- Elite experts may set fixed rates

### How is payment handled?
- Retainer and milestone billing are standard
- Payment terms vary by expert and client

### Do experts provide demonstratives?
- Many do, but this should be scoped early
- Additional fees may apply for visual aids

## Glossary
- Admissibility: Court acceptance of expert testimony
- Daubert: Standard for expert reliability in federal courts
- Frye: General acceptance standard in some states
- Retainer: Upfront funds held against future work
- Shortlist: Curated set of expert candidates
- Rebuttal expert: Expert responding to opposing testimony
- Consulting expert: Non-testifying subject-matter advisor
- Testifying expert: Expert who provides deposition or trial testimony
- Scheduling order: Court-ordered timeline of deadlines
- Work product: Materials protected as attorney work product
- Scope creep: Expansion of work beyond the original scope
- Blended rate: Single rate for varied tasks
- Minimum billable: Smallest time increment billed
- Disclosed expert: Expert identified to opposing parties
- Ghostwritten report: Improperly drafted opinion by others
