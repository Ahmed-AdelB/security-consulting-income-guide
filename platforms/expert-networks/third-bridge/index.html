Always follow Third Bridge guidance, your employer policies, and local regulations.

    Platform Overview¶

    Third Bridge is a global expert network connecting clients with industry specialists. The platform supports research-driven consultations across many sectors and regions. Engagements are typically short, high-impact conversations or targeted surveys. Third Bridge operates as an intermediary between clients and experts. Experts are engaged as independent contractors, not employees. Most engagements are scheduled in advance through a client request workflow. Some engagements are rapid-response with short lead times. Third Bridge supports multilingual and cross-border engagements. Experts are expected to provide practical, experience-based insights. Clients seek qualitative guidance, market context, and operational detail. Third Bridge is best known for its buy-side and strategy consulting client base. The company also runs a membership-based research product called Third Bridge Forum. Forum members can be called for quick feedback or deeper diligence projects. The platform typically handles payment processing, compliance, and scheduling. Experts receive payments after completing engagements and approval cycles.

    How Third Bridge Makes Money¶

    Third Bridge earns fees from client subscriptions and per-project usage. Clients typically pay a premium for access to curated experts. The platform charges higher rates for hard-to-find or senior experts. Forum subscriptions provide recurring revenue for access to research and expert calls. Project management and compliance vetting are part of the value proposition. Third Bridges margin comes from the spread between client bill rates and expert pay. This spread funds sourcing, screening, compliance, and customer support.

    Product Lines at a Glance¶

    • Expert calls and interviews
    • Third Bridge Forum research and membership
    • Surveys and quick polls
    • In-person or virtual roundtables
    • Custom diligence projects
    • Transcript and recording services where permitted
    • Thematic research briefings

    Typical Engagement Workflow¶

    • Client submits a project brief with target profile and discussion topics.
    • Third Bridge scouts, screens, and recruits matching experts.
    • Experts receive a request with topic scope and compliance checks.
    • Experts accept, decline, or request clarification.
    • Scheduling is coordinated by Third Bridge staff or automated tools.
    • Pre-call compliance is confirmed and documented.
    • The call is conducted, usually 30 to 60 minutes.
    • Post-call compliance attestation may be required.
    • Third Bridge collects client feedback and closes the project.
    • Expert payment is initiated based on completion and approvals.

    Third Bridge Forum Membership¶

    Third Bridge Forum is a subscription research platform and expert access channel. Forum members receive access to research reports, briefings, and expert call hours. Forum engagement can include scheduled calls and rapid-response requests. Forum clients often focus on investment diligence and market intelligence. Forum experts may be asked to respond quickly to short, targeted queries. Forum can include ongoing coverage on public and private companies. Forum coverage often clusters by sector, value chain, or business model. Forum membership is designed to be a repeat-use, high-touch client experience.

    Forum Membership Eligibility¶

    • Experts generally need credible, recent, and relevant industry experience.
    • Seniority and decision-making exposure are valued for Forum inclusion.
    • Reputable professional history and verifiable credentials are required.
    • Experts must pass compliance screenings and conflict checks.
    • Some sectors require stricter vetting due to regulatory risk.
    • Availability and responsiveness influence Forum invitations.
    • Membership may be limited in crowded sectors to maintain quality.

    Forum Membership Benefits¶

    • Higher visibility to high-activity clients.
    • More frequent call requests if coverage needs are ongoing.
    • Opportunity to shape broader thematic discussions.
    • Potentially higher rates for high-demand expertise.
    • Streamlined scheduling and repeat engagements.
    • Access to curated topic briefs that reduce prep time.
    • Professional relationship building with recurring clients.

    Forum Membership Obligations¶

    • Maintain accurate and updated professional profile information.
    • Respond to requests within stated time windows.
    • Avoid sharing confidential or nonpublic information.
    • Respect client timelines and agreed call durations.
    • Confirm compliance statements before each engagement.
    • Keep records of your own participation if required by employer.
    • Notify Third Bridge of any changes in employment or restrictions.

    Hourly Rate Landscape (0 to 1000+ per hour)¶

    Rates depend on role, seniority, scarcity, urgency, and client budget. Typical rates for many experts fall in the 150 to 500 per hour range. Highly specialized executives can command 600 to 1000+ per hour. Rates under 100 per hour can occur for surveys or junior roles. Some screening calls are unpaid and can be effectively 0 per hour. Longer or multi-session projects may use blended or discounted rates. Rates can vary by region, currency, and sector demand. Third Bridge may offer a rate, but experts can often negotiate.

    Rate Drivers and Negotiation Factors¶

    • Seniority and decision-making responsibility
    • Recency of experience in the relevant role
    • Uniqueness of the experience or niche expertise
    • Urgency of the client request and short lead times
    • Duration and complexity of the project
    • Value of a repeat relationship with a client
    • Compliance limitations that reduce candidate supply
    • Geographic market rates and time zone convenience
    • Past performance, feedback, and reliability

    Project Timelines and Intensity¶

    • Standard calls are scheduled within 2 to 7 days.
    • Urgent calls can be same-day or next-day.
    • Surveys may be sent with 24 to 72 hour response windows.
    • Multi-session projects can span weeks or months.
    • Some diligence sprints involve multiple calls in a single week.
    • Retainer-style advisory engagements are less common on networks.
    • Third Bridge may limit call length to enforce scope.

    Compliance Requirements Overview¶

    Third Bridge enforces strict compliance to protect clients and experts. Compliance is typically centered on confidentiality and material nonpublic information. Experts are expected to speak only from publicly available or personal experience. Experts should avoid disclosing any trade secrets or proprietary data. Third Bridge may use recorded compliance attestations before calls. Calls can be monitored or recorded when allowed by jurisdiction. Experts should keep records of approved engagements when required. Compliance standards can vary by region and by client type.

    Confidential Information and MNPI¶

    • Do not share material nonpublic information about public companies.
    • Do not disclose internal revenue figures that are not public.
    • Avoid forward-looking guidance not publicly disclosed.
    • Do not share confidential client lists or customer contracts.
    • Avoid discussing nonpublic pipeline or bidding details.
    • Use ranges or directional insights based on public signals.
    • Explain uncertainty and avoid precise numbers when uncertain.
    • If unsure, pause and ask the moderator for clarification.

    Conflicts of Interest and Independence¶

    • Do not advise on direct competitors if restricted by employer.
    • Disclose any personal or financial conflicts before the call.
    • Avoid overlapping consulting relationships that may bias answers.
    • Do not accept requests that violate non-compete obligations.
    • Confirm you can speak as an independent expert.

    Employer Consent and Restrictions¶

    • Review your employment contract and policies.
    • Some employers require pre-approval for external consulting.
    • Do not use employer devices or accounts if restricted.
    • Ensure the timing does not conflict with core job duties.
    • Separate personal email and payment details from employer systems.

    Personal Trading and Restricted Lists¶

    • Follow any personal trading policies for public equities.
    • Do not share nonpublic information that could be trading material.
    • Avoid discussing any trades or position sizes.
    • Clients are expected to use information ethically, but compliance is shared.

    Anti-Bribery and Gift Rules¶

    • Do not accept gifts or side payments beyond standard fees.
    • Do not promise outcomes or access to privileged information.
    • Avoid any quid-pro-quo arrangements.
    • Report suspicious requests to Third Bridge compliance.

    Data Privacy and Recording Rules¶

    • Confirm call recording policies in your jurisdiction.
    • Avoid sharing personal data about individuals.
    • Keep notes secure and consistent with privacy rules.
    • Do not forward client materials without permission.

    Engagement Success Strategies¶

    Profile Optimization Strategies¶

    • Use clear, outcome-oriented role descriptions.
    • Highlight PnL ownership, budget size, and decision impact.
    • List specific products, channels, and regions you know well.
    • Include measurable outcomes like growth rates or cost reductions.
    • Avoid confidential metrics or nonpublic details.
    • Keep dates and timelines current and accurate.
    • Add certifications or licenses where relevant.
    • Note languages and cross-border experience.
    • Explain niche expertise in 2 to 3 concise bullets.

    Screening and Scoping Strategies¶

    • Ask for a clear scope and exclusions before agreeing.
    • Confirm the exact company, market, or product focus.
    • Clarify what level of detail is expected.
    • Confirm whether any companies are off-limits due to conflicts.
    • Request an agenda if the scope is broad.
    • Ask about expected call duration and format.
    • If you are unsure about compliance, say so early.

    Call Delivery Strategies¶

    • Open with your role and relevant experience.
    • State time boundaries at the start of the call.
    • Provide structured answers with frameworks.
    • Distinguish between facts, estimates, and opinions.
    • Be explicit about the timeframe of your experience.
    • Avoid speculation outside your expertise.
    • Offer examples that are generalized and non-confidential.
    • Validate client assumptions and flag weak logic.
    • Keep answers concise and leave room for questions.
    • Stop immediately if a question feels non-compliant.

    Post-Call Follow-Up Strategies¶

    • Submit compliance attestations promptly.
    • Provide quick clarifications if requested and allowed.
    • Keep your internal notes brief and secure.
    • Track your hours and rates for personal records.
    • Ask for feedback to improve future calls.

    Real Consultant Earnings Scenarios¶

    Earnings are highly variable and not guaranteed. The examples below are illustrative and use simple math. All amounts are gross and do not include taxes or expenses.

    Scenario A: Occasional Expert¶

    • 2 calls per month
    • 45 minutes per call
    • 200 USD per hour
    • Monthly gross: 2 * 0.75 * 200 = 300 USD
    • Annual gross: 3,600 USD

    Scenario B: Active Expert¶

    • 6 calls per month
    • 60 minutes per call
    • 300 USD per hour
    • Monthly gross: 6 * 1.0 * 300 = 1,800 USD
    • Annual gross: 21,600 USD

    Scenario C: High-Demand Expert¶

    • 10 calls per month
    • 60 minutes per call
    • 500 USD per hour
    • Monthly gross: 10 * 1.0 * 500 = 5,000 USD
    • Annual gross: 60,000 USD

    Scenario D: Top-Tier Executive¶

    • 6 calls per month
    • 45 minutes per call
    • 900 USD per hour
    • Monthly gross: 6 * 0.75 * 900 = 4,050 USD
    • Annual gross: 48,600 USD

    Scenario E: Mixed Workload with Surveys¶

    • 4 calls per month at 300 USD per hour
    • 6 surveys per month at 35 USD each
    • Monthly gross: (4 * 1.0 * 300) + (6 * 35) = 1,410 USD
    • Annual gross: 16,920 USD

    Earnings Math and Utilization¶

    • Annual earnings = hourly rate * billable hours * utilization rate.
    • Utilization rate is actual billable time divided by availability.
    • Low utilization is common if your niche is narrow.
    • Cancellations reduce utilization without warning.
    • Short calls can feel less efficient due to prep time.

    Managing Cancellations and No-Shows¶

    • Ask about cancellation policies during scheduling.
    • Track your own cancellation rate for planning.
    • Build a pipeline to reduce reliance on single projects.
    • Confirm calendar holds and reminders before the call.

    Taxes, Invoicing, and Payment Tips¶

    • Track payments and dates for tax reporting.
    • Maintain a simple ledger with call length and rate.
    • Confirm whether payments are via wire, ACH, or PayPal.
    • Ensure your tax forms are current with the platform.
    • Set aside taxes based on your local requirements.

    Expert Lifecycle Checklist¶

    Onboarding¶

    • Complete your profile with accurate employment history.
    • Verify contact details and time zone.
    • Provide payment information and tax forms.
    • Review Third Bridge compliance policies.
    • Confirm employer approval if required.

    Pre-Call¶

    • Read the brief and verify scope.
    • Reject any request that conflicts with restrictions.
    • Confirm rate, duration, and format.
    • Prepare 3 to 5 key insights to share.
    • Plan a short structured overview.

    During Call¶

    • Open with role and relevance.
    • Confirm scope and compliance boundaries.
    • Keep answers within your experience timeframe.
    • Offer data-backed insights where possible.
    • Ask clarifying questions to focus the discussion.

    Post-Call¶

    • Submit compliance attestations.
    • Log call duration and topic for your records.
    • Follow up if clarification is requested and allowed.

    Sample Scripts and Templates¶

    Decline Script (Scope or Conflict)¶

    • Thank you for the invitation.
    • I have a potential conflict with this request.
    • I need to decline to remain compliant.
    • Please feel free to reach out for other topics.

    Accept Script (Confirming Scope)¶

    • I can help with this topic based on my experience.
    • Please confirm the focus on [topic] and exclude [restricted area].
    • I am available on [dates] for a [duration] call.

    Compliance Boundary Script¶

    • I can speak to general industry practices.
    • I cannot discuss nonpublic metrics or confidential details.
    • I will keep the discussion at a high level if needed.

    Rate Negotiation Script¶

    • Based on the scope and my seniority, my standard rate is [rate].
    • If this is urgent or highly specialized, I can prioritize at [rate].
    • Let me know if the budget allows this rate.

    Red Flags and Common Pitfalls¶

    • A request asks for confidential figures or future guidance.
    • The client pushes for internal KPIs not publicly disclosed.
    • The call description is vague or avoids compliance language.
    • The request seems to target a specific public company event.
    • You are asked to share internal documents or slides.
    • The client asks for customer names or contract terms.
    • You feel pressure to speculate beyond your experience.

    Deep Dive: Engagement Playbooks¶

    Playbook for Diligence Calls¶

    • Focus on market structure and competitive landscape.
    • Provide value chain mapping and key cost drivers.
    • Share typical unit economics in ranges.
    • Explain demand drivers and buying triggers.
    • Highlight regulatory or supply chain risks.

    Playbook for Product Calls¶

    • Outline customer pain points and buying criteria.
    • Compare product differentiation and feature gaps.
    • Discuss adoption timelines and integration challenges.
    • Provide insights on pricing sensitivity and packaging.
    • Mention switching costs and churn dynamics.

    Playbook for Go-to-Market Calls¶

    • Describe channel mix and partner strategies.
    • Discuss sales cycle length and procurement barriers.
    • Share typical sales enablement needs.
    • Explain the role of marketing versus outbound sales.
    • Outline common objections and buyer concerns.

    Playbook for Operations Calls¶

    • Describe throughput constraints and bottlenecks.
    • Discuss labor availability and productivity levers.
    • Explain inventory and working capital sensitivities.
    • Highlight quality metrics and common failure modes.
    • Describe automation or outsourcing trade-offs.

    Playbook for M&A Calls¶

    • Explain typical valuation drivers by segment.
    • Discuss integration risks and synergy capture.
    • Outline due diligence focus areas.
    • Describe common integration timelines and costs.
    • Highlight cultural or regulatory pitfalls.

    Quick Reference Matrices¶

    When to Increase Your Rate¶

    • You are uniquely qualified or hard to find.
    • The topic is urgent with short scheduling lead time.
    • The client wants multiple sessions or follow-ups.
    • The discussion touches niche regulatory or technical areas.
    • You are giving strategic guidance rather than tactical detail.

    When to Accept a Lower Rate¶

    • The engagement is low effort and straightforward.
    • You want to build a relationship with the client.
    • The topic is closely aligned with your expertise and easy to deliver.
    • The call is short and highly structured.

    When to Decline¶

    • The scope overlaps with confidential or restricted information.
    • The client insists on sensitive forward-looking details.
    • The project is outside your expertise and cannot be reframed.
    • You are uncomfortable with compliance implications.

    Detailed Compliance Checklist¶

    • Confirm you are not restricted by employment or contract.
    • Confirm you are not sharing nonpublic information.
    • Confirm you are not advising on personal trades.
    • Confirm you are not using insider knowledge about earnings.
    • Confirm you are not sharing customer or vendor names.
    • Confirm you are not disclosing confidential pricing terms.
    • Confirm you are not providing proprietary documents.
    • Confirm you are not speaking on behalf of your employer.
    • Confirm the call will not be recorded without consent.
    • Confirm you will stop if the discussion goes off-scope.

    Client Value Expectations¶

    • Clients want clarity on what drives outcomes in the sector.
    • Clients want practical metrics and ranges they can model.
    • Clients want a view on what is changing and why.
    • Clients want to know how real operators think and decide.
    • Clients want an honest perspective on risks and caveats.

    Third Bridge Forum: Practical Tips¶

    • Expect more repeat interactions over time.
    • Keep a personal knowledge base to answer common questions quickly.
    • Update your profile when you change roles.
    • Be proactive about availability if you want more requests.
    • Avoid overcommitting to maintain high quality.

    Call Structure Template¶

    • 2 minutes: introductions and scope confirmation.
    • 5 minutes: your background and relevance.
    • 20 minutes: core discussion guided by client questions.
    • 5 minutes: summary and key takeaways.
    • 3 minutes: compliance wrap-up and next steps.

    Note on Surveys and Written Responses¶

    Surveys can be a low-effort way to stay active on the platform. Survey rates are lower but require minimal scheduling. Written responses should stay within compliance boundaries. Do not share documents or attachments unless approved.

    Frequently Asked Questions¶

    How quickly do calls get scheduled¶

    • Many calls are scheduled within a week.
    • Urgent requests can be scheduled faster.

    Can I request a higher rate¶

    • Yes, rates are often negotiable.
    • Be clear about the value and urgency.

    Can I decline a request¶

    • Yes, you can decline at any time before scheduling.
    • Decline if compliance is uncertain.

    Do I need to prepare slides¶

    • Typically no, unless explicitly requested and approved.
    • Keep responses verbal and compliant.

    Will my calls be recorded¶

    • Recording rules vary by jurisdiction and client.
    • You should be informed if a call is recorded.

    Glossary of Expert Network Terms¶

    • Expert network: A platform connecting clients with specialists.
    • Expert call: A scheduled consultation with a subject matter expert.
    • Diligence: Research to validate an investment or strategy.
    • Forum: Subscription research service with expert access.
    • MNPI: Material nonpublic information.
    • Compliance attestation: Confirmation that rules were followed.
    • Screening call: A short call to confirm fit and scope.
    • Survey: A structured questionnaire for quick insights.
    • Panel: Multiple experts on a shared call.
    • Transcript: A written record of the call, if permitted.
    • Curated list: A vetted list of experts for a project.
    • B2B diligence: Commercial assessment of a business model.
    • KPI: Key performance indicator.
    • Value chain: The flow of inputs to customer delivery.
    • PnL: Profit and loss responsibility.
    • Conflict: A restriction due to employer or contract.
    • Non-compete: Contractual restriction on competitive activities.
    • NDA: Non-disclosure agreement.
    • SOW: Statement of work for project scope.
    • Rate card: Typical pricing by seniority or role.
    • Utilization: Share of availability used for paid work.
    • No-show: A canceled or missed call without notice.
    • Follow-up: Additional clarifications after the call.
    • Expert brief: A short summary of the project topic.
    • Rapid response: Short-lead call request.
    • Lead time: Time between request and call.
    • Eligibility: Criteria to be selected for projects.
    • Onboarding: Process to verify identity and qualifications.
    • Compliance script: Standard language to set boundaries.
    • Recency: How recent the relevant experience is.
    • Scarcity: How rare the expertise is in the market.
    • Buy-side: Investment firms such as PE, HF, and VC.
    • Sell-side: Banks, brokers, and advisory firms.
    • Channel checks: Interviews about sales channels or vendors.
    • Customer references: Discussions about buyer behavior.
    • Operational benchmarking: Comparing metrics across companies.
    • Sourcing: Finding qualified experts for a project.
    • Briefing call: A short call to clarify scope.
    • Rate negotiation: Adjusting compensation based on value.
    • Compliance review: The internal process to assess risk.
    • Expert availability: The time you can dedicate to calls.
    • Timeboxing: Limiting call duration to agreed time.
    • Follow-on project: Additional work after the first call.
    • Thematic coverage: Ongoing research on a topic area.
    • Decision rights: The authority you had in a role.
    • Data hygiene: Keeping records accurate and secure.
