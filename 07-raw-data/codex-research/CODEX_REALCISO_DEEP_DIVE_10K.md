## Executive Summary
- RealCISO is a purpose-built marketplace and delivery platform for virtual CISO services.
- It connects organizations with vetted security leaders and provides tooling to run engagements.
- The platform combines discovery, matching, scoping, delivery management, and reporting.
- Differentiation comes from a data-driven matching engine and outcome-oriented playbooks.
- Revenue is driven by marketplace fees, subscriptions, and optional managed service layers.
- Success hinges on trust, measurable security outcomes, and repeatable delivery workflows.

## Platform Overview
### Mission
- Make executive-grade security leadership accessible to organizations of any size.
- Reduce buyer uncertainty through transparent expertise, pricing, and outcomes.
- Standardize delivery so vCISO services are efficient, repeatable, and scalable.

### Vision
- Become the most trusted marketplace for fractional security leadership.
- Provide a full-stack operating layer for vCISOs and their clients.
- Enable resilient security programs for the underserved mid-market.

### Value Proposition
- Clients gain immediate access to seasoned leaders without long-term hiring risk.
- Providers get qualified demand, reduced sales friction, and delivery infrastructure.
- Partners gain a scalable channel to bundle security leadership with their offerings.

### Platform Pillars
- Marketplace: discovery, profiles, and vetting of vCISO providers.
- Matching: data-driven alignment of client needs to provider expertise.
- Delivery: program management, assessment tools, and reporting templates.
- Insights: benchmarks, maturity scoring, and risk posture tracking.
- Commerce: proposals, contracts, billing, and compliance documentation.

### Positioning Statement
- RealCISO is the operating system for modern, fractional security leadership.
- It blends marketplace matchmaking with robust delivery workflows and governance.
- It emphasizes measurable outcomes over vanity assessments.

### Primary Customer Segments
- SMB and mid-market organizations lacking full-time security leadership.
- Regulated startups preparing for compliance or fundraising events.
- MSPs and technology partners expanding their security portfolios.
- High-growth firms needing interim leadership during hiring transitions.

### Core Outcomes Delivered
- Faster security program initialization and maturity improvements.
- Reduced audit and compliance friction through structured deliverables.
- Improved risk prioritization and board-level visibility.
- Stronger security governance without hiring a full-time CISO.

## Market Context and Problem Space
### Why vCISO Demand Is Rising
- Full-time CISO talent is scarce and expensive for smaller organizations.
- Regulatory pressure and customer security requirements are increasing.
- Fundraising and enterprise sales cycles require credible security leadership.
- Security incidents demand leadership beyond basic IT management.

### Buyer Pain Points
- Difficulty validating vendor expertise and fit.
- Lack of clarity on what a vCISO engagement delivers.
- Uncertain pricing and value alignment.
- Fragmented tooling and ad hoc reporting.

### Provider Pain Points
- High cost of customer acquisition and long sales cycles.
- Inconsistent client readiness and unclear scope.
- Administrative overhead for proposals, reporting, and billing.
- Limited access to industry benchmarks and playbooks.

### Competitive Landscape
- Traditional security consultancies offer expensive project engagements.
- MSSPs focus on tooling and monitoring rather than leadership.
- Freelancer marketplaces lack governance and delivery infrastructure.
- Boutique vCISO firms scale slowly without matching engines or automation.

## Client Matching and Intake Engine
### Intake Workflow
- Client selects engagement goals and urgency level.
- Platform captures organization size, industry, and tech stack.
- Risk profile gathered via maturity survey and incident history.
- Budget range and engagement timeline collected.
- Preferred communication cadence and leadership style indicated.

### Matching Inputs
- Industry fit and regulatory exposure.
- Desired frameworks and compliance targets.
- Required leadership experience and board communication skills.
- Geographic and time zone alignment.
- Budget compatibility and minimum engagement thresholds.

### Matching Methodology
- Weighted scoring across expertise, fit, and availability.
- Rule-based exclusions for conflicts of interest.
- Dynamic adjustments based on provider workload and response time.
- Human review for high-complexity or regulated clients.

### Match Output
- Shortlist of 3 to 5 providers with rationale.
- Comparative view of pricing, experience, and focus areas.
- Suggested engagement model and expected timeline.
- Option to schedule introductory calls directly in-platform.

### Client Matching Experience
- Transparent explanation of why each provider fits.
- Ability to adjust preferences and re-run match logic.
- Guardrails to prevent under-scoping or unrealistic timelines.

### Provider Matching Experience
- New lead summary with priority indicators.
- Suggested discovery questions based on intake responses.
- Proposal templates tailored to the client’s context.

## Pricing Models and Monetization
### Pricing Model Overview
- Subscription retainers for ongoing leadership and governance.
- Project-based engagements for assessments or compliance readiness.
- Hourly consulting for advisory or emergency response.
- Outcome-based pricing for defined deliverables or compliance milestones.

### Subscription Retainer Tiers
- Essentials: 5 to 10 hours per month, lightweight governance.
- Growth: 10 to 20 hours per month, policy and roadmap support.
- Regulated: 20 to 40 hours per month, compliance and audit prep.
- Enterprise: custom scope with executive reporting and program buildout.

### Typical Retainer Range
- Essentials: $2,500 to $5,000 monthly.
- Growth: $5,000 to $10,000 monthly.
- Regulated: $10,000 to $20,000 monthly.
- Enterprise: $20,000+ monthly.

### Project-Based Pricing
- Security maturity assessment: $7,500 to $20,000.
- Compliance readiness sprint: $15,000 to $40,000.
- Incident response planning: $10,000 to $25,000.
- Vendor risk program setup: $12,000 to $30,000.

### Outcome-Based Pricing
- SOC 2 Type I readiness with milestones and evidence collection.
- ISO 27001 stage 1 readiness and internal audit support.
- Board-level risk program delivered with defined reporting cadence.

### Platform Monetization
- Marketplace success fee on closed engagements.
- Subscription fee for premium discovery or analytics tools.
- Paid add-ons for templates, policy libraries, and risk scoring.
- Enterprise partnerships with MSPs and compliance vendors.

### Pricing Transparency Principles
- Clear scope boundaries and included deliverables.
- Visible assumptions, dependencies, and client responsibilities.
- Optional add-ons clearly priced and scheduled.

## Client Experience Journey
### Phase 1: Discovery
- Client completes intake and maturity survey.
- Platform explains recommended engagement models.
- Client receives shortlist and provider comparisons.

### Phase 2: Matching
- Introductory calls scheduled with top providers.
- Provider proposes scope, timeline, and success criteria.
- Client selects final provider and signs engagement.

### Phase 3: Assessment
- Provider performs risk and compliance assessment.
- Baseline maturity score and critical gaps documented.
- Initial roadmap and executive summary delivered.

### Phase 4: Program Build
- Policies, governance structures, and priorities established.
- Risk register created and ownership assigned.
- Security roadmap milestones tracked.

### Phase 5: Ongoing Leadership
- Monthly or quarterly reporting for executives and boards.
- Continuous risk management and roadmap adjustments.
- Support for audits, vendor security reviews, and incident response.

### Phase 6: Renewal or Transition
- Engagement success metrics reviewed.
- Options for renewal, transition to full-time CISO, or downgrade.
- Offboarding checklist and documentation handoff.
