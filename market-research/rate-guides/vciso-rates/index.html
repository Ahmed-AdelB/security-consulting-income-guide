    Engagement Structures¶

    Retainer Model (Most Common)¶

    • Fixed monthly fee
    • Set number of hours
    • Predictable revenue
    • Best for: Established relationships

    Example:

    $8,000/month for 20 hours of vCISO services = $400/hr effective rate Overages billed at $450/hr

    Hourly Model¶

    • Flexible engagement
    • Lower commitment
    • Variable income
    • Best for: New clients, project work

    Example:

    $400/hr, minimum 10 hours/month Monthly range: $4,000-$8,000

    Project-Based Model¶

    • Fixed scope and price
    • Higher effective rate possible
    • Risk of scope creep
    • Best for: Specific deliverables

    Example:

    SOC 2 Readiness Assessment: $25,000 Timeline: 6-8 weeks Effective rate: $500-$650/hr

    Rate Negotiation¶

    Setting Your Rate¶

    1. Calculate your floor:
    2. Target annual income / (hours per year available)

    3. Example: $300,000 / 1,500 hours = $200/hr minimum

    4. Add overhead and profit:

    5. Insurance, tools, training: +15-20%
    6. Profit margin: +20-30%

    7. Result: $260-$300/hr target

    8. Adjust for market:

    9. Compare to platform rates
    10. Consider your differentiators
    11. Factor in competition

    Negotiation Scripts¶

    Initial Proposal:

    "For a comprehensive vCISO engagement covering security program oversight, policy development, and compliance preparation, my standard retainer is $12,000/month for approximately 25 hours. This includes executive reporting, board presentations, and on-call availability during business hours."

    Handling Price Objections:

    "I understand budget constraints. We could structure a smaller engagement starting at $6,000/month focused on [specific priority]. As you see value, we can expand the scope."

    Value Justification:

    "The average cost of a data breach for companies your size is $3.9 million. My role is to reduce that risk significantly while ensuring you can close deals that require security attestation. Most clients see ROI within the first quarter when they close deals that required SOC 2."

    Contract Essentials¶

    Key Terms to Include¶

    • Scope of services: Detailed deliverables
    • Hours/availability: Monthly hours, response times
    • Exclusions: What's NOT included
    • Term: 12-month minimum recommended
    • Termination: 30-60 day notice
    • Rate escalation: Annual increase clause (3-5%)
    • Liability cap: Limit exposure
    • Insurance requirements: E&O, cyber liability

    Red Flags to Avoid¶

    • Clients wanting unlimited hours
    • No contract or informal agreements
    • Scope that includes hands-on IT work
    • Expectations of 24/7 availability without premium
    • Fixed price for open-ended compliance projects

    Getting Started¶

    Prerequisites¶

    • 10+ years security experience
    • Management/leadership experience
    • Relevant certifications (CISSP, CISM)
    • Professional liability insurance
    • LLC or business entity
    • Contract templates
    • Service descriptions and pricing sheet

    Building Client Base¶

    1. Start with 1-2 platform clients
    2. Build references and case studies
    3. Add direct clients for higher margins
    4. Transition to 70% direct / 30% platform
