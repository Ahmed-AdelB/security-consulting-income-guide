    EXPERT NETWORK PLATFORMS: COMPREHENSIVE RESEARCH REPORT 2025¶

    EXECUTIVE SUMMARY¶

    The expert network industry has surpassed $2.5 billion in 2024 with a 9% growth rate. This comprehensive report analyzes the top 10 expert network platforms based on real user experiences, payment terms, hourly rates, work volume, and overall satisfaction.

    Key Findings:

    • Hourly rates range from $100-$5,000, with typical rates of $250-$500/hour
    • Payment timelines vary from 3-75 days depending on platform
    • Work volume is inconsistent ("feast or famine") across all platforms
    • The "Big Five" leaders are: GLG, AlphaSights, Third Bridge, Guidepoint, and AlphaSense-Tegus
    • User satisfaction ratings vary significantly (2.3/5 to 4.9/5 on Trustpilot)

    PLATFORM-BY-PLATFORM ANALYSIS¶

    1. GLG (Gerson Lehrman Group)¶

    Overview:

    • Founded: 1998
    • Size: World's largest expert network with 1.2M+ professionals
    • Offices: 13 countries
    • Market Position: Industry leader, most established

    Hourly Rates:

    • Entry-level professionals: $150-$300/hour
    • Mid-career (VP level): $400-$700/hour
    • C-level executives: $1,000+/hour
    • Surveys: $40-$70 for 15 minutes
    • Special consultations: Some experts report $300+ for 10 minutes

    Rate Reality:

    • Experts set their own rates via online calculator (annual salary รท 2,000 hours)
    • GLG is "notorious for being stingy on rates" - difficult to land projects at $500+/hour
    • Rate changes require support ticket (difficult to increase later)
    • Clients pay $1,000+/hour while experts receive $300-500/hour (GLG keeps the spread)

    Payment Speed:

    • Timeline: 7-14 days via direct deposit
    • Method: Bank transfer (no fees)
    • Reliability: Generally reliable with few complaints

    Work Volume:

    • High volume of opportunities due to massive network
    • Both 1-on-1 calls and frequent survey invites
    • Steady flow but competitive selection process

    User Satisfaction:

    • Trustpilot: 4.6/5 (228-236 reviews)
    • Rating Breakdown: 76% 5-star, 16% 1-star
    • Positive: Professional staff, easy payment, good mobile app
    • Negative: Rate negotiations difficult, some project mismatches

    Red Flags:

    • Difficult to negotiate higher rates once set
    • Some experts report skill mismatches leading to unproductive calls
    • Region-specific surveys with late-stage filtration

    Tips for Success:

    • Set competitive initial rate (hard to change later)
    • Respond quickly to invitations (24-48 hour decision window)
    • Focus on recent, specific expertise

    Sources:

    • GLG Consulting Review - Expert Network Offers High Hourly Rates
    • How much do clients pay GLG for an hour with an expert? - Quora
    • GLG Network Members Info

    2. AlphaSights¶

    Overview:

    • Size: One of the "Big Five," 2,000+ employees (2025)
    • Market Position: Premium tier, known for speed and efficiency
    • Offices: Major financial hubs globally (NYC, SF, London, Hong Kong, Seoul)

    Hourly Rates:

    • New consultants: $150-$300/hour
    • Experienced professionals: $250-$500/hour
    • Senior executives: $700-$900/hour
    • C-suite: $900-$1,200/hour
    • Elite (former CEOs, noted speakers): $1,000-$1,800+/hour
    • Experts set their own rates completely

    Rate Reality:

    • Clients pay $1,000-$1,500/hour (experts receive $250-$500 typically)
    • Fully self-determined rates accepted
    • Price difference between client and expert rates contributes to AlphaSights profit

    Payment Speed:

    • Processed promptly and reliably
    • Timeline: Generally within 7-14 days
    • Consistently praised for payment efficiency

    Work Volume:

    • "Feast or famine" - inconsistent
    • Less than 25% of onboarded experts are selected for calls (per Glassdoor employee reviews)
    • No guarantee of work even after qualification
    • High-demand experts on hot topics may get many requests

    User Satisfaction:

    • Trustpilot: 4.9/5 (~8,000 reviews) - HIGHEST RATED
    • Positive: Professional staff, seamless scheduling, prompt payment
    • Negative: Aggressive recruiting methods, "glorified call center" criticism from employees
    • Concerns: Some Reddit users report "unprofessional" outreach

    Red Flags:

    • Aggressive recruiting tactics reported
    • Employee reviews note "less than 25% of experts you onboard are selected"
    • Inconsistent work flow
    • Some describe outreach as feeling like "a scam" initially

    Tips for Success:

    • Start at $150-$300, raise to $500 after good reviews
    • Specialists with notable credentials can push to $1,000+
    • Respond rapidly to opportunities
    • Build reputation over time

    Employee Perspective (Glassdoor):

    • Pros: Start-up culture, free snacks, easy to make friends
    • Cons: "No hard skills," "limited exit opportunities," "way below market salary"

    Sources:

    • AlphaSights Review - Can You Really Earn $500+ an Hour?
    • AlphaSights Review: How are Consultants Really Treated?
    • Alphasights Review - Making $500 for a Phone Call

    3. Guidepoint¶

    Overview:

    • Size: 1.75M+ advisors globally
    • Employees: 1,000+ across 16 offices on 4 continents
    • Market Position: One of "Big Five," competitive pricing

    Hourly Rates:

    • Early career: $100-$200/hour
    • Mid-career (VP level): $400-$700/hour
    • C-level executives: $1,000+/hour
    • Industry surveys: $40-$80 for 10-20 minutes
    • One consultant reported: Started at $300/hour, quickly moved to $500/hour

    Rate Reality:

    • Rates vary by specialty, experience, supply/demand
    • Generally competitive with other networks
    • Flexible on negotiation

    Payment Speed:

    • Timeline: Within 1-2 weeks typically
    • Method: Direct deposit
    • Issue: ONLY paid for time on phone (no "waiting time" if client is late)

    Work Volume:

    • High volume of invitations
    • Requires long questionnaires for each request
    • Multiple brokers may send same request
    • Many invitations don't result in paid calls

    User Satisfaction:

    • Trustpilot: 3.2/5 (592 reviews) - LOWEST OF BIG FIVE
    • Glassdoor (Employee): 3.4/5 (Consultants rate compensation 2.6/5)
    • Positive: Professional staff, user-friendly platform, transparent communication
    • Negative: Payment delays reported, long questionnaires, many unproductive matches

    Red Flags:

    • Long questionnaires with no guarantee of paid call
    • Same request from multiple brokers (inefficient)
    • Payment only for actual call time (not waiting if client is late)
    • Some consultants give up after 3-5 unproductive attempts
    • Payment delay complaints

    Tips for Success:

    • Be selective with questionnaire responses
    • Negotiate higher rates after initial success
    • Manage expectations on conversion rate

    Sources:

    • Guidepoint Global Advisors Review
    • Guidepoint Reviews - Trustpilot
    • A GuidePoint Expert Network Review

    4. Third Bridge¶

    Overview:

    • Market Position: One of "Big Five"
    • Known for: Analyst-led interviews, transcript library
    • Model: Pay-as-you-go (PAYG) and fixed fee options

    Hourly Rates:

    • Manager-level: $110-$350/hour
    • Specialists/Directors: $350-$550/hour
    • Senior executives: $550-$750/hour
    • C-level: $750-$1,000+/hour
    • General range most experts: $250-$500/hour
    • Associates target: $100-$500/hour (above $500 impacts their KPIs)

    Rate Reality:

    • Associates negotiate rates with experts
    • One employee review: "Third Bridge advised us to say anything we wanted to keep their rate as low as possible"
    • Higher expert rates = lower individual pay for associates (creates conflict of interest)
    • Fixed hourly rate model available

    Payment Speed:

    • Standard industry timeline
    • Method: PAYG model charges per interaction
    • Reliability: Generally reliable

    Work Volume:

    • Sporadic and inconsistent
    • Typical project: 1-hour call
    • No guarantees of work
    • Some consultants get steady projects, others occasional

    User Satisfaction:

    • Trustpilot: 4.1/5 (93 reviews)
    • Glassdoor (Employee): 3.6/5 overall
    • Positive: Work from home, flexible hours, decent pay when calls happen
    • Negative: Fresh graduates managing projects, high turnover, "call center" atmosphere

    Red Flags:

    • Associates incentivized to negotiate expert rates DOWN
    • "Bottom line is all that matters"
    • Employees described as "expendable"
    • High workload for employees suggests pressure on experts

    Tips for Success:

    • Negotiate firmly on rates (don't accept lowball offers)
    • Expect inconsistent work flow
    • Specialize in niche areas for better rates

    Employee Perspective:

    • Pros: Competitive bonuses, rapid salary increases, international office opportunities
    • Cons: Inexperienced managers, high pressure, excessive workload

    Sources:

    • Third Bridge Expert Network Review
    • Third Bridge Review - Expert Network Consulting
    • Third Bridge Glassdoor Reviews

    5. Dialectica¶

    Overview:

    • Founded: 2015
    • Size: 900+ employees
    • Offices: Athens, London, New York, Montreal
    • Market Position: Global Leader (Inex One 2024 upgrade)

    Hourly Rates:

    • Entry-level: $150/hour minimum
    • Standard: $300-$500/hour
    • Elite experts (former CEO, book authors): Up to $1,000/hour
    • Screening calls: NOT compensated

    Rate Reality:

    • Market is "fairly efficient" - keep rates competitive with other networks
    • Easy to increase from $150 to $300-$500 with experience
    • Impressive credentials can justify $1,000/hour

    Payment Speed:

    • Timeline: 10 business days (updated from previous 3-7 days)
    • Method: Direct bank transfer
    • Reliability: Generally reliable, consistently on time

    Work Volume:

    • Growing volume due to 2024 market leader status
    • Some complaints about "project spam" - frequent requests off-topic
    • Generally aligned projects

    User Satisfaction:

    • Trustpilot: 4.7/5 (2,112 reviews) - VERY HIGH
    • Positive: Clear communication, project alignment, user-friendly platform, transparency
    • Negative: Frequent off-topic consulting requests (spam)

    Red Flags:

    • Payment timeline extended from 3-7 days to 10 days
    • Project spam for off-topic consultations
    • Screening calls not compensated

    Recent Developments:

    • 2024: Upgraded to Global Leader by Inex One
    • 2025: Hired former Tegus Chief Compliance Officer
    • Heavy investment in content and products for large investment managers

    Tips for Success:

    • Keep rates aligned with other networks
    • Build confidence to increase from $150 to $300-$500
    • Leverage strong credentials for premium rates

    Sources:

    • Dialectica Review - High Paying Expert Network
    • Dialectica Expert Network Review
    • Dialectica Trustpilot Reviews

    6. Coleman Research (VISASQ/COLEMAN)¶

    Overview:

    • Acquired: August 2021 by VisasQ for $102M
    • Size: 650,000+ experts globally (175,000 in Japan)
    • Employees: 600+ across 7 offices (NYC, Raleigh, Boston, SF, LA, London, Hong Kong)

    Hourly Rates:

    • Entry-level (HR Director): $150-$200/hour
    • IT Decision-maker: $250+/hour
    • CEO: $500+/hour
    • Self-determined based on experience and complexity

    Rate Reality:

    • Experts choose their own hourly rate
    • Based on industry standards and relative experience
    • More complex projects = higher rates
    • Competitive payment structure

    Payment Speed:

    • Timeline: Quick and easy (billing starts almost immediately after call)
    • Method: Direct deposit
    • Reliability: Consistently praised as "quick and easy"

    Work Volume:

    • BIGGEST COMPLAINT: Not enough work
    • No guarantees of project selection
    • Projects sometimes fall through
    • Inconsistent opportunities

    User Satisfaction:

    • Trustpilot: 210 reviews
    • Glassdoor (Employee): 3.8/5 (63% would recommend)
    • Positive: Efficient processes, quick payment, well-organized, transparency
    • Negative: Insufficient work volume, lack of feedback, client cancellations

    Red Flags:

    • Lack of work is the primary complaint
    • No guarantee of ever being selected
    • Projects can fall through even after selection
    • Limited feedback to experts

    Tips for Success:

    • Register but don't rely on this as primary income
    • Set competitive rates based on credentials
    • Maintain presence on multiple networks

    Sources:

    • Coleman Research Review - Expert Network Consulting
    • VISASQ/COLEMAN Trustpilot Reviews
    • Coleman Research Glassdoor Reviews

    7. Maven¶

    Overview:

    • Self-described: "World's largest micro consulting company"
    • Size: 1 million+ professionals in 200+ countries
    • Model: Direct expert-client matching

    Hourly Rates:

    • Claims: "Up to 4x your hourly rates"
    • Self-determined by experts
    • Variable based on expertise

    Rate Reality:

    • Experts set own rates
    • Receives confirmation of compensation after call
    • Payment via bank transfer, PayPal, or prepaid credit card

    Payment Speed:

    • Timeline: Variable, some delays reported
    • Method: Bank transfer, PayPal, prepaid credit card
    • Reliability: MIXED - significant concerns

    Work Volume:

    • Positive: "Steady feed of opportunities for paid surveys and expert opinions"
    • Negative: Some experts had "zero consults in a whole year with 50+ survey invitations that didn't work"
    • Much smaller project volume than GLG or Guidepoint

    User Satisfaction:

    • Trustpilot: 4.0/5 (3.9/5 on some pages)
    • G2: Mixed reviews
    • Positive (Dec 2024): "Steady feed of opportunities"
    • Negative: Payment delays, survey payment issues, lack of transparency

    Red Flags - SEVERE:

    • Payment delays: "Told there are delays for months"
    • Clawbacks: "Actually took away money they had already paid due to vague claims responses had not met company standards"
    • Survey issues: Completed surveys deemed "unqualified" after completion = no payment
    • One expert comparison: "GLG, Guidepoint, Third Bridge - all professional. Maven had zero consults in a year"
    • February 2025 review: "Terrible company, not transparent"

    Tips for Success:

    • Use with extreme caution
    • Don't rely as primary platform
    • Document all communications
    • Consider avoiding based on payment issues

    8. Tegus¶

    Overview:

    • Founded: 2016 by two brothers (finance background, AlphaSights alumni)
    • Location: Chicago (relocated from San Francisco in 2018)
    • Acquisition: June 2024 by AlphaSense for $930M
    • Model: Expert calls + transcript library (unique)

    Hourly Rates:

    • Young professionals/lower managers: $100-$200/hour
    • Mid-level (Directors, VPs): $200-$500/hour
    • Senior executives (EVPs, SVPs, C-level): $500+/hour
    • 2025 typical ranges: Mid-level $350-$500, Senior $500-$700, Former executives $700-$1,000
    • Initial outreach: $250-$300/hour (negotiable higher)

    Rate Reality:

    • Clients pay $500-$600/call (as of June 2024)
    • Subscription model: $20-25K/user/year + $550/published call
    • 2024 price increase after AlphaSense acquisition
    • Transparent hourly rates displayed in expert profiles

    Payment Speed:

    • Method: USD payment, 1099NEC if over $600/year
    • Timeline: Standard processing
    • Issues: Multiple payment complaints

    Work Volume:

    • Typical: 45-60 minute calls
    • No preparation or follow-up required
    • Inconsistent project flow

    UNIQUE MODEL CONCERNS:

    • Call transcripts published to entire client base
    • First client pays full rate, subsequent clients access for free
    • No residuals for experts
    • "Tegus gets subscription fees, I get nothing - no reason to hire me again"

    User Satisfaction:

    • Trustpilot: 2.3/5 (6 reviews) - LOWEST OVERALL
    • Severe complaints about business practices

    RED FLAGS - CRITICAL:

    • Project cancellations: "Full process completed, then 'client priorities shifted' - zero compensated calls"
    • No payment for client cancellations: "Client cancels, they will not pay you, even if waiting 15 minutes"
    • Partial payment: "Received email stating only half the agreed amount - paid only for time in call"
    • Transcript sharing raises competitive concerns
    • High project cancellation rate with no compensation

    Tips for Success:

    • Negotiate payment terms clearly upfront
    • Confirm payment for cancellations
    • Be aware transcripts become public to all Tegus clients
    • Consider impact of transcript library on future opportunities

    Sources:

    • Tegus Expert Network Review
    • Tegus Consulting Review
    • Tegus Trustpilot Reviews

    • "More than a few complaints about not sending payment at all"

    Recent User Feedback:

    • "Payment takes some time"

    • "Payment cycle has exceeded the initially promised four weeks"

    Positive Experiences:

    • "Great experience, coordination was fast, clear, professional"
    • "Process from appointment to interview to payment went smooth"

    Tips for Success:

    • Expect 4+ week payment delays

    • Follow up proactively on payments
    • Good platform if you can tolerate payment delays

    TIPS FOR MAXIMIZING INCOME¶

    1. Speed & Responsiveness (CRITICAL)¶

    • 24-48 Hour Window: Most decisions happen within first 24-48 hours of project posting
    • Set up notifications for all expert network emails
    • Respond within hours, not days - timing more important than most realize
    • Late responses significantly reduce selection chances even with strong qualifications

    2. Optimize Your Profile¶

    • Different from resume/LinkedIn: Focus on what you KNOW, not what you CAN DO
    • Associates spend 10 seconds scanning profiles - make it SIMPLE
    • Recent & specific beats broad & dated
    • Use keywords - AI-powered selection increasingly common in 2025
    • Update regularly with current experience

    3. Strategic Pricing¶

    • Start competitive: $150-$300 for new consultants, $300-$500 for experienced
    • Test higher rates after building reviews: $500-$750
    • Elite credentials justify $1,000+ (former CEO, book author, TedX speaker, unique expertise)
    • Don't undersell: Clients pay $1,000-$1,500+ to networks; your $300 becomes their $1,200
    • GLG caveat: Hard to change rates later, set carefully initially
    • Remember value: Portfolio managers making million-dollar decisions will pay for quality

    4. Multiple Networks Strategy¶

    • Register on 3-5 networks minimum
    • Top combinations: GLG + AlphaSights + Dialectica + Guidepoint
    • Avoid relying on single platform - work is "feast or famine" everywhere
    • Keep rates consistent across networks (market is efficient)

    5. During Calls - Maximize Value¶

    • Go off-script: Clients appreciate insights they haven't considered
    • Don't be afraid to disagree: They pay premium for honest expertise, not yes-men
    • Brief explanations + examples work best
    • Focus on recent, firsthand experience
    • Be honest about limitations - builds trust for future calls

    6. Build Reputation¶

    • First calls critical: Good reviews lead to more opportunities
    • Be selective: Better to decline than underperform
    • Stick to expertise: Don't stretch background for more projects
    • Consistency matters: Associates trained to spot inconsistencies

    7. Tax Planning¶

    • Track all income: You'll receive 1099 if over $600/year
    • Set aside 25-30% for taxes (self-employment + income tax)
    • Document expenses: Home office, internet, phone can be deductible
    • Consider quarterly payments if earning $1,000+/quarter

    8. Realistic Earnings Expectations¶

    • Typical: $3,000-$6,000/year with a few networks at $250/hour
    • Active: $10,000-$20,000/year with multiple networks and good reputation
    • Elite: $50,000+/year if in-demand niche with premium rates
    • Time investment: A few hours per month to 5-10 hours/month
    • Remember: "Feast or famine" - some months zero, some months $5K+

    9. Platform-Specific Strategies¶

    • GLG: Set high initial rate (hard to change), respond to surveys for steady income
    • AlphaSights: Build to $500+/hour after initial calls, emphasize speed
    • Dialectica: Start $150, confidence-build to $300-$500, patient 10-day payment
    • Guidepoint: Be selective with questionnaires, negotiate after success
    • Avoid: Maven (payment issues), Tegus (cancellation policies)

    10. Long-term Positioning¶

    • Develop niche: Specialized expertise commands premium (cybersecurity, AI/ML, biotech)
    • Stay current: Recent experience (last 2-3 years) most valuable
    • Build speaking profile: TedX, conferences, publications boost credibility
    • Consider IANS Faculty: Cybersecurity experts may prefer exclusive faculty model

    Realistic Goal: With 3-4 networks, good profile, $300-$500/hour rates, and responsive behavior, earning $5,000-$15,000/year working 10-20 hours total is achievable.

    RED FLAGS TO AVOID¶

    For Choosing Platforms¶

    CRITICAL - Avoid These Platforms:

    1. Tegus (2.3/5 rating)
    2. No payment if client cancels (even after 15 min wait)
    3. Partial payments: "Only half agreed amount"
    4. Public transcripts erode competitive advantage

    5. High project cancellation with no compensation

    6. Maven (Payment issues)

    7. Payment delays lasting months
    8. Clawbacks: Taking back already-paid money
    9. Survey scams: Complete survey, deemed "unqualified," no pay

    10. "Zero consults in whole year with 50+ survey invites"

    11. Lynk Answers/Global (NOT in main 10, but research found)

    12. 60-75 working days payment timeline
    13. Experts unpaid for 2-3 years reported
    14. "Scam company" reviews
    15. Consider legal action discussions

    Caution - Use with Awareness: 4. Atheneum

    • 4+ week payment delays (chronic)
    • "Above average payment complaints"
    • Some consultants never paid

    • Good platform otherwise, if you can tolerate delays

    • Guidepoint

    • Only pay for call time (not waiting time)
    • Long questionnaires, many don't convert
    • Payment delay reports
    • Low Trustpilot (3.2/5) vs. competitors

    Behavioral Red Flags¶

    Expert Behaviors That Get You Flagged/Banned:

    1. Misrepresenting experience - AI compliance monitoring scans calls
    2. Sharing non-public/insider information - immediate removal
    3. Inconsistencies between LinkedIn, profile, screener answers
    4. Underperforming - networks stop sending projects
    5. Stretching background beyond actual expertise
    6. Long-winded responses in screeners - keep to 1-2 sentences
    7. Slow responses - projects filled in 24-48 hours

    Platform Behaviors to Watch:

    1. Aggressive recruiting - if outreach feels scam-like (AlphaSights complaint)
    2. Rate pressure - associates trying to lower your rate (Third Bridge)
    3. Project spam - frequent off-topic requests (Dialectica complaint)
    4. Unclear payment terms - if not crystal clear, get in writing
    5. No screening call compensation - standard but be aware
    6. High client cancellation rates - Tegus issue

    Compliance Red Flags - DO NOT CROSS THESE LINES¶

    1. Never discuss current employer proprietary information
    2. Never consult for competitors of current employer
    3. Never share non-public information (material non-public info = insider trading)
    4. Respect confidentiality of clients and projects
    5. Follow network compliance training - taken seriously post-scandals
    6. Document employer approval if required by your contract

    IRS Red Flags (Tax Compliance):

    1. Unreported 1099 income - networks report to IRS
    2. Aggressive deductions without documentation
    3. Mixing personal/business expenses without clear records
    4. Not making quarterly payments if earning $1,000+/quarter

    Client/Project Red Flags¶

    1. Vague project descriptions - may indicate fishing expedition
    2. Pressure to share specific company information
    3. Questions about confidential processes or data
    4. Requests for documents or materials (almost always prohibited)
    5. Unclear scope - nail down time commitment before accepting

    Warning Signs - When to Walk Away¶

    • Platform asks you to work for free (Lynk tried "donation" suggestion)
    • Payment terms change after work completed (Tegus partial payment)
    • No written agreement on compensation
    • Pressure to lower rate excessively (Third Bridge associate behavior)
    • Client questions cross compliance lines - end call immediately
    • Platform has criminal/regulatory issues - research background

    Protect Yourself¶

    1. Screenshot all rate agreements before calls
    2. Document completion of calls/surveys
    3. Track all payments and follow up on delays beyond stated timeline
    4. Save all communications for at least 3 years (tax records)
    5. Review platform terms quarterly (they change)
    6. Join expert forums for real-time issue sharing
    7. Report violations to network compliance teams
    8. Consider liability insurance if doing high-volume consulting

    Bottom Line: Stick to established platforms (GLG, AlphaSights, Dialectica, Guidepoint), avoid those with payment issues (Maven, Tegus), respond quickly, price appropriately, stay compliant, and don't rely on any single platform.
