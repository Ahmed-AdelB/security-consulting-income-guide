    Executive Summary¶

    In 2025, Business Continuity Planning (BCP) and Disaster Recovery (DR) have evolved from compliance checkboxes to strategic imperatives. The convergence of escalating cyber threats (ransomware), supply chain volatility, and strict regulatory enforcement has driven a robust market for resilience expertise. Organizations are moving beyond static PDF plans to dynamic, tested, and automated recovery capabilities.

    Key Insight: The "Resilience Architect" is emerging as a premium role, blending traditional BCP with cyber recovery and cloud resilience, commanding higher rates than traditional "planner" roles.

    Market Landscape & Demand Drivers¶

    The global market for BCDR solutions and services is projected to grow significantly (CAGR ~8-12%), driven by:

    1. Ransomware as a Disaster: Cyberattacks are now the primary driver for DR invocation. Traditional "fire and flood" DR is insufficient; "cyber recovery" vaults and immutable backups are in high demand.
    2. Regulatory Pressure:
      • Finance: DORA (EU), SEC rules (US).
      • Healthcare: HIPAA security rule updates.
      • Critical Infrastructure: CIRCIA (US).
    3. Digital Dependency: Zero tolerance for downtime in cloud-native and always-on digital services.
    4. Supply Chain Fragility: Global disruptions have forced companies to map and mitigate 3rd and 4th party risks.

    Pricing Models & Project Scoping¶

    Fixed-Price Project Packages¶

    Moving away from hourly billing is recommended for defined deliverables.

    1. The "Compliance Starter" (Small Biz)¶

    • Target: Small organizations needing basic policy and emergency response plans.
    • Scope:
      • Risk Assessment (High level)
      • Business Impact Analysis (BIA) (Top 5 processes)
      • Basic BCP Document
      • Emergency Call Tree
    • Price Range: $5,000 - $15,000
    • Duration: 2-4 Weeks

    2. The "Mid-Market Resilience" (Medium Org)¶

    • Target: 500-2000 employees, regulated industries.
    • Scope:
      • Comprehensive ISO 22301 aligned BIA
      • Departmental Plans (Finance, HR, Ops, IT)
      • IT DR Plan Review
      • Tabletop Exercise (1 session)
    • Price Range: $30,000 - $80,000
    • Duration: 2-3 Months

    3. The "Enterprise Transformation" (Large Org)¶

    • Target: Global footprint, complex supply chain.
    • Scope:
      • Multi-site BIA & Risk Assessment
      • Supply Chain Risk Management (SCRM) integration
      • Automated DR Failover Orchestration design
      • Crisis Management Team (CMT) training
      • Full-scale simulation
    • Price Range: $100,000 - $300,000+
    • Duration: 6-12 Months

    Service Menu & Engagement Types¶

    Detailed list of billable services consultants can offer.

    A. Advisory & Planning¶

    • Business Impact Analysis (BIA): Identifying critical processes, RTO (Recovery Time Objective), and RPO (Recovery Point Objective).
    • Risk Assessment (RA): Evaluating threats (natural, cyber, physical, supply chain).
    • Plan Development: Writing BCP, DRP, and CMP (Crisis Management Plan) documents.
    • Gap Analysis: Auditing current state against ISO 22301 or NFPA 1600.

    B. Technical Disaster Recovery (IT Focus)¶

    • DR Architecture Design: Cloud failover strategies (AWS/Azure DR), immutable backups.
    • Cyber Recovery Vaults: Designing isolated recovery environments for ransomware resilience.
    • Failover Testing: Technical orchestration of switching production to secondary sites.

    C. Validation & Training¶

    • Tabletop Exercises (TTX): Facilitated scenario-based walkthroughs for leadership.
    • Full-Scale Drills: Real-world simulation of outages.
    • Awareness Training: Staff training on safety and continuity roles.

    D. Managed Services (Retainer)¶

    • "Resilience as a Service" (RaaS): Monthly retainer to maintain plans, update BIAs, and run quarterly drills.
    • Price: $2k - $10k / month depending on org size.

    Methodologies & Frameworks¶

    Successful consultants align with recognized standards:

    • ISO 22301: The global standard for Business Continuity Management Systems (BCMS).
    • NIST SP 800-34: Contingency Planning Guide for Federal Information Systems.
    • NFPA 1600: Standard on Continuity, Emergency, and Crisis Management.
    • DRII (Disaster Recovery Institute International): Professional practices.
    • BCI (Business Continuity Institute): Good Practice Guidelines (GPG).

    Industry-Specific Considerations¶

    Financial Services¶

    • Driver: DORA, SEC, OCC.
    • Focus: "Operational Resilience" – not just recovering, but tolerating disruption within impact tolerances.
    • Rate Premium: High.

    Healthcare¶

    • Driver: HIPAA, Patient Safety.
    • Focus: Clinical continuity during ransomware downtime (paper charting procedures).
    • Rate Premium: Medium-High.

    Manufacturing / Logistics¶

    • Driver: Supply Chain interruptions.
    • Focus: Physical site loss, logistics rerouting, alternative suppliers.
    • Rate Premium: Medium.

Scope of Work - Phase 1: Discovery & BIA - Interview 10 department heads. - Define RTO/RPO for top 20 critical applications. - Phase 2: Plan Development - Update Master BCP. - Develop IT DR Playbook for Ransomware. - Phase 3: Validation - Design and facilitate 4-hour Executive Tabletop Exercise. - Produce After-Action Report (AAR).

    3. Deliverables - Business Impact Analysis Report. - Updated Business Continuity Plan (PDF & Word). - Ransomware DR Playbook. - Tabletop Exercise AAR & Improvement Plan.

    4. Timeline & Fees - Duration: 8 Weeks. - Fee Structure: Fixed Fee $45,000 (billed 50% start, 50% completion). - Assumptions: Client provides timely access to stakeholders.
