    OT/ICS Industrial Security Consulting — 10K Research Dossier¶

    Purpose: Provide a comprehensive, vendor-aware research briefing for OT/ICS industrial security consulting, with a focus on Claroty, Nozomi Networks, and Dragos. Includes market landscape, service models, typical deliverables, rate benchmarks, and practical guidance for consulting engagements.

    Scope: Critical infrastructure and industrial environments (manufacturing, energy, oil & gas, utilities, water/wastewater, transportation, chemicals, food & beverage, pharmaceuticals, mining, and discrete manufacturing). Covers OT/ICS, IT/OT convergence, and industrial IoT security.

    Method: Synthesized from industry best practices, common vendor professional services patterns, and typical enterprise procurement models. Rates are indicative ranges and should be validated via vendor quotes or partner proposals.

    1) Executive Summary¶

    Industrial cybersecurity consulting is transitioning from ad‑hoc assessments to long‑term, outcome‑based programs that combine asset visibility, segmentation, detection, and incident readiness. Enterprises increasingly adopt a “controls‑first” program model that begins with asset inventory and risk quantification, then evolves into network segmentation, OT SOC integration, and mature incident response.

    Three dominant specialist vendors—Claroty, Nozomi Networks, and Dragos—anchor many OT/ICS programs. They offer adjacent software platforms and professional services, often delivered in collaboration with GSIs, regional MSSPs, or boutique OT consultancies. Pricing structures typically blend fixed‑fee assessments with time‑and‑materials implementation.

    Key takeaways:

    • Demand drivers: IT/OT convergence, ransomware targeting industrial operations, regulatory oversight, and supply chain pressure.
    • Most common entry points: Asset discovery and network visibility, followed by risk assessments and segmentation planning.
    • Value hinge: Deliverables that translate technical risk into operational impact, enabling leadership to prioritize remediation.
    • Rate realities: OT/ICS consulting commands premium rates due to scarce talent, operational risk, and specialized tooling. Expect broad bands by region and specialization.

    2) OT/ICS Threat Landscape¶

    Industrial environments are uniquely exposed to targeted disruption and safety risks. Unlike enterprise IT, OT emphasizes availability, integrity, and safety; security controls must protect operations without disrupting process control.

    Common Threat Vectors¶

    • Ransomware with operational impact: IT compromise cascading into OT shutdowns.
    • Credential misuse: Shared engineering accounts, weak remote access controls.
    • Legacy protocol exploitation: Modbus, DNP3, PROFINET, Ethernet/IP with limited security controls.
    • Supply chain and contractor access: Vendor laptops, third‑party remote support portals.
    • Misconfiguration and unpatched systems: Hard‑to‑patch PLCs, HMIs, and historians.

    Operational Consequences¶

    • Safety events and environmental harm
    • Downtime and production loss
    • Regulatory enforcement and fines
    • Brand and supply chain disruption

    3) OT/ICS Consulting Market Overview¶

    Buyer Profiles¶

    • Plant managers: Focused on uptime and safety.
    • OT engineers: Control system stability, change management.
    • CIO/CISO: Enterprise risk, governance, compliance.
    • Regulators: NERC CIP, TSA directives, IEC 62443.

    Market Dynamics¶

    • Scarce expertise: OT security consultants are limited in number; rates are premium.
    • Hybrid delivery: Vendor professional services plus channel partner implementation.
    • Long lifecycle: Multi‑year modernization programs with phased remediation.

    4) Vendor Profiles (Claroty, Nozomi, Dragos)¶

    Claroty¶

    Positioning: Broad industrial asset visibility, risk assessment, and secure remote access across OT, IoT, and XIoT environments. Strong in healthcare and manufacturing contexts.

    Typical Services:

    • OT asset discovery and profiling
    • Risk assessment and exposure analysis
    • Secure remote access architecture and onboarding
    • Segmentation strategy and policy design
    • OT SOC integration and workflow design

    Strengths:

    • Large device coverage and integrations
    • XIoT posture management
    • Emphasis on safe remote access

    Potential Gaps:

    • Requires mature integration with enterprise SOC for full value
    • Depends on partner ecosystem for large transformation programs

    Nozomi Networks¶

    Positioning: Deep industrial monitoring, anomaly detection, and asset visibility with a focus on OT and critical infrastructure. Strong in energy and utilities.

    Typical Services:

    • Passive monitoring deployment and tuning
    • Asset inventory and vulnerability management
    • Threat detection, incident response alignment
    • ISA/IEC 62443 assessment support

    Strengths:

    • Deep protocol coverage and anomaly detection
    • OT‑centric telemetry and alerting

    Potential Gaps:

    • Complexity of tuning for large environments
    • Program outcomes depend on SOC integration maturity

    Dragos¶

    Positioning: Threat intelligence‑driven OT security with a strong emphasis on industrial threat activity, detection, and incident response. Highly regarded for adversary expertise.

    Typical Services:

    • Threat‑informed OT monitoring and detection
    • Industrial incident response readiness
    • Threat intelligence integration
    • Advanced OT SOC design

    Strengths:

    • Strong OT threat intel and adversary mapping
    • Incident response and rapid response capabilities

    Potential Gaps:

    • Focused on higher‑end enterprise/critical infrastructure
    • May be heavier investment for smaller facilities

    5) Typical Consulting Engagement Types¶

    5.1 Discovery & Baseline Assessment¶

    • Network mapping and asset discovery
    • Topology diagrams and data flow analysis
    • Criticality classification of assets
    • Initial risk register

    5.2 Risk & Compliance Assessment¶

    • IEC 62443 gap analysis
    • NIST CSF/800‑82 mapping
    • NERC CIP readiness (for applicable sectors)
    • Threat modeling and exposure analysis

    5.3 Architecture & Segmentation Planning¶

    • Purdue model alignment
    • Zone/conduit design
    • Firewall rules and policy definition
    • Access control strategy for vendors and contractors

    5.4 Monitoring & OT SOC Integration¶

    • OT‑specific alert tuning and triage workflows
    • SIEM/SOAR integration
    • OT SOC runbook development

    5.5 Incident Response & Tabletop Exercises¶

    • OT‑specific IR plan development
    • Playbooks for ransomware in OT environments
    • Joint exercises with operations leadership

    6) Engagement Deliverables (Detailed)¶

    Core Deliverables¶

    • Asset inventory: Device list, model/firmware, protocol, criticality
    • Network diagrams: OT zones, conduits, trust boundaries
    • Risk register: Prioritized list with operational impact
    • Segmentation blueprint: Target architecture and phased rollout
    • Detection strategy: OT alerts mapped to playbooks

    Optional Deliverables¶

    • Compliance evidence package (62443, NIST 800‑82)
    • Vendor remote access strategy and SOPs
    • Change management and patching roadmap
    • OT security policy set (access, logging, backups)

    7) Consulting Rate Benchmarks (Indicative)¶

    Important: The following ranges are indicative estimates based on common OT/ICS consulting market norms. Actual pricing is set by vendor agreements, scope, and geography. Use as budgeting guidance, not an official quote.

    7.1 Indicative Hourly Rate Bands (USD)¶

    Provider Junior/Analyst Senior Consultant Principal/Lead Notes
    Claroty (PS or partner‑delivered) $125–$175/hr $200–$300/hr $300–$425/hr Often bundled with deployment packages

    Dragos (PS/IR focus) $150–$210/hr $250–$350/hr $350–$500/hr Premium for threat‑informed services

    7.2 Fixed‑Fee Assessment Benchmarks¶

    • Single‑site visibility assessment: $25k–$75k
    • Multi‑site baseline program (3–5 sites): $125k–$350k
    • Segmentation design + rollout planning: $75k–$250k
    • IR readiness + tabletop: $30k–$120k

    7.3 Regional Adjustments¶

    • North America: Baseline for ranges above
    • Western Europe: -10% to -20% vs. NA (varies by country)
    • APAC: -15% to -30% depending on market maturity
    • Middle East: Often +10% to +30% for specialized expertise

    7.4 Key Pricing Drivers¶

    • Site complexity and operational criticality
    • Remote vs. onsite access constraints
    • Regulatory compliance obligations
    • Integration scope (SIEM/SOAR, IAM, CMDB)
    • Vendor‑partner delivery model

    8) Methodology for OT/ICS Consulting Projects¶

    Phase 1: Scoping & Access¶

    • Stakeholder interviews (engineering, IT, safety)
    • Data collection: network diagrams, asset inventories
    • Define operational constraints and maintenance windows

    Phase 2: Visibility & Baseline¶

    • Passive monitoring deployment (TAPs/SPAN)
    • Asset inventory with criticality scoring
    • Protocol analysis and communication mapping

    Phase 3: Risk & Gap Analysis¶

    • Threat modeling for key assets (PLCs, SIS, HMIs)
    • Compliance mapping (IEC 62443, NIST 800‑82)
    • Prioritization based on safety and uptime impact

    Phase 4: Architecture & Controls¶

    • Zone/conduit segmentation design
    • Remote access controls and MFA
    • Backup, recovery, and patching strategy

    Phase 5: Monitoring & Response¶

    • OT alert triage mapping to playbooks
    • SOC integration and escalation paths
    • Tabletop exercises and readiness validation

    9) Standards & Frameworks¶

    IEC 62443¶

    • Core for industrial cybersecurity maturity
    • Defines security levels, zones/conduits
    • Guides product/system requirements

    NIST SP 800‑82¶

    • ICS‑specific security guidance
    • Emphasizes risk management and segmentation

    NIST CSF¶

    • Use for enterprise reporting and risk alignment
    • Map OT controls to Identify/Protect/Detect/Respond/Recover

    Sector‑Specific¶

    • NERC CIP: Bulk electric systems
    • TSA Security Directives: Pipeline and rail
    • ISO 27001 with OT extensions

    12) Vendor Selection Guidance¶

    Claroty vs. Nozomi vs. Dragos¶

    • Claroty: Best for broad visibility and secure remote access across mixed OT/XIoT estates.
    • Nozomi: Strong for deep protocol monitoring and anomaly detection in critical infrastructure.
    • Dragos: Highest impact where threat intel and IR depth are the priority.

    Decision Criteria¶

    • Sector fit and references
    • Integration capability (SIEM/SOAR/CMDB)
    • Professional services availability
    • Partner ecosystem strength in region
    • Total cost of ownership (software + services)

    13) Common Challenges & Risk Mitigation¶

    Challenges¶

    • Limited maintenance windows
    • Legacy systems with no patch path
    • Cultural friction between OT and IT teams
    • Vendor and contractor access control

    Mitigation Strategies¶

    • Build a phased change plan aligned to production cycles
    • Emphasize compensating controls
    • Establish OT governance and joint change control
    • Enforce privileged access management for vendors

    14) Sample Engagement Plan (12‑Week)¶

    Weeks 1–2: Stakeholder alignment, data gathering, remote access planning Weeks 3–5: Visibility deployment, asset inventory, baseline risk Weeks 6–8: Risk assessment, zone/conduit design Weeks 9–10: SOC integration plan, OT IR readiness Weeks 11–12: Final report, roadmap, executive briefing

    • Mean time to detect (OT incidents)
    • Mean time to respond and recover

    • Level 1: Ad‑hoc controls, low visibility
    • Level 2: Asset inventory and baseline monitoring
    • Level 3: Segmentation and formalized change control
    • Level 4: Integrated OT SOC and IR readiness
    • Level 5: Threat hunting and continuous improvement

    22) Sample Scope of Work (Condensed)¶

    Objective: Establish OT asset inventory, prioritize risks, and provide a phased remediation roadmap for three plants.

    In Scope:

    • Passive monitoring at each site
    • Asset classification and risk register
    • Segmentation target design
    • OT incident response readiness review

    Out of Scope:

    • Physical security or safety system modifications
    • Firmware upgrades (unless explicitly approved)

    Acceptance Criteria:

    • Asset inventory validated by plant teams
    • Segmentation design approved by OT and IT leads
    • Executive report delivered and reviewed

    23) Program Governance Best Practices¶

    29) Frequently Asked Questions¶

    Q: Why are OT consulting rates higher than IT?
    A: Scarcity of OT‑trained talent, on‑site requirements, and operational risk exposure.

    Q: How long does a typical assessment take?
    A: Single‑site assessments often take 4–8 weeks; multi‑site can take 3–6 months.

    Q: Can we deploy monitoring without downtime?
    A: Yes—passive monitoring is usually non‑intrusive but must be validated per site.

    30) Recommendations for Next Steps¶

    3. Engage a vendor or partner for a baseline assessment.
    4. Build a phased roadmap with segmentation as a core control.
    5. Operationalize monitoring with OT‑specific IR playbooks.
